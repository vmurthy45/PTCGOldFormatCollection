# How to Pilot — Virizion/Genesect (2014)

_Format: NXD-on 2014 (XY era). "VirGen." This build mirrors Andrew Estrada's 2014
World Championship-**winning** list (VirGen went 1st/2nd/3rd in Masters at Worlds)._

## One-line identity
A consistent acceleration deck: **Virizion-EX's Emerald Slash** loads Grass Energy onto
a benched **Genesect-EX**, which then 1HKOs anything with the **G Booster** ACE SPEC and
drags up whatever it wants with **Red Signal**. Immune to Special Conditions, hits huge,
and plays the same reliable game every match.

## The meta it lived in
2014's format rewarded consistency and the ability to punch through gimmicks (Pyroar,
Suicune, high-HP walls). VirGen did exactly that — G Booster ignores protective effects
and 1HKOs anything — and it was so reliable it swept the Masters podium at 2014 Worlds.
Its kryptonite: Fire attackers (both key Pokémon are Fire-Weak) and the ever-present
Garbodor.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Virizion-EX (9/101) ×4** — the accelerator and ideal starter. **Emerald Slash**:
  60 damage *and* attach 2 Grass Energy from the deck to a benched Pokémon (usually
  Genesect). **Verdant Wind** (Ability): your Grass-Energy Pokémon are immune to Special
  Conditions — a huge shield vs. LaserBank. Run 4 so one's in play turn 1.
- **Genesect-EX (11/101) ×4** — the attacker. **Megalo Cannon**: 100 + 20 bench snipe.
  **Red Signal** (Ability): when you attach a Plasma Energy to it, switch the opponent's
  Active with a benched Pokémon — a free gust to line up KOs (saves Catcher slots).
- **G Booster (92/101, ACE SPEC)** — the finisher. A Tool giving Genesect an attack that
  does **200 and ignores all effects on the Defending Pokémon** (discard 2 Energy). This
  1HKOs anything and beats Pyroar/Suicune/Safeguard. The single biggest reason the deck
  is strong.
- **Jirachi-EX (60/101) ×1** — **Stellar Guidance**: search any Supporter; consistency
  glue (and a fetch for the exact draw card you need).
- **Mr. Mime (47/116) ×1** — **Bench Barrier**: blocks bench damage (protects vs.
  Landorus/opposing Genesect snipe).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×4 / Skyla ×4 / Colress ×1** — draw + fetch; Skyla grabs
  Professor's Letter / G Booster / the right Item.
- **Ultra Ball ×4** — the only good Virizion search; also thins tech cards.
- **Energy Switch ×3** — move Grass off a spent Virizion onto Genesect; keeps G Booster
  streaming without wasting Emerald Slashes.
- **Skyarrow Bridge ×3** — free retreat for Virizion/Genesect (both retreat 1), so you
  pivot between accelerator and attacker for free.
- **Muscle Band ×3** — +20; nudges Megalo Cannon over KO lines.
- **Tool Scrapper ×2** — remove Garbodor's Tool to reinstate Verdant Wind / free
  retreat, and strip opposing Tools.
- **Enhanced Hammer ×2** — strip opposing special Energy (great vs. Plasma).
- **Shadow Triad ×2 / Professor's Letter ×1 / Colress Machine ×1** — recur
  Pokémon/Supporters, fetch Grass, extra acceleration; **Super Rod ×1 / Town Map ×1**
  for recovery and prize fixing.
- **9 Grass / 4 Plasma** — Grass for Emerald Slash + attack costs (needs a lot, since
  Slash pulls it from deck); Plasma enables Red Signal.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** get **Virizion-EX** active with a Grass attached (or, if you can't,
  attach Grass to Genesect so you still threaten a turn-2 Megalo Cannon). Skyarrow down
  for free pivoting.
- **Turn 2:** **Emerald Slash** — 60 damage and 2 Grass onto a benched Genesect. Now
  Genesect is charging toward Megalo Cannon / G Booster.

## The core loop
Emerald Slash to load Genesect while chipping the Active → promote Genesect →
**Megalo Cannon** (100 + snipe) or, when you need a clean kill, **G Booster** for 200
ignoring effects. Use **Red Signal** to drag the target you want up, **Energy Switch**
to keep G Booster fueled, and **Verdant Wind** to shrug off Lasers the whole time.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Get Virizion active turn 1** — it's the whole engine; without the turn-2 Emerald
  Slash you fall behind.
- **Save G Booster for the target that needs it** (a wall or a big EX); Megalo Cannon
  handles the rest.
- **Tool Scrapper vs. Garbodor** — reinstating Verdant Wind + free retreat is often
  game-deciding.
- **Respect the late-game N** — the deck can stall on a small hand; sequence draw so a
  topdeck N doesn't brick you.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire attackers (Charizard-EX, Flareon)** — both key Pokémon are Fire-Weak and get
  1HKO'd.
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — shuts off Verdant Wind and Red Signal; you need Scrapper.
- **Slow starts** — no turn-1 attack means a whiffed Virizion setup can lose tempo you
  don't get back; the deck can be "cloggy."

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Emerald Slash: **60 + attach 2 Grass from deck to a benched Pokémon**.
- Megalo Cannon: **100 + 20 bench snipe**.
- G Booster: **200, ignores all effects on the Defending Pokémon** (discard 2 Energy).
- Verdant Wind: **immunity to Special Conditions** for Grass-Energy Pokémon.
