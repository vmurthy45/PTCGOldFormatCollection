# How to Pilot — LuxChomp (2010)

_Format: HGSS-on / DP-on 2010 (SP-dominated). This build is essentially Yuta
Komatsuda's 2010 World Championship-**winning** list — the DialgaChomp variant,
running Dialga G + Dragonite FB + Professor Oak's New Theory._

## One-line identity
The defining deck of the format: a Pokémon SP toolbox that takes **six prizes in six
turns** by using free switching (Bright Look) and free-target sniping (Dragon Rush)
to pick off the opponent's weakest, most valuable targets — you don't out-damage, you
out-manoeuvre.

## The meta it lived in
SP decks had ruled for over a year; the HGSS reprint of **Double Colorless Energy**
pushed Garchomp C LV.X over the top and made LuxChomp the deck to beat. It won more
sanctioned events in 2009–2010 than anything else and took Masters + Seniors at 2010
Worlds. If you were building any deck that year, it needed a LuxChomp answer — it was the
undisputed **best deck in the format (BDIF)**.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Luxray GL LV.X (109/111)** — Poké-Power **Bright Look**: drag any benched Pokémon
  into the Active spot (like a free Catcher). This is how you pull up a helpless
  support Basic and KO it. Flash Impact also makes it a real attacker in a pinch.
- **Garchomp C LV.X (145/147)** — **Dragon Rush**: 80 to *any* Pokémon, bench or
  active, no weakness/resistance dodging you. Snipes their setup Pokémon (Uxie,
  Azelf) or finishes a target. Powered by DCE + Energy Gain.
- **Uxie (43/146)** — **Set Up**: draw until you have 7. The deck burns its hand fast,
  so Uxie is your refill. Searchable off Pokémon Collector.
- **Cyrus's Conspiracy (105/127) ×4** — the glue: search a Supporter + a Team
  Galactic Invention + a basic Energy in one card. The most important card in the deck.
- **Dialga G / Dialga G LV.X** — the "Dialga" in DialgaChomp: its LV.X Poké-Body
  (Deafen-style) shuts off opposing Trainers/Poké-Bodies, a tech vs. other SP and
  lock decks.
- **Dragonite FB (56/147)** — tech vs. the SP mirror: Mach Blow does 80 to a Defending
  Pokémon SP with no chance to respond — enough to KO most SP attackers.

## The Team Galactic's Invention (TGI) toolbox — the deck's "tricks"
- **G-105 Poké Turn ×4** — pick up your own SP Pokémon (+ everything on it) back to
  hand. Heals damage, **re-uses Bright Look** (Luxray only triggers Bright Look when
  it comes into play), and dodges KOs.
- **G-101 Energy Gain ×4** — SP Pokémon attacks cost 1 less. Turns DCE into a full
  Dragon Rush.
- **G-103 Power Spray ×3** — free counter: negate an opponent's Poké-Power as they use
  it. Save these for high-value Powers (opposing setup, Regigigas, etc.).
- **G-109 SP Radar ×3** — search any SP Pokémon.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** get a Basic + draw engine going. Pokémon Collector for Uxie(s) + your SP
  Basics; Uxie's Set Up to load the hand. Cyrus's Conspiracy to assemble the next play.
- **Going second, you can attack immediately:** with Energy Gain down, Garchomp C can
  chip ~50 with DCE, and donks are possible (Garchomp C / Luxray GL / a DCE'd attacker
  can OHKO a lone Basic on the first turn).
- **"Set up" is achieved** when you have: a Garchomp C LV.X with Energy Gain + DCE ready
  for Dragon Rush, a Luxray GL on the bench ready to level up, and Poké Turns in hand to
  recycle.

## The core loop
Bright Look their weak/benched target into the Active spot → KO it (or Dragon Rush
snipe it directly) → Poké Turn to reset Luxray / heal / dodge → repeat. Play
**reactively**: answer whatever they just threatened, then snowball the prize lead.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Deal with the opponent's Garchomp C LV.X first** in SP mirrors — it's the biggest
  reciprocal threat.
- **Don't drop high-retreat Pokémon** without a Poké Turn available (you can get
  stranded Active / lured up).
- Spend **Power Spray** only on Powers that matter; don't waste it on the first Uxie.
- Prizes-in-6 mindset: every turn should take or set up a prize, not just trade.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Donphan (Prime/other)** — Fighting resistance + damage-reduction Body + cheap big
  hits. Luxray is nearly useless into it; you grind with Crobat G poison + Dragon Rush.
- **Disruption/lock decks (Sablock, Vileplume variants)** — Judge + resource denial can
  cut you off before you assemble attacks; lean on consistency and Dialga to break locks.
- Anything that OHKOs your LV.X attackers before you can Poké Turn them.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Dragon Rush: **80 to any Pokémon** (bench or active).
- Bright Look: free switch of an opponent's Pokémon (once per entry into play → recycle
  with Poké Turn).
- Win plan: **6 prizes ASAP**, off weak targets, not a damage race.
- Energy: 4 DCE do most of the lifting; Lightning/Metal support Luxray/Dialga.
