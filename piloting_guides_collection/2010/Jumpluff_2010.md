# How to Pilot — Jumpluff (2010)

_Format: HGSS-on 2010. Based on the Junior-division World Championship-**winning**
list, Yuka Furusawa's "Power Cottonweed."_

## One-line identity
A fast Grass swarm: **Jumpluff** attacks for a single Grass Energy and scales its
damage off your board, so you flood the bench, evolve quickly, and hit for big
numbers as early as turn 1–2 — then just keep replacing Jumpluffs as they trade.

## The meta it lived in
A field of low-HP Basics and Pokémon SP was perfect prey for a cheap, high-damage
swarm. Jumpluff dominated the younger divisions (it won Juniors at 2010 Worlds) and
stayed relevant up top thanks to its low attack cost leaving room for techs. It later
fused with Luxray into "LuxPluff."

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Jumpluff (6/123) ×4** — the whole plan. **Mass Attack**: for one Grass Energy, does
  10 damage per Basic + Evolution Pokémon in play (scaling up to ~120, more with help).
  **Leaf Guard** reduces damage taken by 30 next turn — when you can't KO, use this to
  make Jumpluff obnoxious to remove.
- **Hoppip / Skiploom** — the evolution line; run heavy to keep swarming.
- **Broken Time-Space (104/127) ×3** — evolve the turn you play/bench, so Hoppip →
  Skiploom → Jumpluff can happen fast (turn-1 attacks are possible).
- **Rare Candy ×3** — skip Skiploom to hit Jumpluff a stage faster.
- **Claydol (15/106) ×3** — **Cosmic Power**: draw/refresh engine; critical for getting
  *multiple* Jumpluff out and recovering them after KOs.
- **Unown R (77/146) ×4** — extra "R" bodies count toward Mass Attack and add a little
  draw — a Power Cottonweed signature that boosts your damage ceiling.
- **Uxie (43/146) ×2** — Set Up draw to power the fast start.

## Support / tech
- **Pokémon Collector ×4 / Pokémon Communication ×4 / Bebe's Search / Luxury Ball** —
  huge search package to find bodies and evolutions consistently.
- **Judge ×2** — hand disruption; cut the opponent's resources once you're ahead so they
  can't stabilize.
- **PlusPower ×3** — nudge damage over the line for key KOs.
- **Warp Point ×2** — switch/gust effect to bring up a target or escape; also cleans up
  the last prizes (a 3rd copy was considered a real upgrade).
- **Expert Belt ×1** — +20/+20 on a key Jumpluff.
- **Night Maintenance ×2** — recycle KO'd Pokémon back into the deck to keep swarming.
- **Crobat G ×1** — free damage counters to reach numbers.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** Collector for Hoppip(s) + support; bench wide. With Broken Time-Space
  and/or Rare Candy you can even evolve into a Jumpluff and start attacking turn 1.
- **Turn 2:** Claydol online for draw, multiple Jumpluff set up, Mass Attack for a
  meaningful hit that scales with your (now full) bench.

## The core loop
Keep the bench full (more bodies = more Mass Attack damage), attack every turn for one
Grass, and use **Leaf Guard** on turns you can't KO to survive the return hit. When a
Jumpluff trades, Claydol/Night Maintenance replace it immediately. Add reach with the
**Luxray GL LV.X** line if your build runs it (Bright Look to pull + KO a benched
target).

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Bench width = damage.** Don't under-commit bodies; a fuller board is a bigger hit.
- **Leaf Guard when you can't KO** — a −30 Jumpluff is very hard to remove profitably.
- Use **Judge** when *you're* set up and *they* aren't — it's a tempo weapon, not a
  panic button.
- Watch for whiffing the single Grass attachment — it stalls a whole turn of attacks.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Gardevoir / PLOX (Psychic Lock)** — shuts off your Poké-Powers (Claydol/Uxie),
  choking your draw and your ability to keep swarming. A real problem matchup.
- **Fire / Rain of Fire spreaders** — Grass is Fire-weak; spread damage can wipe
  Hoppip/Skiploom/Jumpluff lines.
- **Anything that answers a single Power** (Mesprit/Power Spray) can slow your setup.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Mass Attack: **10 × (Basics + Evolutions in play)** for **1 Grass Energy** (~120 base,
  higher with Unown R / PlusPower / Belt).
- Leaf Guard: **−30 damage taken** next turn.
- Fastest attack: **turn 1** possible via Broken Time-Space / Rare Candy.
- Recovery: Claydol + Night Maintenance keep the swarm going.
