# How to Pilot — Boltevoir (2010)

_Format: HGSS-on 2010. This is Michael Pramawat's 2010 World Championship
**finalist** deck (2nd, Masters) — he lost a very close final to Komatsuda's LuxChomp._

## One-line identity
A Psychic control/toolbox built on **Gardevoir**: lock the opponent out of Poké-Powers
with **Psychic Lock** while a rotating cast of attackers (Gallade, Machamp, Dusknoir)
handles every specific threat, and **Gardevoir LV.X** cleans up KOs by teleporting in
to finish damaged Pokémon.

## The meta it lived in
2010 was an SP/Power-heavy format (LuxChomp, DialgaChomp, Gyarados, Jumpluff all lean
on Poké-Powers or setup Powers). A deck that shuts Powers off *and* has a silver-bullet
attacker for each archetype was a strong, skill-rewarding answer — good enough to reach
the Worlds final.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Gardevoir (7/132) ×3** — the core. **Psychic Lock**: 60 damage *and* the opponent
  can't use Poké-Powers next turn — chokes SP setup, Claydol/Uxie draw, Bright Look,
  everything. **Telepass** (Poké-Power) lets you use a Supporter from the opponent's
  discard, giving huge consistency.
- **Gardevoir LV.X (131/132)** — **Teleportation** Poké-Power swaps your Active for
  free; **Bring Down** KOs the most-damaged Pokémon in play. Combo: soften a target,
  then teleport Gardevoir LV.X in and Bring Down for the KO. (Careful — Bring Down can
  hit *your own* most-damaged Pokémon.)
- **Gallade (6/132)** — the 1HKO button. **Psychic Cut** flips prizes and does 60 +20
  per prize flipped (up to ~180) — enough to one-shot almost anything, and you see your
  prizes. **Sonic Blade** drops a target to 50-from-KO and forces a switch, setting up
  Bring Down.
- **Machamp (20/100) [via Rare Candy, 1-0-1]** — anti-SP / anti-Basic tech. Takes down
  Basic Pokémon (including SP) cheaply; Hurricane Punch off a DCE can hit big. The 3
  Rare Candy exist largely to jump straight to Machamp (and Gardevoir/Gallade).
- **Dusknoir (1/100) [Duskull evolves same turn]** — **Dark Palm** Poké-Power shuffles a
  benched opposing Pokémon back into the deck (disruption vs. wide benches); Hard
  Feelings for scaling damage.

## Support / tech (why the 1-ofs)
- **Spiritomb (32/99) ×3** — early-game shield/setup: hide behind it while you build,
  and (Keeper of the deck era) slow the opponent's Trainers. Great vs. faster decks.
- **Claydol (15/106)** — Cosmic Power draw engine (note: your own Claydol Power is off
  under an opponent's lock too, so sequence carefully).
- **Azelf LV.X / Azelf / Uxie** — Psychic support: Azelf LV.X negates weakness (key in
  mirror and vs. CurseGar), pixies dig/draw.
- **Judge ×1 / Looker's Investigation ×2 / Lucian's Assignment** — hand disruption to
  pair with Psychic Lock: strip resources while they can't use Powers to recover.
- **DCE ×4 + Call Energy ×3** — DCE powers Psychic Lock / Psychic Cut / Machamp's
  Hurricane Punch; **Call Energy** grabs two Basics early (turn-1 setup).
- **Expert Belt ×2** — +20/+20, usually on Gallade or Dusknoir to push a Bring Down
  setup, or to make Psychic Lock hit harder.
- **Moonlight Stadium ×2 + Warp Point** — Psychic switching/recovery and gust effects;
  Moonlight helps retreat your Psychic types.

> Note (collection accuracy): Pramawat's own Worlds report says he ran the **Stormfront
> Machop** (retreat 1); the packaged WC deck printed the wrong Machop (retreat 2).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** hide behind **Spiritomb**, or **Call Energy** to fetch two Basics (Ralts
  + support). Begin building Ralts → (Rare Candy) → Gardevoir/Gallade.
- **Early turns:** get the first **Psychic Lock** online ASAP — every turn they can't use
  Powers is a turn they fall behind against your toolbox.

## The core loop
Psychic Lock every turn for 60 + Power denial; when a specific threat needs removing,
switch to the right tool — **Gallade** to 1HKO a big attacker, **Machamp** vs. Basics/SP,
**Dusknoir** to shuffle away a key benched Pokémon — then use **Sonic Blade + Teleport +
Bring Down** to convert damaged targets into clean KOs. Layer **Judge/Looker** so they
can't rebuild while locked.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Establish the lock early**, then keep it up — your disruption Supporters are far
  stronger while their Powers are off.
- **Watch your own most-damaged Pokémon** before using Bring Down (it can KO yours).
- **Pick the right attacker for the matchup** rather than forcing Gardevoir into
  everything — the toolbox is the point.
- Manage bench size vs. their damage (e.g. vs. Jumpluff, a wide bench feeds their Mass
  Attack; Dusknoir can trim benches).

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Speed** — SP decks (LuxChomp) are faster and can go up on prizes; you win by missing
  their attack turns via lock + disruption, not by racing.
- **Cards that ignore the lock's value** (fast Basic aggro that doesn't need Powers) can
  pressure you before the lock matters.
- Your own reliance on Powers (Telepass/Claydol) means *opposing* Psychic Lock / Power
  denial hurts you too.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Psychic Lock: **60 damage + no opponent Poké-Powers** next turn.
- Psychic Cut (Gallade): **60 +20 per prize flipped**, up to ~180 (1HKO range).
- Sonic Blade: drop target to **50 HP from KO** + force switch → Bring Down finish.
- Setup: Stage 2 attackers via **Rare Candy** (Ralts base, skip Kirlia).
