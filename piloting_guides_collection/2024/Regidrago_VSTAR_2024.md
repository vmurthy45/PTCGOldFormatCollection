# How to Pilot — Regidrago VSTAR (2024)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet Standard 2024 (TWM-on, the Teal Mask Ogerpon era). This build
mirrors Evan Pavelski's 2024 Senior World Championship-**winning** Regidrago VSTAR — the
single most popular deck at Worlds 2024._

## One-line identity
"All of Pokémon's best dragons in one deck." **Regidrago VSTAR's Apex Dragon** copies the
attack of **any Dragon Pokémon in your discard pile** — so you discard a toolbox of dragons
and pick the perfect attack each turn: **Dragapult ex's Phantom Dive** (200 + spread),
**Giratina VSTAR's Lost Impact** (280 nuke), **Hisuian Goodra VSTAR's** shield, or **Kyurem's
Trifrost** (multi-prize). **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex + Energy Switch** powers it up by turn 2.

## The meta it lived in
2024 Standard was one of the widest metagames ever — 19+ viable decks (Charizard ex, Lugia,
Raging Bolt, Iron Thorns, Gardevoir, Miraidon…). Regidrago was the **anti-meta toolbox**: it
had favorable matchups into Charizard ex and Gardevoir ex, could pivot its attack to whatever
the board demanded, and held **four of the Top 8** at the Masters World Championship while
winning the Senior Division. It was the most popular deck at Worlds 2024 and, later in the
year, came to be regarded by many as the **best deck in the format (BDIF)** — it dominated
LAIC with six of the Top 12. Its flexibility is unmatched — the deck rarely has a truly dead
matchup.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Regidrago V (135/195) ×4 → Regidrago VSTAR (136/195) ×3** — the core. **Apex Dragon**
  (3 Energy): use the attack of any Dragon in your discard — the whole toolbox. **Legacy Star**
  (VSTAR Power, once per game): discard top 7, then take **2 cards from discard to hand** —
  digs deep *and* recovers key pieces (Energy Switch, Prime Catcher). **Save Legacy Star** as
  your out to Iono/Unfair Stamp disruption — don't reflexively fire it turn 2.
- **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex (25/167) ×3** — the engine. **Teal Dance** (Ability): attach a Grass
  Energy from hand to Ogerpon + draw a card — draw *and* acceleration in one, every turn.
  **Energy Switch** then moves that Grass onto Regidrago to power Apex Dragon by turn 2. Its
  **Myriad Leaf Shower** also 1HKOs a Charizard ex (Grass) — a real attacker when needed.
- **Dragapult ex (130/167) ×2** — the primary discard target. **Phantom Dive**: 200 to the
  Active + **6 damage counters spread** on the bench — 2-shots anything and snipes/softens
  their board (great vs. Charizard/Gardevoir setup Pokémon and Dusknoir).
- **Energy Switch ×4** — move the Teal-Dance Grass from Ogerpon onto Regidrago; the accelerant
  that enables the turn-2 attack.

## The dragon toolbox (why each 1-of)
- **Giratina VSTAR (131/196) ×1** — **Lost Impact** via Apex Dragon: **280** (put 2 Energy in
  the Lost Zone) — the big nuke for high-HP targets a Phantom Dive can't reach.
- **Hisuian Goodra VSTAR (136/196) ×1** — **Rolling Iron**: a defensive attack that reduces
  incoming damage — survivability vs. decks that would OHKO Regidrago.
- **Kyurem (47/64) ×1** — **Trifrost**: 110 to each of 3 of the opponent's Pokémon (discard
  Kyurem's Energy — cheaper on Regidrago at 3) — a multi-prize/spread finisher, great vs.
  Stage-2 decks (snipe their Basics before they evolve).
- **Mew ex (151/165) ×1** — **Restart** draw (fill hand to a size) — consistency when your hand
  is small.
- **Squawkabilly ex (169/193) ×1** — **Squawk and Seize** (a turn-1 Professor's Research) —
  discard your hand, draw, *and* seed Dragons into the discard for Apex Dragon.
- **Fezandipiti ex (38/64) ×1** — **Flip the Script**: draw 3 after a Pokémon was KO'd last
  turn — comeback draw.
- **Hawlucha (127/172) ×1** — **Flying Entry**: chip the bench (helps Phantom Dive KO 70-HP
  Basics).

## Support / tech (why the counts)
- **Professor's Research ×4 / Iono ×3 / Boss's Orders ×2** — draw + discard (seed Dragons) +
  disruption + gust. **Iono** disrupts *and* refills; **Boss's Orders** pulls the KO target.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Nest Ball ×4 / Earthen Vessel ×4** — search + discard: **Earthen Vessel**
  (discard 1 → 2 Basic Energy) and Ultra Ball both put Dragons in the discard for Apex Dragon.
- **Superior Energy Retrieval ×1 / Super Rod ×1** — recover Energy/Pokémon (recur attackers +
  refuel).
- **Prime Catcher ×1 (ACE SPEC) / Switch ×1** — Prime Catcher gusts *and* switches (the
  premier disruption/KO tool); Switch pivots.
- **Canceling Cologne ×1** — shut off an opposing Ability for a turn (answers Mimikyu's
  Safeguard, etc.).
- **Temple of Sinnoh ×1** — Stadium: turns off Special Energy (a tech vs. Lugia's Special-
  Energy engine / V Guard Energy math).
- **7 Grass + 3 Fire** — Grass fuels Teal Dance/Apex Dragon; Fire enables specific dragon
  attacks (Radiant Charizard-style / off-costs).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** get **Regidrago V** down + a **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex**; **Teal Dance** a Grass;
  **Squawkabilly's Squawk and Seize** to dig and seed a **Dragapult ex** into the discard.
- **Turn 2:** **Energy Switch** the Grass onto Regidrago, attach + evolve to **Regidrago VSTAR**,
  and **Apex Dragon → Phantom Dive (200 + spread)** — off to the races.

## The core loop
Discard the right dragon → **Apex Dragon** copies its attack: **Phantom Dive** to 2-shot +
spread, **Lost Impact** to nuke, **Trifrost** for multi-prize, **Goodra** to tank. **Teal
Dance + Energy Switch** re-power Regidrago each turn; **Boss's Orders/Prime Catcher** pick the
KO; **Legacy Star** (saved for late) recovers pieces and beats Iono. You take prizes on
multiple fronts and adapt to any board.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Seed the discard first** — Apex Dragon copies dragons *in the discard*; Squawkabilly/Ultra
  Ball/Earthen Vessel exist to put Dragapult (and the right tech) there. No discarded dragon =
  no attack.
- **Save Legacy Star** — it's your once-per-game out to Iono/Unfair Stamp; don't waste it turn
  2 unless you must.
- **Pick the attack for the board** — Phantom Dive (spread/2HKO), Lost Impact (nuke), Trifrost
  (vs. evolution decks / multi-prize), Ogerpon Myriad Leaf Shower (vs. Charizard). This choice
  is the skill.
- **Turn-2 Apex Dragon** is the tempo goal — Teal Dance + Energy Switch is the line.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Iono / Unfair Stamp disruption** — hits your combo hand; you rely on Legacy Star + Ogerpon
  draw to recover (hence saving the VSTAR Power).
- **Raging Bolt ex** — a high-HP attacker where Phantom Dive can't take multi-prize turns; a
  grind (Giratina/Kyurem help).
- **Prized key pieces** — if Ogerpon or your dragons are prized, setup slows badly.
- **Fast aggro that races the turn-2 setup** — you need the turn-2 attack; a slow open falls
  behind.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Apex Dragon: **use any Dragon's attack from your discard** (3 Energy).
- Phantom Dive: **200 + 6 counters spread**; Lost Impact: **280**; Trifrost: **110 × 3 Pokémon**.
- Teal Dance: **attach 1 Grass from hand to Ogerpon + draw 1** (Ability); Energy Switch moves it.
- Legacy Star (VSTAR): discard top 7, **take 2 from discard** (once per game — save for Iono).
