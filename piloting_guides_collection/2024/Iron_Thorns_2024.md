# How to Pilot — Iron Thorns (2024)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet Standard 2024. "Crushing Thorn" — this build mirrors Fernando
Cifuentes's 2024 Masters World Championship-**winning** Quad Iron Thorns ex deck._

## One-line identity
The ultimate disruption/stall deck: **Iron Thorns ex's Initialization** turns off **all
Abilities of your opponent's Rule-Box Pokémon** (ex, V, Radiant) while it's Active — a
built-in Path to the Peak that never leaves. Behind that lock you strip their Energy with
**Crushing Hammer**, drag up and KO their setup Pokémon with **Pokémon Catcher/Prime
Catcher**, and grind out the game with a lone 230-HP wall that just won't die.

## The meta it lived in
Worlds 2024's expected field — Regidrago, Lugia/Archeops, Charizard ex, Raging Bolt,
Gardevoir — was **entirely built on Rule-Box Abilities** (Teal Dance, Infernal Reign, Fezandipiti,
Radiant Greninja). Iron Thorns' Initialization shuts every one of them off, and Cifuentes
rode this **four-Pokémon, coin-flip-heavy disruption deck** undefeated through Day 1 to the
title. It's a slow, high-variance control deck that "did what Path to the Peak used to do" —
except it's stapled to your Active attacker.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Iron Thorns ex (77/167) ×4** — the entire deck. **Initialization** (Ability, while Active):
  your opponent's **Rule-Box Pokémon can't use Abilities** — a permanent Ability-lock. **Volt
  Cyclone**: 140 + move an Energy from Iron Thorns to a benched Iron Thorns (sets up your *next*
  attacker while you swing). 230 HP makes it a real chore to KO. Four copies = you always open
  with one and can chain them (Legacy-style) as they trade.
- **Future Booster Energy Capsule (164/182) ×3** — Tool: +20 damage to the Active *and* **free
  retreat** for your Future Pokémon — negates Iron Thorns' brutal 4-Energy retreat (the key
  pivot enabler) and pushes Volt Cyclone to 160 (two attacks = 320).
- **Technical Machine: Turbo Energize (179/182) ×1** — a TM that accelerates Energy from deck —
  helps you reach Volt Cyclone's cost faster (there's no natural Energy acceleration otherwise).
- **7 Lightning + 4 Double Turbo Energy** — Lightning powers Volt Cyclone; **Double Turbo**
  (2 Energy, −20 damage) speeds the setup (Volt Cyclone still 120 with it).

## The disruption package (why the counts)
- **Crushing Hammer ×4** — flip a coin; heads → discard an Energy from an opponent's Pokémon.
  The signature card: against Ability-acceleration decks (Gardevoir, Ogerpon) it can cost them
  a whole turn. High variance, high impact.
- **Pokémon Catcher ×4** — flip a coin; heads → gust a benched Pokémon Active. Chase down and KO
  their setup/support Pokémon while they're locked under Initialization.
- **Prime Catcher (157/162) ×1 (ACE SPEC)** — a *guaranteed* gust + switch (no coin flip) — the
  premier version of the Catcher effect for the KO that matters.
- **Canceling Cologne ×2** — shut off an opposing Active Ability for a turn — critically
  **answers Mimikyu** (whose Safeguard would make it immune to your ex attacker) so Volt Cyclone
  can KO it.
- **Techno Radar ×2** — search 2 Future Pokémon (find your Iron Thorns fast — you only run 4).

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Arven ×4** — search a Tool (Future Booster Capsule) + an Item (Crushing Hammer/Catcher) —
  the consistency engine that assembles your disruption.
- **Professor's Research ×3 / Judge ×3 / Boss's Orders ×3 / Iono ×1** — draw + disruption + gust.
  **Judge** (both hands to 4) is your main hand-disruption (kicks decks that need lots of cards,
  like Gardevoir/Lost Zone); **Boss's Orders** is the guaranteed gust to complement the coin-flip
  Catchers.
- **Colress's Tenacity ×2** — search a Stadium + an Energy (finds **Lost City** + Lightning).
- **Penny ×1 / Giovanni's Charisma ×1** — **Penny** picks up a damaged Iron Thorns (heal by
  re-benching / deny a prize); **Giovanni's Charisma** adds damage for an exact KO.
- **Pokégear 3.0 ×4** — dig for the Supporter you need (Judge/Arven/Boss).
- **Earthen Vessel ×1 / Energy Loto ×1 / Lost Vacuum ×1** — Energy search/draw, and Lost Vacuum
  removes a pesky Tool/Stadium (or your own Booster Capsule when you need to re-Tool).
- **Lost City ×3** — Stadium: KO'd Pokémon go to the **Lost Zone** (not discard) — **shuts off
  Regidrago** (no dragons in the discard to copy) and denies all recovery. A key anti-meta
  Stadium.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1 (go first):** open **Iron Thorns ex** Active (you always do — it's your only Pokémon)
  → **Initialization** immediately locks their Abilities. Start attaching Lightning; **Arven** for
  a Booster Capsule + a Crushing Hammer.
- **Turn 2–3:** **Crushing Hammer** their Energy, **Catcher/Boss** their setup Pokémon, and
  **Volt Cyclone** for 140–160 while charging the next Iron Thorns.

## The core loop
Keep an **Iron Thorns ex** Active so Initialization stays on → **disrupt** every turn (Crushing
Hammer their Energy, Judge/Iono their hand, Catcher/Boss + KO their locked-out setup Pokémon) →
**Volt Cyclone** to chip and pass Energy to the next Iron Thorns → **Lost City** to deny
recovery/Regidrago. They can't use their engine; you grind them out from behind the wall.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Keep Iron Thorns Active** — Initialization only works from the Active spot; **Future Booster
  Capsule** (free retreat) is how you pivot between Iron Thorns without stranding Energy.
- **Go first** — you want the turn-1 lock and the Energy runway to Volt Cyclone by turn 3.
- **Disrupt the setup, not just the Active** — Catcher/Boss their key Pokémon *while* it's
  locked and KO it; that's how you convert the lock into prizes.
- **Canceling Cologne for Mimikyu** — the classic Iron Thorns counter (Safeguard) is answered by
  Cologne → then Volt Cyclone KOs it.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Mimikyu (Safeguard)** — immune to your ex attacker's damage; you *must* Canceling Cologne it
  (or Boss around it) or you can't take the KO.
- **Klawf / non-Ability attackers** — decks that don't rely on Abilities and set up past
  Initialization out-attack your weak 140 damage.
- **Gardevoir ex** — can flood Energy back through Crushing Hammer and out-grind; a frustrating
  matchup even with the lock.
- **Coin-flip variance** — Crushing Hammer/Pokémon Catcher are flips; a cold streak loses games
  the lock should have won.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Initialization: opponent's **Rule-Box Pokémon can't use Abilities** while Iron Thorns is
  Active.
- Volt Cyclone: **140** + move 1 Energy to a benched Iron Thorns (160 with Booster Capsule).
- Iron Thorns ex: **230 HP**; Future Booster Capsule: **+20 + free retreat**.
- Lost City: KO'd Pokémon → **Lost Zone** (shuts off Regidrago / denies recovery).
