# How to Pilot — Ancient Toolbox (2024)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet Standard 2024. "Ancient Box" / Roaring Moon — this build mirrors
Sakuya Ota's 2024 Junior World Championship-**winning** Roaring Moon deck; a fast Darkness
Paradox toolbox._

## One-line identity
A turbo Darkness toolbox: **Professor Sada's Vitality** attaches **2 basic Energy from hand**
(and heals), **Dark Patch** accelerates Darkness from the discard, and **Explorer's Guidance**
draws — so you power up a rotating cast of **Ancient Paradox attackers** fast: **Roaring Moon**
(scaling discard damage), **Flutter Mane** (cheap bench snipe), and **Great Tusk** (a big
Fighting hitter). Flexible, aggressive, and consistent.

## The meta it lived in
Ancient Box was a top-tier aggro-toolbox in 2024's wide field — its strength is **speed +
flexibility**: multiple cheap Ancient attackers, each covering different targets, all powered
by the same Sada's Vitality/Dark Patch engine. **Flutter Mane** in particular is a menace,
1HKOing V/ex Basics for a single Energy. Sakuya Ota won the Junior World Championship with a
Roaring Moon build. It races most decks and picks apart benches before they set up.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Roaring Moon (109/162) ×4** — the main attacker (non-ex, single prize). **Frenzied Gouging**/
  its scaling attack does more as your **discard fills with Energy** (Dark Patch/Sada feed it) —
  a hard-hitting single-prize body that trades up on multi-prize decks. The deck "roars back" as
  the discard grows.
- **Flutter Mane (78/162) ×4** — the star tech. **Restart-style Ability** + cheap attacks:
  **its attack 1HKOs a Basic V/ex for very little Energy**, and it snipes/pressures early. Four
  copies because it's the most efficient attacker in the deck.
- **Great Tusk (97/162) ×2** — a **Fighting** attacker. **Land Collapse** discards the top of the
  opponent's deck (mill/disruption; discards 4 if you played an Ancient Supporter) — and its
  Fighting typing covers Lightning/Colorless targets Roaring Moon can't.
- **Professor Sada's Vitality ×4** — the engine Supporter: attach **2 basic Energy from hand to
  your Ancient Pokémon** + heal 40 — turbo acceleration that also keeps your attackers healthy.
- **Dark Patch ×2** — attach a basic Darkness from the **discard** to a benched Darkness Pokémon —
  extra acceleration (and it feeds Roaring Moon's discard-scaling).

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Fezandipiti ex (38/64) ×1** — **Flip the Script**: draw 3 after a KO last turn — comeback
  draw (a rare 2-prize liability you accept for the draw).
- **Radiant Greninja (46/189) ×1** — **Concealed Cards** draw/cycle + **Moonlight Shuriken**
  snipe (extra consistency + a spread option).
- **Ancient Booster Energy Capsule ×4** — Tool for Ancient Pokémon: **+HP and an attack boost**
  (and it's un-disrupted by Future counters) — makes your attackers bulkier and hit harder.
- **Defiance Band (169/198) ×1** — +damage when you're behind on prizes — an exact-KO/comeback
  tool.
- **Secret Box (163/167) ×1 (ACE SPEC)** — search **4 cards** (a Pokémon, an Item, a Tool, a
  Supporter) — the explosive setup/consistency card that assembles a perfect turn.

## Support / tech (why the counts)
- **Explorer's Guidance ×4** — draw: shuffle hand, draw a number based on... (an Ancient draw
  Supporter) — the deck's main draw engine alongside Sada.
- **Boss's Orders ×1** — gust the KO target.
- **Nest Ball ×4 / Earthen Vessel ×4 / Pokégear 3.0 ×4** — search Basics (Nest), Energy (Earthen
  Vessel discards 1 → 2 Basic Energy, feeding the discard), and dig for Supporters (Pokégear).
- **Night Stretcher ×2 / Super Rod ×1 / Pal Pad ×2 / Superior Energy Retrieval ×1** — recover
  Pokémon/Energy/Supporters (recur attackers + refuel the discard-scaling).
- **Counter Catcher ×2** — a *free* gust when you're behind on prizes (great in an aggro deck
  that sometimes falls a prize behind, then swings back).
- **Ultra Ball ×1 / Hisuian Heavy Ball ×1** — search (Heavy Ball grabs your singleton techs).
- **PokéStop ×3** — Stadium: flip 3, take Items, discard the rest — digs for Items *and* feeds
  the discard (Energy for Dark Patch/Roaring Moon).
- **6 Darkness Energy** — powers the Darkness attackers + Dark Patch + Roaring Moon's discard
  scaling. (Sada attaches any basic Energy, so the count is lean.)

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** bench **Flutter Mane** + **Roaring Moon**; **Explorer's Guidance/Squawk-style
  draw** to set up; **Professor Sada's Vitality** to attach 2 Energy and start attacking.
  **Flutter Mane** often takes an early KO on a V/ex Basic for almost nothing.
- **Seed the discard** (Earthen Vessel/PokéStop) so Roaring Moon scales and Dark Patch is live.

## The core loop
Turbo-power an Ancient attacker with **Sada's Vitality + Dark Patch** → pick the tool for the
target: **Flutter Mane** to snipe/1HKO Basics, **Roaring Moon** to hit hard (scaling with your
Energy discard), **Great Tusk** for Fighting coverage + deck disruption. **Counter Catcher/
Boss** pull the KO; **Ancient Booster Capsule** bulks and boosts; **Fezandipiti/Explorer's
Guidance** refill. You race with single-prize attackers and trade up on their ex/V.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Flutter Mane is your efficiency** — 1HKO a V/ex Basic for a single Energy; lead with it to
  take cheap early prizes.
- **Feed the discard** — Roaring Moon scales with discarded Energy, and Dark Patch pulls from
  there; Earthen Vessel/PokéStop/Superior Energy Retrieval keep it stocked.
- **Sada's Vitality is your tempo** — 2 Energy + heal every turn keeps attackers online and
  alive; it's the engine.
- **Single-prize trade** — your attackers give up 1; you can trade them for their ex/V all day —
  don't over-bench 2-prize liabilities (Fezandipiti aside).

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting / specific Weakness** — Darkness attackers have Weakness exposure (and Great Tusk is
  Fighting, itself hit by Grass/Water in spots).
- **Iron Thorns ex** — Initialization shuts off Fezandipiti/Radiant Greninja Abilities (though
  your core attackers don't need Abilities — it's less crippling than for others).
- **Healing / high-HP walls** — decks that out-heal or out-size your single-prize hits can grind
  you.
- **Running out of steam** — an aggro deck; if you stall a turn or run low on Energy in the
  discard, a control deck can stabilize.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Professor Sada's Vitality: attach **2 basic Energy from hand** to Ancient Pokémon + **heal 40**.
- Dark Patch: **1 basic Darkness from discard → benched Darkness Pokémon**.
- Flutter Mane: cheap attacker that **1HKOs V/ex Basics** for minimal Energy.
- Roaring Moon: **single-prize**, damage **scales with discarded Energy**; Secret Box (ACE SPEC):
  search **4 cards**.
