# How to Pilot — Seismitoad Crobat (2015)

_Format: BCR-on 2015 (XY "control" era). "Seismibats." Seismitoad-EX went 2nd/3rd at
2015 Worlds (Masters); the Crobat build was its most popular form._

## One-line identity
A lock-and-grind control deck: **Seismitoad-EX's Quaking Punch** does 30 *and* stops the
opponent from playing Item cards every turn, while a **Zubat → Golbat → Crobat** line
drops free damage counters on evolve and **Hypnotoxic Laser + Virbank** add poison. The
opponent can't use Items, slowly drowns in chip damage, and you heal/reset with **Super
Scoop Up**.

## The meta it lived in
2015 leaned heavily on Items (Blastoise's combo, VS Seeker, ball search, Night March's
Battle Compressor). Seismitoad punished all of it: Quaking Punch every turn is a soft
Item lock that most decks fold to. It was a season-long menace, taking 2nd and 3rd at
Worlds. Its own weaknesses were energy-acceleration decks and high-Supporter decks that
don't need Items.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Seismitoad-EX (20/111) ×3** — the core. **Quaking Punch**: 30 damage + the opponent
  can't play Items from hand next turn, for a single **DCE**. The lock is the deck. High
  HP; run 3–4 so a prized/KO'd Toad doesn't stop you (and you can keep a "blank" backup).
- **Zubat (31/119) ×4 → Golbat (32/119) ×4 → Crobat (33/119) ×3** — the damage engine.
  **Sneaky Bite** (2 counters) / **Surprise Bite** (3 counters) on any opposing Pokémon
  when you evolve — this is what turns Quaking Punch's measly 30 into real KOs. No
  retreat cost.
- **Hypnotoxic Laser ×4 + Virbank City Gym ×3** — poison (20/turn with Virbank) + sleep;
  the other half of your damage output and a disruption flip.
- **Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×2** — **Set Up**: draw to 6; helps you hit the T1 DCE + Muscle
  Band + Laser + Virbank opener.
- **Mewtwo-EX (54/113) ×2** — off-type attacker (X-Ball) for when you need a bigger hit
  or to answer opposing Mewtwo.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Super Scoop Up ×4** — the utility MVP: pick up a **damaged Seismitoad** and instantly
  replace it (denying KOs), *or* re-use a **Bat's Bite** on replay. Being able to Scoop
  while the opponent *can't* (Item lock) is huge.
- **Muscle Band ×3** — +20; makes Quaking Punch a real 2HKO on EXs.
- **Xerosic ×2** — Supporter that discards an opposing Tool *or* Special Energy — great
  vs. Garbodor's Float Stone and vs. special-Energy decks.
- **Juniper ×4 / N ×3 / Colress ×1** — draw + disruption; N shrinks their hand while
  they're already Item-locked.
- **VS Seeker ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Computer Search (ACE SPEC) ×1** — Supporter recursion
  and search (used before the lock, or on your own turn).
- **Lysandre ×1 / AZ ×1** — gust a target up; AZ picks up a Bat to re-Bite or saves a
  Toad (no flip, unlike Super Scoop Up).
- **4 DCE / 3 Water** — DCE powers Quaking Punch in one attachment; Water for the rare
  Grenade Hammer / off-cost.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1 (ideal):** Shaymin **Set Up** into **DCE + Muscle Band + Hypnotoxic Laser +
  Virbank City Gym** on a Seismitoad → **Quaking Punch**. Now they're Item-locked from
  turn 1 and taking poison.
- **Early turns:** bench Zubats and evolve for free Bite counters; keep the lock up every
  turn.

## The core loop
**Quaking Punch every turn** (announce it "for the thirtieth time") to keep Items locked,
stack **Laser + Virbank** poison and **Bat** evolve-counters to build KOs, and **Super
Scoop Up** to reset a damaged Toad (or re-Bite). **N** keeps their hand thin while
they're locked. You grind six prizes through chip damage while they can't execute their
Item-dependent plan.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Never stop Quaking Punching** — if you're not announcing it, you're probably losing.
  The lock is worth more than the 30 damage.
- **Keep a backup Toad** — ideally one with Muscle Band up; Super Scoop Up momentum
  depends on having another Toad ready.
- **Bank Bite damage** — evolve Bats onto the targets you'll KO; Super Scoop Up + AZ let
  you Bite the same board twice.
- **Xerosic vs. Garbodor** — strip the Float Stone/Tool so their Garbotoxin can't turn
  off your (few) Abilities, and to keep pressure on special-Energy decks.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Energy-acceleration decks** — anything that floods Energy (Bronzong, Primal Kyogre,
  fast Fighting) can overpower a 30-damage attacker before the lock wins.
- **Heavy-Supporter / low-Item decks** — they don't care about the Item lock.
- **Rough Seas / healing** (Manectric-EX decks) — heals off your chip damage; you lose
  the Stadium war.
- **Primal Groudon-EX (Ω Barrier)** — shuts off your Lasers/disruption Items and out-
  muscles you; a very hard matchup.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Quaking Punch: **30 + opponent can't play Items next turn**, for 1 DCE.
- Golbat/Crobat on evolve: **2 / 3 damage counters** on any opposing Pokémon.
- Poison w/ Virbank: **20 between turns**; Muscle Band **+20**.
- Setup: **T1 Quaking Punch** off Shaymin + DCE.
