# How to Pilot — Decidueye (2017 NAIC)

_Format: **PRC–GRI Standard** (June 2017, the North American International Championships
meta — **pre-Burning Shadows**, so **Lysandre** not Guzma, no Golisopod/Gardevoir-GX).
A straight Decidueye-GX build with an Alolan Ninetales-GX support splash._

## One-line identity
A control-toolbox: **Decidueye-GX's Feather Arrow** puts 2 free damage counters on any
Pokémon *every turn* (an Ability), so you grind the whole board down without attacking,
while **Alolan Ninetales-GX** and **Tapu Koko** add snipe and reach. **Lysandre** drags up
the target you want to finish. Death by a thousand arrows.

## The meta it lived in
NAIC 2017 was the PRC-GRI format, right after the Guardians Rising release. The field was
**Vespiquen**, **Metagross-GX**, **Garbodor** variants (Espeon/Garb, Drampa/Garb),
**Greninja BREAK**, **Volcanion**, **Zoroark/Drampa**, and **Decidueye** builds. Decidueye
was a proven contender (Top-tier results at Internationals from John Kettler and Igor
Costa). Crucially, this is the **pre-Guzma** format — your only gust is **Lysandre**, so
setting up KOs is slower and more deliberate than the later Golisopod-era decks.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Rowlet (9/149) ×4 → Dartrix (10/149) ×4 → Decidueye-GX (12/149) ×4** — the core.
  **Feather Arrow** (Ability): once per turn per Decidueye, 2 counters on any opposing
  Pokémon — free board control. **Hollow Hunt-GX** recovers cards from the discard. You run
  the full 4-4-4 because arrows-per-turn *is* the deck.
- **Forest of Giant Plants ×4** — Stadium: evolve the turn a Pokémon is played — essential
  to get multiple Decidueye-GX (a Stage 2) online fast.
- **Alolan Vulpix (21/145) ×2 → Alolan Ninetales-GX (22/145) ×1** — support attacker:
  **Beacon** searches Pokémon, and Ninetales-GX's snipe/Ice Path-GX adds reach and a
  defensive GX wall.
- **Tapu Koko (SM30) ×2** — **Flying Flip** snipe + free-retreat pivot; combines with
  Feather Arrow to reach KO numbers.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Espeon-EX (52/122) ×1** — **Miraculous Shine**: devolve the opponent's Pokémon — wrecks
  a Metagross/evolving setup and combos with arrows for KOs.
- **Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×1** — Set Up burst draw (still legal this format).
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×2** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter (Brigette, N, Lysandre).
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×2 / Lysandre ×1 / Skyla ×1 / Delinquent ×1** — draw + control.
  **Lysandre** is your *only* gust (pre-Guzma) — save it for the KO that matters.
  **Delinquent** discards from the opponent's hand + removes their Stadium (disruption, and
  wins the Stadium war for your Forest).
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×4 / Level Ball ×2 / Rescue Stretcher ×1 / Revitalizer ×1** —
  search, Supporter recursion (VS Seeker still legal — get Lysandre back!), and recover
  Grass evolutions.
- **Field Blower ×2 / Choice Band ×2 / Float Stone ×2** — remove Garbodor's Tool/Parallel
  City, push snipes over KO lines, free retreat.
- **4 Grass + 4 DCE** — Grass for attack costs; DCE for flexible/Colorless attacks.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for Rowlets; drop **Forest of Giant Plants**.
- **Turn 2:** evolve one or two **Decidueye-GX** — the moment they're up, **Feather Arrow**
  starts stacking free damage every turn.

## The core loop
Every turn: fire **Feather Arrow** from each Decidueye (2 counters each), add **Tapu Koko**
snipes, and let the damage pile up across the opponent's board. Use **Lysandre** to pull a
softened Pokémon Active and finish it; **Espeon-EX** to devolve a threat; **Ninetales-GX**
for a bigger swing or Ice Path-GX wall. You control the pace and take prizes off arrows the
opponent can't heal fast enough.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Get multiple Decidueye online** — more owls = more arrows/turn = faster board control.
  Forest + Brigette is the priority.
- **Lysandre is precious (pre-Guzma)** — you have one reliable gust; VS Seeker it back and
  time it for the game-deciding KO.
- **Aim arrows to set up KOs** — pre-place counters so a Koko snipe or Lysandre'd attacker
  dies without a big attack.
- **Field Blower the Garbodor** — Garbotoxin turns off Feather Arrow (your whole engine).

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire (Volcanion)** — Grass attackers are Fire-Weak; Volcanion's Power Heater + Steam Up
  pressures you fast.
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — shuts off Feather Arrow; without it you have very little
  damage. Field Blower is mandatory.
- **Fast, aggressive decks** — this is a slower, pre-Guzma control build; decks that race
  (Volcanion, Drampa/Zoroark) can outpace the arrows.
- **Vespiquen** — non-EX attackers that trade prizes efficiently and don't mind arrows can
  be awkward.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Feather Arrow: **2 counters on any Pokémon, per Decidueye, per turn** (Ability).
- Setup: **turn-1 Forest of Giant Plants** + Brigette → turn-2 Decidueye-GX.
- Gust: **Lysandre only** (this is the pre-Guzma format) — recur with VS Seeker.
- Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**; Espeon-EX devolves a threat.
