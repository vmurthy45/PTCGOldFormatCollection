# How to Pilot — Espeon Garbodor (2017 NAIC)

_Format: **PRC–GRI Standard** (June 2017 NAIC meta — pre-Burning Shadows, **Lysandre**
not Guzma). A Psychic disruption-toolbox that was a genuine NAIC-era deck — and Vig's
favourite deck of this format._

## One-line identity
A punishing control deck: **Garbodor's Trashalanche** does 20 × the opponent's Items in
the discard (a 1HKO against Item-reliant decks), while **Espeon-GX's Psybeam** confuses
and its **Psychic** attack scales with the Energy the opponent has attached — so the deck
punishes *both* Items and Energy. **Garbotoxin** shuts off Abilities on the side. You make
every one of the opponent's plays cost them.

## The meta it lived in
NAIC 2017 (PRC-GRI) was full of Item-heavy and Ability-heavy decks — exactly what this
deck preys on. Trashalanche punishes the Item turbo (Ultra Ball, VS Seeker, Float Stone),
Psychic punishes Energy-loaders, and Garbotoxin cripples Ability engines. It put up strong
results (a writer's own NAIC deck; Top-4 Mexico City Regionals). Its Achilles' heel:
Gardevoir wasn't in this format yet, but **Darkrai** (Psychic-resistant) and **Greninja**
were rough.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Eevee (63/98) ×4 → Espeon-GX (61/149) ×3** — the attacker. **Energy Evolution**
  (Eevee): when you attach a Psychic to Eevee, evolve it instantly into Espeon-GX — a free
  tempo jump. **Psybeam**: 20 + Confusion (buys turns, forces awkward retreats). **Psychic**:
  scales with the *opponent's* attached Energy — 1HKOs over-loaded Pokémon. **Divide-GX**
  spreads damage for multi-KO turns.
- **Trubbish (56/122) ×4 → Garbodor** — the split line:
  - **Garbodor (51/145, "Trashalanche") ×3** — **Trashalanche**: 20 × opponent's Items in
    the discard; the main non-EX attacker (120 HP, trades great).
  - **Garbodor (57/122, "Garbotoxin") ×1** — **Garbotoxin** (with a Tool): all Abilities off.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter (Brigette, Lysandre, N).
- **Flareon (13/98) ×1** (from Eevee) — a Grass/Fire tech attacker (**Flare Blitz**-style)
  that forces certain matchups (e.g. Vespiquen) to change plans; also punishes evolving
  decks.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×4 / Lysandre ×3 / Hex Maniac ×2 / Brigette ×1** — a heavy control
  Supporter suite. **Lysandre** (×3, your only gust pre-Guzma) drags up a target to Psychic/
  Trashalanche; **Hex Maniac** shuts off Abilities for a turn; **N** disrupts.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×3 / Float Stone ×3 / Field Blower ×1 / Rescue Stretcher ×1** —
  search, recursion (VS Seeker → more Lysandre), **Float Stone** (free retreat *and* the Tool
  for Garbotoxin), Tool/Stadium removal, recovery.
- **Choice Band ×3** — +30 vs. GX/EX so Psybeam/Psychic and even Trashalanche hit exact KO
  numbers.
- **8 Psychic + 4 DCE** — a high Psychic count (8) is deliberate: it maximizes the turn-1
  Psybeam and keeps Energy Evolution live; DCE powers Trashalanche/Psychic's Colorless.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for Eevee + Trubbish; ideally a **Psybeam** off
  Energy Evolution to confuse and start chipping. Bench a Trubbish toward Garbodor.
- **Early turns:** decide the plan — set up **Garbotoxin** vs. Ability decks, or hold
  **Trashalanche** as the finisher vs. Item decks, while **Psybeam** stalls.

## The core loop
Chip and disrupt: **Psybeam** (Confusion) to slow the Active, **Psychic** to punish an
over-loaded attacker, **Trashalanche** to blow up an Item-heavy opponent, and **Garbotoxin**
to lock Abilities. **Lysandre** pulls the exact target; **Divide-GX** takes a multi-prize
turn when the board is spread. Every Item/Energy the opponent commits feeds your damage.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Hold your Energy back** — attach Psychic before DCE, and spread attachments so an
  opposing Espeon-GX's Psychic can't punish *you*; Psychic scales off *their* Energy, so
  bait them into over-committing.
- **Pick the right Garbodor** — Garbotoxin vs. Ability decks, Trashalanche vs. Item decks.
- **Lysandre is your only gust** — VS Seeker it back; time it for the KO that wins.
- **Field Blower / remove your own Float Stone** to toggle Garbotoxin when you need it (or
  off when *you* need Abilities).

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Darkrai** — Dark Pokémon resist Psychic; a documented 40-60 (or worse) matchup.
- **Greninja BREAK** — plays "solitaire," ignores your disruption, and out-grinds you; tied
  for the worst matchup.
- **Drampa/Zoroark** — high HP + efficient attacks that don't over-commit Energy/Items give
  Espeon/Garb trouble.
- **Low-Item / low-Ability decks** — blunt both your punishers at once.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Trashalanche: **20 × opponent's Items in the discard**.
- Psychic (Espeon-GX): scales with **the opponent's attached Energy**.
- Psybeam: **20 + Confusion**; Energy Evolution: attach Psychic to Eevee → instant Espeon-GX.
- Gust: **Lysandre only** (pre-Guzma); Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
