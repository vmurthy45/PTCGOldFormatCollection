# How to Pilot — Zoroark Drampa (2017 NAIC)

_Format: **PRC–GRI Standard** (June 2017 NAIC meta — pre-Burning Shadows, **Lysandre**
not Guzma). "The Elusive Fox" — a Zoroark BREAK / Drampa-GX build tuned for NAIC._

## One-line identity
A consistent, flexible Dark deck: **Zoroark BREAK's Mind Jack** scales with the
opponent's bench (and **Foul Play** copies their Active's attack), while **Drampa-GX's
Berserk** hits a hard **180** — the format's benchmark KO number. Zoroark (BKT) draws you
through the deck with **Trade**, and you punish whatever the opponent presents.

## The meta it lived in
NAIC 2017 (PRC-GRI): Metagross-GX, Vespiquen, Garbodor variants (Espeon/Garb, Drampa/Garb),
Greninja, Volcanion, Vikavolt/Tapu Bulu. Zoroark/Drampa was a hyped, versatile pick — its
attacks feed off the opponent's own board and attacks, so it flexes into most matchups. It
notably took **Zoroark BREAK to Top 8 at NAIC** (Andrew Mahone). It's a "your damage comes
from *their* choices" deck, which makes it consistent but weak to opponents who deny it
those angles.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Zorua (89/162) ×4 → Zoroark (91/162) ×4 → Zoroark BREAK (92/162) ×2** — the core.
  Zoroark **BKT (91)** has **Stand In** (free switch Ability) *and* **Mind Jack** (20 ×
  opponent's benched Pokémon). Zoroark **BREAK** keeps that and adds **Foul Play**: copy an
  attack of the Defending Pokémon for a single DCE — a devastating mirror to their own best
  attacker. (Note: this is the BKT Zoroark line, *not* Zoroark-GX — different card, Trade
  not printed here; Stand In is the switch engine.)
- **Drampa-GX (115/145) ×2** — the muscle. **Berserk**: 180 if your opponent has damage/
  played a Supporter (the KO benchmark); **Righteous Edge** chips 30 and knocks an Energy off
  their Active — great vs. Energy-reliant decks. Colorless, so DCE powers it.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter (Brigette, Lysandre, N, Kukui).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Eevee (63/98) ×1 → Flareon (13/98) ×1** — a Grass/Fire tech attacker; a specific answer
  (e.g. Vespiquen / evolving decks) that Mind Jack alone can't handle.
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×3 / Lysandre ×2 / Hex Maniac ×1 / Teammates ×1 / Professor Kukui ×1 /
  Brigette ×1** — draw + reach. **Lysandre** (your only gust pre-Guzma) drags the KO;
  **Kukui** (+20) pushes Mind Jack/Foul Play/Berserk over exact lines (e.g. Foul Play +
  Choice Band + Kukui = 180 on a Volcanion-EX); **Hex Maniac** shuts off Abilities;
  **Teammates** rebuilds after a KO.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×4 / Rescue Stretcher ×2 / Field Blower ×1 / Special Charge
  ×1** — search, recursion (VS Seeker → more Lysandre), recover Zoroark/Drampa, Tool/Stadium
  removal, and **Special Charge** to get DCE back (you lean on it heavily).
- **Choice Band ×3 / Float Stone ×2** — +30 vs. GX/EX for exact KOs; free retreat (Stand In
  covers Zoroark, Float Stone covers Drampa).
- **4 DCE + 4 Rainbow + 5 Darkness** — DCE/Rainbow power Foul Play and Drampa cheaply;
  Darkness for Riotous/Mind Jack's Dark cost and Rainbow flexibility.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for a full **Zorua** squad + a Drampa-GX; attach
  a Darkness/DCE. A strong Zorua-heavy open is what powers the rest of the game.
- **Turn 2:** evolve Zoroark(s), start **Trade**-style digging (Stand In for mobility), and
  either **Mind Jack** (if their bench is wide) or set up **Berserk** for 180.

## The core loop
Draw and dig with your Zoroark engine, then pick the right attack: **Mind Jack** when their
bench is wide (2HKO/KO), **Foul Play** to turn their own best attack against them, or
**Drampa Berserk** for a clean 180. **Righteous Edge** strips Energy from Energy-reliant
decks. **Lysandre + Kukui + Choice Band** line up exact KOs. **Stand In** keeps you mobile
without spending your Supporter.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Set up multiple Zoroark** — the deck's consistency and Mind Jack both want a wide board;
  a Brigette turn 1 is huge.
- **Match the attack to the board** — Mind Jack (wide bench), Foul Play (big-attack Active),
  Berserk (clean 180). Reading which to use is the skill of the deck.
- **Lysandre is your only gust** — VS Seeker it; time it (often with Foul Play/Berserk) for
  the KO that matters.
- **Manage DCE** — Foul Play and Drampa lean on it; Special Charge/careful discarding keep
  you stocked.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting** — Zoroark (Dark) is Fighting-Weak (though this format's Fighting is limited;
  post-rotation Buzzwole/Lycanroc make it worse).
- **Item/Ability-lock or decks that deny board width** — if the opponent keeps a tiny bench,
  Mind Jack falls off, and low-Energy/low-Supporter decks blunt Berserk/Righteous Edge.
- **Metagross-GX / Greninja / big Stage-2 setups** — Drampa/Zoroark "can't really do
  anything to handle its bad matchups"; you pray for a turn-2 Berserk and out-tempo.
- **Decidueye/Vileplume** — Item lock + stall gives this deck (no lock of its own) trouble.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Mind Jack: **20 × opponent's benched Pokémon** (Zoroark BKT/BREAK).
- Foul Play: **copy the Defending Pokémon's attack** for a single DCE.
- Berserk (Drampa-GX): **180** (when the condition is met).
- Gust: **Lysandre only** (pre-Guzma); Kukui **+20**, Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
