# How to Pilot — Greninja (2016)

_Format: XY Standard 2016. "Ninja Blitz" — this build mirrors Cody Walinski's 2016
World Championship **finalist** Greninja BREAK deck._

## One-line identity
A grindy spread-and-lock deck: **Greninja BREAK's Giant Water Shuriken** puts 6 damage
counters on any Pokémon every turn (for a discarded Water) *without attacking*, while
**Greninja's Shadow Stitching** can lock the opponent's Abilities. You chain multiple
Shurikens per turn via free retreat, pick off benched Shaymin-EX, and win on damage the
opponent can never quite answer.

## The meta it lived in
2016 belonged to Night March, and Greninja was one of its best answers — the Shuriken
snipes Joltik/Combee/Shaymin-EX off the bench, and 170-HP Greninja BREAK is far bulkier
than any Night Marcher. The deck's fix for its historically slow setup was **Talonflame**,
which lets a Stage-2 line come online fast. Its enduring claim to fame: reliably KO'ing
opposing benched Shaymin-EX.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Greninja BREAK (41/122) ×3** — the engine. 170 HP; **Giant Water Shuriken**: once per
  turn as your Active, discard a Water to put **6 damage counters (60)** on any opposing
  Pokémon — *before* your attack, and it doesn't cost your attack. Free retreat lets you
  rotate a fresh Greninja BREAK in to fire *another* Shuriken.
- **Greninja (40/122) ×3 + (41/146) ×1** — the Stage 2. **Shadow Stitching**: 40 and
  locks the opponent's Abilities next turn (shuts off Shaymin/Octillery/Trevenant).
  **Moonlight Slash**: up to 80. Free retreat — key to chaining Shurikens.
- **Frogadier (39/122) ×4** — the setup engine. **Water Duplicates**: search up to 3
  Frogadier straight to the bench, skipping the Basic step — this is how a Stage-2 deck
  keeps pace.
- **Talonflame (96/114) ×4** — **Gale Wings**: starts as your Active on turn 1 even though
  it's a Stage 2; **Aero Blitz** does 40 and draws 2. Free retreat. The card that makes
  the deck consistent enough to compete.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Bursting Balloon ×4** — Tool that damages the *attacker* when they hit the holder;
  crucially, it lets you **2-shot Mega Evolutions** and chip toward Shuriken KOs even
  under Ability lock (Garbodor).
- **Rough Seas ×3** — Stadium; heal 30 off your Active/benched Water Pokémon between
  turns. Combined with free retreat, you cycle damaged Greninja out and heal them —
  making 2HKOs very hard for the opponent.
- **Dive Ball ×4 / Level Ball ×2** — search your Water Pokémon (Froakie → Greninja BREAK,
  Octillery if run); the deck's main search.
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×4 / Ace Trainer ×1 / Fisherman ×1 / Pokémon Ranger ×1** — draw +
  utility. **Ace Trainer** disrupts (good after a Frogadier is KO'd, since you're behind
  on prizes); **Fisherman** returns 4 Water to hand (a big Shuriken refuel); **Pokémon
  Ranger** removes effects (frees you from Chaos Wheel / status).
- **VS Seeker ×4 / Super Rod ×2 / Battle Compressor ×1 / Startling Megaphone ×1** —
  Supporter recursion, recover Pokémon/Energy, dig, strip Tools.
- **8 Water + 2 Splash Energy** — Water fuels Shuriken/attacks; **Splash Energy** returns
  a Greninja to hand when KO'd (recur your attackers).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** open **Talonflame** (Gale Wings puts it Active) — Aero Blitz for 40 + draw
  2, or just set up. Bench a **Froakie**, and use **Frogadier's Water Duplicates** to flood
  the bench with Frogadier. By turn 2–3 you have multiple Greninja → Greninja BREAK.

## The core loop
Each turn: **Giant Water Shuriken** (60 counters) on a target *before* attacking, then
free-retreat to a second Greninja BREAK and **Shuriken again** — two or three Shurikens a
turn adds up fast. Attack with **Shadow Stitching** to lock Abilities when it matters, or
Moonlight Slash for damage. **Rough Seas** heals your board; **Fisherman** refuels Water;
**Splash Energy** brings KO'd Greninja back. Snipe **Shaymin-EX** off the bench for cheap
prizes.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Chain Shurikens** — free retreat means multiple 60-counter drops per turn; this is the
  deck's real damage output, not the attacks.
- **Snipe benched Shaymin-EX / support** — the Shuriken hits anything; picking off draw
  engines wins games.
- **Rough Seas + free retreat = near-un-2HKO-able** — cycle damaged Greninja and heal 30
  each turn.
- **Shadow Stitching at the right moment** locks the opponent's whole engine — time it.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — turns off Giant Water Shuriken (your entire engine); with
  Megaphone/Xerosic rotated, your only out is **Lysandre** the Trubbish before it evolves,
  or 2-shotting with Bursting Balloon. The deck's biggest fear.
- **Mega Audino** — high HP + Magical Symphony + Pokémon Center Lady + Parallel City is a
  brutal matchup (it beat Greninja in the 2016 Worlds final).
- **Hex Maniac** — a one-turn Ability lock stops your Shuriken that turn.
- **Slow starts** — it's effectively a Stage-3 deck; a bad Talonflame-less open falls
  behind fast.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Giant Water Shuriken: **6 counters (60)** on any Pokémon, once per turn as Active, for a
  discarded Water — chainable via free retreat.
- Shadow Stitching: **40 + Ability lock** next turn.
- Rough Seas: **heal 30** off your Water Pokémon each turn.
- Setup: **Talonflame T1**, Frogadier's Water Duplicates to flood the bench.
