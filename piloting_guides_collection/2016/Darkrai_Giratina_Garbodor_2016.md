# How to Pilot — Darkrai Giratina (2016)

_Format: XY Standard 2016. "Black Dragon" — this build mirrors Shunto Sadahiro's 2016
Junior World Championship-**winning** list._

## One-line identity
A fast, controlling toolbox: **Max Elixir** + **Double Dragon Energy** power a
turn-2 **Darkrai-EX** whose **Dark Pulse** scales with Darkness Energy in play (up to
180+), while **Giratina-EX's Chaos Wheel** locks the opponent out of Tools, Stadiums,
*and* Special Energy — and **Garbodor's Garbotoxin** shuts off their Abilities. Hit
hard early, then strangle the game.

## The meta it lived in
2016 Standard was full of decks reliant on Abilities (Greninja, Vespiquen, Volcanion's
setup) and Special Energy (Night March's DCE, Mega Rayquaza, other Dragons). Darkrai/
Giratina/Garbodor attacked all of that at once: Chaos Wheel + Garbotoxin is a
double lock, while Dark Pulse provided the raw damage. With Startling Megaphone/Xerosic
rotated, opponents had almost no way to remove Garbodor's Tool. It won Juniors Worlds
and put up strong Masters results.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Darkrai-EX (74/122) ×2** — the main attacker. **Dark Pulse**: 20 + 20 per Darkness
  Energy on *all* your Pokémon. With Max Elixir + Double Dragon (counts as Darkness) +
  Reverse Valley, it quickly reaches 180 for a big EX KO. Note: this is the BKP Darkrai
  (attacker), not the old Dark Cloak one.
- **Giratina-EX (57/98) ×2** — the lock. **Chaos Wheel**: 90 damage *and* the opponent
  can't play Tools, Stadiums, or Special Energy next turn. **Renegade Pulse** (Ability):
  immune to damage/effects from Mega Evolutions. Mostly sits on the bench feeding Double
  Dragon to Darkrai until you want the lock.
- **Garbodor (57/122) ×2** (from Trubbish ×2) — **Garbotoxin**: while it has a Tool
  attached, all Abilities (both players') are shut off. Turns off Greninja's Shuriken,
  Vespiquen/Yanmega engines, Shaymin draw, etc. Attach Float Stone so it can retreat.
- **Yveltal (65/114) ×2** — **Oblivion Wing**: 30 + attach a Darkness from the discard to
  a Pokémon; a non-EX attacker and extra acceleration toward big Dark Pulses.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Max Elixir ×4** — look at the top 6 cards and attach a basic Darkness to a Basic
  Pokémon; the deck's turbo acceleration (this is why it wants a high Darkness count).
- **Double Dragon Energy ×3** — counts as 2 of every type: fuels Giratina's Chaos Wheel
  *and* boosts Darkrai's Dark Pulse (as Darkness) — the glue between the two attackers.
- **Fighting Fury Belt ×3** — +40 HP and +10 damage; makes Darkrai/Giratina bulkier and
  nudges Chaos Wheel/Dark Pulse over KO lines.
- **Float Stone ×3** — free retreat, especially to keep **Garbodor** mobile (and to pivot
  attackers).
- **Reverse Valley ×1 / Parallel City ×1** — Reverse Valley boosts your Dark damage (+10)
  and bumps opposing Stadiums; Parallel City shrinks benches / cuts Water damage.
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×2 / Lysandre ×2 / Xerosic ×1 / Pokémon Ranger ×1 / AZ ×1** — draw +
  control. **Lysandre** drags up Shaymin-EX/support for KOs; **Xerosic** strips a Tool/
  Special Energy; **Pokémon Ranger** frees *you* from an opposing Chaos Wheel/lock; AZ
  heals/resets.
- **Hoopa-EX (36/98) ×1 / Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×2** — Hoopa pulls the EX trio (Darkrai/
  Giratina/Shaymin) for setup; Shaymin burst draw.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×4 / Trainers' Mail ×2 / Super Rod ×1** — search, Supporter
  recursion, dig, recovery.
- **10 Darkness + 3 Double Dragon** — Darkness is the whole point (Max Elixir + Dark
  Pulse scaling).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** **Hoopa-EX** for Darkrai/Giratina/Shaymin, hit **Max Elixir(s)** to bank
  Darkness on a benched Darkrai, and get a **Giratina-EX** down with Double Dragon. By
  turn 2 you can Dark Pulse for a meaningful hit; aim to establish **Garbodor** around
  turn 3.

## The core loop
Accelerate Darkness (Max Elixir + Oblivion Wing) so **Dark Pulse** climbs toward 180 →
attack while **Garbodor** shuts off their Abilities → drop **Chaos Wheel** when you want
to lock Tools/Stadiums/Special Energy and stall them out. **Lysandre** picks off benched
support; **N** keeps their hand thin under the lock. You control the game from a prize
lead.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Bank Darkness aggressively** — Dark Pulse and Max Elixir both want a high Darkness
  count; more Energy in play = bigger hits.
- **Establish Garbodor early** vs. Ability decks (Greninja, Vespiquen, Volcanion) —
  Garbotoxin is often the whole matchup; protect the Tool.
- **Chaos Wheel to stall / vs. Special-Energy decks** — locking DCE/Double Dragon cripples
  Night March and other Dragons.
- **Keep a Float Stone on Garbodor** so it isn't stranded Active.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Yveltal/Garbodor** — **Yveltal BKT (Fright Night)** turns off your Float Stone (and
  Abilities), and Pitch-Black Spear snipes your bench; a genuinely awful matchup.
- **Yveltal-EX** — strong anti-Giratina card; 2HKOs Chaos Wheel Giratina cheaply, so you
  can't lean on the lock into it.
- **Volcanion-EX** — fast Fire pressure (Darkrai has no Fighting Weakness, but Volcanion's
  bench damage + speed race you).
- **Pokémon Ranger / effect removal** — opponents can shrug off your Chaos Wheel lock for
  a turn.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Dark Pulse: **20 + 20 per Darkness Energy on all your Pokémon** (→ ~180 with setup;
  Double Dragon counts as Darkness).
- Chaos Wheel: **90 + opponent can't play Tools / Stadiums / Special Energy** next turn.
- Garbotoxin: **all Abilities off** while Garbodor holds a Tool.
- Max Elixir: attach a basic Darkness from the **top 6** to a Basic Pokémon.
