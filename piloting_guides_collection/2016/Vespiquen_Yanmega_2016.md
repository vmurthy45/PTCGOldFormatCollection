# How to Pilot — Vespiquen/Yanmega (2016)

_Format: XY Standard 2016. A Grass non-EX toolbox pairing **Vespiquen's** discard-
scaling attack with the energy-free **Yanmega BREAK** — the archetype that took the
2016 Senior World Championship (Jesper Eriksen)._

## One-line identity
Two complementary non-EX attackers: **Yanmega BREAK** hits hard for **zero Energy**
(when your hand has exactly 4 cards) as an early-game rush, and as it trades it feeds
the discard for **Vespiquen**, whose **Bee Revenge** scales with *any* Pokémon in the
discard for a powerful late game. Cheap, resilient, and hard to disrupt.

## The meta it lived in
2016 was Night March's format, and the best answers to Night March (Yveltal,
Seismitoad, Greninja, Trevenant) were exactly what Vespiquen was *good* against —
because Bee Revenge scales off any Pokémon (not just Night Marchers) and Vespiquen/
Yanmega have more HP than Joltik/Pumpkaboo, they shrugged off the chip damage that
answered Night March. It won Seniors Worlds and put up strong Masters results.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Vespiquen (10/98) ×4** (from Combee ×4) — the late-game closer. **Bee Revenge**:
  20 + 10 × the number of Pokémon in your discard. Any Pokémon counts, so it can exceed
  Night March's ceiling with a full discard, and it's a Grass type (hits Seismitoad for
  Weakness). Free retreat.
- **Yanmega (7/114) ×4** (from Yanma ×4) — the early rush. **Sonic Vision** (Ability):
  if you have *exactly 4 cards in hand*, Yanmega (and its BREAK) attack for **no Energy**.
  Assault Boom does 50, or 120 if the target has a Tool attached.
- **Yanmega BREAK (8/114) ×3** — 140 HP, keeps Sonic Vision, and adds **Barrier Break**:
  100 damage that ignores all effects on the Defending Pokémon — for zero Energy. Your
  main early attacker; when it's KO'd, 3 Pokémon hit the discard to pump Bee Revenge.
- **Forest of Giant Plants ×4** — Stadium letting you evolve the turn a Pokémon is
  played. This is what makes turn-1 Yanmega/Vespiquen possible (skip the wait on Stage 1
  → Stage 2).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Bursting Balloon ×4** — a Tool that KOs the *attacker* when they hit the holder;
  free damage/deterrent, and it makes opponents hesitate. Reusable pressure.
- **Judge ×2 / N ×2** — draw *and* hand-size control: Judge sets both hands to 4, which
  is exactly the count Sonic Vision wants for a free Yanmega attack (while disrupting the
  opponent).
- **Sycamore ×4 / Lysandre ×1 / AZ ×1** — draw; Lysandre drags up Shaymin-EX/support for
  a KO; AZ resets a damaged attacker.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Level Ball ×2 / VS Seeker ×4 / Battle Compressor ×2** — search and
  discard-loading (Ultra Ball/Compressor put Pokémon in the discard to grow Bee Revenge).
- **Revitalizer ×1 / Special Charge ×1 / Super Rod (implied recovery)** — recur Grass
  Pokémon / DCE so you don't run out of attackers or Energy.
- **Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×3** — Set Up burst draw (note: it's an EX liability vs. Lysandre,
  but the draw is worth it).
- **Spinarak/Ariados (1-1) & Klefki (if run)** — minor tech; **4 DCE** is the only Energy
  (Yanmega needs none; Vespiquen uses DCE).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** with **Forest of Giant Plants**, evolve Yanma → Yanmega (→ BREAK). Manage
  your hand to **exactly 4 cards** (Judge helps) so **Sonic Vision** lets Yanmega BREAK
  **Barrier Break for 100, free**. Meanwhile discard Pokémon (Ultra Ball) to seed Bee
  Revenge.

## The core loop
Rush with **Yanmega BREAK** (free Barrier Break, ignores effects) to take early prizes;
as your Yanmegas trade, they fill the discard. Transition to **Vespiquen** mid-to-late
game, hitting 100–130+ Bee Revenge to 1HKO EXs. Use **Judge** to keep your hand at 4 for
free attacks (and disrupt), **Bursting Balloon** for extra damage/deterrent, and
**Lysandre** to snipe Shaymin-EX.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Hand size 4 = free Yanmega attacks** — track your hand count; Judge is your lever.
- **Save Vespiquen for the late game** — it's your closer once the discard is stacked;
  don't force it early.
- **Barrier Break ignores effects** — it's your out to Glaceon/Regice/effect walls
  (though Pokémon Ranger backs that up if run).
- **Discard Pokémon actively** — Bee Revenge only gets bigger; Ultra Ball/Compressor
  fuel it.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Ability lock (Garbodor Garbotoxin, Hex Maniac)** — turns off **Sonic Vision**, so
  Yanmega suddenly needs Energy it doesn't run. A real problem (Lysandre the Trubbish
  before it evolves).
- **Fire attackers** — both key lines are Grass (Fire-Weak); a Volcanion/Fire deck hits
  hard.
- **Mega Audino** — high HP + Magical Symphony snipes your Combee/Shaymin and out-trades
  Vespiquen.
- **Parallel City / Delinquent** — bench-shrinking and Stadium removal hurt your Grass
  Stadium reliance and setup.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Bee Revenge: **20 + 10 × Pokémon in your discard**.
- Barrier Break (Yanmega BREAK): **100, ignores effects**, free with Sonic Vision.
- Sonic Vision: attack for **0 Energy** when your hand is **exactly 4 cards**.
- Setup: **turn-1 evolve** via Forest of Giant Plants.
