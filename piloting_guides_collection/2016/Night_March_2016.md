# How to Pilot — Night March (2016)

_Format: XY Standard 2016 (Primal Clash–Fates Collide, "BREAKpoint" era). The Tier-0
deck of the format — won nearly half of Masters State Championships._

## One-line identity
The fastest, most consistent aggro deck in the game: **Battle Compressor** dumps
**Joltik / Pumpkaboo / Lampent** into the discard, and each one's **Night March**
attack does 20 × the number of Night Marchers in the discard — up to 200+ for a single
DCE, often on the very first attack. Cheap, single-prize attackers that 1HKO EXs.

## The meta it lived in
Night March was *the* deck to beat in 2016 — speed, power, and consistency all at once.
Two BREAKpoint cards pushed it over the top: **Puzzle of Time** (retrieve 2 DCE from
the discard, breaking the old "only 4 DCE" ceiling) and **Fighting Fury Belt** (Joltik
to 70 HP, dodging Yveltal's Oblivion Wing). It was so dominant that entire decks
(Greninja, Mega Audino, Trevenant) existed mainly to counter it — Night March was the
consensus **best deck in the format (BDIF)** for much of the year.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Joltik (26/119) ×4** — Lightning-type Night Marcher; the primary attacker. 1HKOs
  Yveltal-EX (Lightning Weakness) and hits huge with a full discard.
- **Pumpkaboo (44/119) ×4** — Psychic-type Night Marcher; different typing covers
  matchups Joltik can't, and it survives some attacks Joltik dies to. With **Dimension
  Valley**, its attack costs less Energy.
- **Lampent (42/119) ×4** — the third Night Marcher; you want all 12 (4 of each) in the
  discard for maximum damage. Not usually an attacker — it's fuel.
- **Mew (29/124) ×2** — **Memories of Dawn**: copies Night March while a real Night
  Marcher stays safe on the bench. Free retreat, single prize, expendable — the safe
  way to attack.
- **Dimension Valley ×3** — Stadium reducing Psychic attack costs by a Colorless; makes
  Pumpkaboo/Mew attack for a single DCE (or even free in some lines).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Battle Compressor ×4** — the engine: discard up to 3 Night Marchers (or Supporters
  for VS Seeker) instantly. This is what makes turn-1 lethal possible.
- **Puzzle of Time ×4** — play 2 together to retrieve any 2 cards — usually DCE, so your
  4-DCE limit effectively becomes 8. The card that made Night March nearly
  disruption-proof.
- **VS Seeker ×4 / Trainers' Mail ×3 / Ultra Ball ×4** — dig and set up the discard;
  Ultra Ball also pitches Night Marchers.
- **Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×3** — **Set Up**: draw to 6; the burst-draw that assembles a
  first-turn kill.
- **Sycamore ×3 / N ×1 / Hex Maniac ×2 / Lysandre ×2 / Teammates ×1 / AZ ×1 / Pokémon
  Ranger ×1** — draw + disruption + utility. **Hex Maniac** shuts off Abilities (huge vs.
  Greninja/Trevenant); **Pokémon Ranger** removes effects (frees you from Giratina's
  Chaos Wheel / Regice); **Lysandre** drags up Shaymin-EX for easy prizes.
- **Fighting Fury Belt ×2** — +40 HP (Joltik→70, Pumpkaboo→100) so chip damage (Bats,
  Oblivion Wing) can't trade with you.
- **Special Charge ×1 / Startling Megaphone ×1 / Escape Rope ×1 / Target Whistle ×1** —
  recur DCE, strip Tools, switch tricks, and pull a KO'd Pokémon back onto their bench
  as a Lysandre target.
- **4 DCE** — the only Energy (doubled effectively by Puzzle of Time).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1 (ideal):** Shaymin **Set Up** → **Battle Compressor** the Night Marchers into
  the discard → Ultra Ball/Trainers' Mail to dig → attach **DCE** → **Night March** (or
  Mew's Memories of Dawn) for a huge, often lethal hit. A first-turn KO on an EX is
  routine.

## The core loop
Keep 10–12 Night Marchers in the discard so every swing is 200+. Attack with **Mew** to
keep a real Night Marcher safe on the bench; when Mew dies, promote a fresh Joltik/
Pumpkaboo. **Puzzle of Time** back your DCE so you never run dry, **Lysandre** picks off
Shaymin-EX for cheap prizes, and **Hex Maniac** turns off the Abilities of your counters
when needed. You out-race everything.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Load the discard fully** — damage scales with Night Marchers in the discard; get all
  12 down fast.
- **Attack with Mew when you can** — it protects your actual attackers and gives up only
  1 prize.
- **Belt your attackers vs. chip decks** — 70-HP Joltik dodges Oblivion Wing/Bat damage.
- **Hex Maniac is your out** to Ability-lock counters (Greninja's Shadow Stitching,
  Trevenant, Vileplume); **Pokémon Ranger** answers Chaos Wheel/Regice effects.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Pyroar (Intimidating Mane)** — blocks damage from Basics; Joltik/Pumpkaboo can't
  touch it without Hex Maniac (Mew is Basic too). A hard wall.
- **Item lock (Seismitoad-EX, Trevenant, Vileplume)** — shuts off Battle Compressor/
  Puzzle/VS Seeker; you get one turn of setup before Toad locks you.
- **Mega Audino** — 220 HP wall that feasts on your benched Shaymin-EX and low-HP
  Basics; a bad matchup that beat Night March at 2016 Worlds.
- **Karen / Bunnelby mill / Yveltal BKT (Fright Night)** — resource denial and turning
  off your Fighting Fury Belt.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Night March: **20 × Night Marchers in the discard** (up to ~200, +20 with a Belt on a
  Marcher isn't damage — Belt adds HP, not damage).
- Puzzle of Time (×2): retrieve **any 2 cards** (usually DCE → effectively 8 DCE).
- Fighting Fury Belt: **+40 HP** (Joltik 70, Pumpkaboo 100).
- Setup: **turn-1 lethal** is realistic off Shaymin + Battle Compressor.
