# How to Pilot — Buzz Garb Shrine (2018)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2018, **post-rotation** (SM-on, Celestial Storm era —
Strong Energy/Max Elixir gone). "Buzzwole/Shrine" — a single-prize Fighting control deck
that was a defining post-rotation archetype._

## One-line identity
A single-prize grind deck: attack almost entirely with **non-GX Buzzwole** (Jet Punch
spread + Sledgehammer 1HKOs), lock the opponent's Abilities with **Garbodor's
Garbotoxin**, and let **Shrine of Punishment** quietly place damage on every GX/EX the
opponent benches. You give up few prizes, punish their GX-heavy boards, and win the
attrition war.

## The meta it lived in
After the 2018 rotation (Strong Energy and Max Elixir gone), the format slowed down and
Shrine of Punishment — which had already made noise on Day 1 of Worlds 2018 — became a
menace. In a GX-flooded meta (Zoroark, Rayquaza, Malamar all lean on GX Pokémon), a deck
that's **all single-prize attackers** + Garbotoxin + Shrine flips the prize math: you take
2-prize KOs while giving up 1, and Shrine chips their board for free. It was a consistent
League/Regional performer.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Buzzwole (77/131) ×4** — the main attacker, a **non-GX** (1 prize). **Jet Punch**: 30
  active + 30 bench snipe. **Sledgehammer**: 120 when the opponent has 4 prizes left — a
  single-prize 1HKO on most GXs. Being all-baby-Buzzwole means Shrine doesn't hurt *you*.
- **Trubbish (56/122) ×3 → Garbodor (51/145) ×2** — **Garbotoxin** (with a Tool): all
  Abilities off. Shuts off Zoroark's Trade, Malamar's Psychic Recharge, Octillery — the
  glue of the format's engines.
- **Shrine of Punishment (143/168) ×3** — Stadium: between turns, put damage on **each GX/EX**
  in play. Your board is (almost) all non-GX, so it's a one-sided clock against the field.
  Multiple copies win the Stadium war.
- **Diancie Prism Star (74/131) ×1 + Beast Energy Prism Star ×1** — Fighting damage boosts;
  the modifiers (with Choice Band/Strong) that push Jet Punch/Sledgehammer to exact KO
  numbers now that Strong Energy is scarcer.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Remoraid (32/162) ×2 → Octillery (33/162) ×2** — **Abyssal Hand** draw engine. (Note: an
  Ability — you toggle your own Garbotoxin with Field Blower when you need to draw.)
- **Sycamore ×4 / Cynthia ×3 / N ×3 / Guzma ×4** — a heavy Supporter suite for a Supporter-
  reliant control deck. **4 Guzma** = you always drag up the target to snipe/KO; **N**
  disrupts their comeback.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Nest Ball ×3 / Field Blower ×3 / Rescue Stretcher ×2 / Special Charge ×1**
  — search, bench Basics, remove opposing Tools (and toggle your own Garbotoxin), recover.
- **Float Stone ×3 / Choice Band ×4** — free retreat (and Garbotoxin's Tool); **4 Choice
  Band** is the primary damage modifier to hit KO numbers post-Strong-Energy.
- **4 Strong Energy + 3 Rainbow + 1 Psychic** — Strong (+20 to Fighting) is the key modifier;
  Rainbow enables Garbodor's cost and flexibility; Psychic for off-costs. (Note: Rainbow
  hurts you 10 — but with mostly non-GX attackers and Shrine on your side, the trade is
  fine.)

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** **Jet Punch** early (30 + 30 snipe), bench Trubbish + Remoraid, and get
  **Shrine of Punishment** down so their GX board starts taking chip damage.
- **Establish Garbotoxin** around turn 2–3 vs. Ability decks (Zoroark/Malamar) — it's often
  the whole matchup.

## The core loop
Spread with **Jet Punch**, lock with **Garbotoxin**, and let **Shrine** tick their GX board
down between turns. Use **Guzma** to drag up a softened Pokémon and finish it (often with a
single-prize Buzzwole, keeping the prize trade lopsided). When they hit 4 prizes,
**Sledgehammer** 1HKOs. You trade 1-for-2 and grind them out.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Stay single-prize** — the deck's whole edge is giving up 1 prize per KO while taking 2;
  don't lean on Buzzwole-GX-style 2-prize liabilities unless necessary.
- **Shrine is one-sided** — keep it in play (multiple copies, win the Stadium war); it damages
  *their* GX board, not your non-GX one.
- **Garbotoxin vs. Ability decks; toggle it off** (Field Blower your own Tool) when you need
  Octillery to draw.
- **Play the 4-prize window** for Sledgehammer; sequence KOs so it lands.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Psychic attackers (Malamar/Necrozma, Mewtwo)** — Buzzwole is Psychic-Weak; a Psychic deck
  that ignores Shrine (few GX) is rough.
- **Non-GX / low-GX decks** — Shrine does nothing to them, and they don't fear your prize
  math as much.
- **Fast 1HKO decks** — anything that races before Garbotoxin/Shrine take over.
- **Field Blower decks** — opponents removing Shrine/your Garbotoxin Tool blunt both your
  engines.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Jet Punch: **30 active + 30 bench snipe**; Sledgehammer: **120 at opponent's 4 prizes**
  (single-prize attacker).
- Garbotoxin: **all Abilities off** while Garbodor holds a Tool.
- Shrine of Punishment: damage on **each GX/EX** in play between turns.
- Strong Energy **+20** to Fighting; Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
