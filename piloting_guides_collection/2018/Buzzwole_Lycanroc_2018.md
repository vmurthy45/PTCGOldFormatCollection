# How to Pilot — Buzzwole Lycanroc (2018)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2018 (BKT–FLI, "Buzzroc"). The premier Fighting deck of
the year and the top counter to Zoroark-GX._

## One-line identity
Fast, disruptive Fighting aggression: **Buzzwole-GX's Jet Punch** does 30 to the Active
*and* 30 to a benched Pokémon for a single Energy, **Lycanroc-GX's Bloodthirsty Eyes**
drags up whatever you want to KO (no Supporter needed), and **Beast Ring** + **Sledgehammer**
give you explosive comeback turns. You dismantle the opponent's board and take prizes a
turn early.

## The meta it lived in
Buzzroc was Tier 1 all year and the deck that kept Zoroark-GX honest — Buzzwole hits
Zoroark for Weakness and can 1HKO it with the right modifiers. It's a highly disruptive
"take down whatever they set up" deck: between 4 Guzma and 2 Lycanroc-GX you have **six**
ways to drag up a target. With Beast Ring, it also has some of the best comeback turns in
the format once the opponent is at 4 prizes.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Buzzwole-GX (57/111) ×2** — the star. **Jet Punch**: 30 active + 30 bench snipe for one
  Fighting (Landorus-EX's old attack). **Knuckle Impact**: 160 (can't attack next turn —
  pivot out). **Absorption-GX**: 40 × your remaining prizes (a huge early nuke).
- **Buzzwole (77/131) ×3** — the non-GX "baby" Buzzwole. **Sledgehammer**: 120 if the
  opponent has 4 prizes left — a single-prize attacker that 1HKOs GXs at the right moment.
  Bulky and hard for Zoroark to profitably KO.
- **Rockruff (75/131) ×2 → Lycanroc-GX (74/145) ×2** — **Bloodthirsty Eyes** (Ability): when
  it evolves, switch one of the opponent's benched Pokémon to Active — a free gust to line
  up KOs. **Dangerous Rogue-GX** scales with their bench for a big finish; **Claw Slash**
  hits hard with modifiers.
- **Remoraid (32/162) ×2 → Octillery (33/162) ×2** — **Abyssal Hand** draw engine; the
  deck's consistency glue ("without Octillery this deck would be garbage").

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Diancie Prism Star (74/131) ×1** — boosts your Fighting attackers +20; the modifier that
  turns Jet Punch/Sledgehammer/Claw Slash into exact KO numbers on Zoroark/Lycanroc.
- **Regirock (80/168) ×1** — another Fighting damage boost / bulky body for exact math.
- **Max Elixir ×2** — accelerate a basic Fighting from the top 6 onto a Basic — speeds up
  Knuckle Impact / a second attacker.
- **Beast Ring ×2** — when the opponent has 4 or fewer prizes, attach **2 basic Fighting from
  the deck** to your Ultra Beasts — an explosive refuel that powers a comeback KO (or two).
- **Sycamore ×4 / Cynthia ×3 / Guzma ×3 / N ×2** — draw + gust. High Guzma count = you always
  choose the target (stacks with Bloodthirsty Eyes).
- **Ultra Ball ×3 / Field Blower ×2 / Super Rod ×1 / Brooklet Hill ×3** — search, Tool/Stadium
  removal, recovery, and **Brooklet Hill** to fetch a Fighting/Water Basic to the bench each
  turn.
- **Fighting Fury Belt ×2 / Choice Band ×2 / Float Stone ×2** — +40 HP/+10, +30 vs. GX/EX,
  free retreat (Buzzwole's retreat is heavy).
- **9 Fighting + 4 Strong Energy + 1 Beast Energy Prism** — Strong Energy (+20 to Fighting)
  and Beast Energy are the damage modifiers that hit KO numbers; Beast Energy also gives
  extra when your Beast is Active.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1 (going 2nd is great):** **Jet Punch** immediately — 30 active + 30 to a key
  benched Pokémon (a Rockruff, Remoraid, Zorua). Bench Rockruff and Remoraid.
- **Early turns:** stack Jet Punch snipes + **Bloodthirsty Eyes** to line up KOs; keep the
  opponent from stabilizing.

## The core loop
Jet Punch to spread (active + bench snipe) → **Bloodthirsty Eyes / Guzma** to drag up the
softened target → KO it with Buzzwole-GX or a buffed baby Buzzwole. Once they hit 4 prizes,
**Sledgehammer** 1HKOs and **Beast Ring** refuels a fresh Buzzwole for a huge swing. Use
**Absorption-GX** early (it's biggest when you have the most prizes) for a nuke, and
**Dangerous Rogue-GX** to close.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Snipe to set up multi-prize turns** — Jet Punch's 30 bench + a KO = tempo the opponent
  can't match.
- **Play the prize count** — Sledgehammer wants them at 4; Absorption-GX wants *you* at 6.
  Sequence KOs so your big attacks land at the right prize totals.
- **Six gusts (4 Guzma + 2 Lycanroc)** — nothing on their board is safe; pull up the piece
  that breaks their plan.
- **Diancie/Strong/Choice Band math** — know your exact numbers to 1HKO Zoroark/Lycanroc.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Psychic (Malamar/Necrozma, Mewtwo, Mew-EX)** — Buzzwole-GX is Psychic-Weak; Dawn Wings
  Necrozma and Mewtwo punish it (lean on Lycanroc, which doesn't share the Weakness).
- **Decks that avoid the 4-prize window** — a careful opponent taking double-KO turns denies
  your Sledgehammer/Beast Ring.
- **Mirror / other Fighting** — comes down to snipe sequencing and modifier math.
- **High-HP walls without Psychic Weakness** — can be awkward for baby Buzzwole to close.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Jet Punch: **30 active + 30 bench snipe** for 1 Fighting.
- Sledgehammer (baby Buzzwole): **120 when opponent has 4 prizes** (single-prize attacker).
- Beast Ring: attach **2 basic Fighting from deck** to your Beasts when opponent ≤4 prizes.
- Absorption-GX: **40 × your remaining prizes**; Bloodthirsty Eyes: **free gust on evolve**.
