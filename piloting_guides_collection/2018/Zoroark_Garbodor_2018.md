# How to Pilot — Zoroark Garbodor (2018)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2018 (BKT–FLI, the Zoroark-GX era). "ZoroGarb" — this
build mirrors Stephane Ivanoff's 2018 World Championship-**winning** list._

## One-line identity
The consistency king of the format with a disruption package bolted on: **Zoroark-GX's
Trade** draws you 2 cards a turn (an Ability), **Riotous Beating** hits 20 × your
Pokémon, and a split **Garbodor** line either shuts off all Abilities (**Garbotoxin**)
or punishes Item decks (**Trashalanche**). You out-draw everyone, then lock or grind
them out.

## The meta it lived in
2018 Standard was *the* Zoroark-GX year — it was the best card in the format, and five of
the top decks were built on it. Stephane Ivanoff took ZoroGarb all the way to the 2018
World Championship (beating Tord Reklev in the final). Garbotoxin was the key edge: it
turns off the mirror's Trade, Malamar's Psychic Recharge, and every other Ability engine,
letting a fair-and-square Zoroark deck grind ahead.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Zorua (52/73) ×4 → Zoroark-GX (53/73) ×4** — the core. **Trade** (Ability): once per
  turn, discard a card to draw 2 — the best draw engine in the game and the reason you set
  up multiple. **Riotous Beating**: 20 × your Pokémon in play (up to ~120+) for a DCE.
  **Trickster-GX** copies any attack in a pinch.
- **Trubbish (56/122) ×3 → Garbodor** — the split line (the deck's disruptive teeth):
  - **Garbodor (51/145, "Garbotoxin") ×2** — with a Tool attached, all Abilities off (both
    players). Shuts off the mirror's Trade and Malamar. The primary plan in most matchups.
  - **Garbodor (57/122, "Trashalanche") ×1** — 20 × the opponent's Items in the discard; a
    finisher vs. Item-heavy decks.
- **Kartana-GX (70/111) ×1** — a **Metal** attacker; **Blade-GX** and its cheap attack give
  you a non-Dark angle and a body that dodges Fighting Weakness in spots.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — **Wonder Tag**: search any Supporter (Brigette, Guzma, N).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Puzzle of Time ×4** — play 2 together to retrieve any 2 cards from the discard — the
  recursion engine that lets you loop DCE, Guzma, Field Blower, or a KO'd Zoroark endlessly.
- **Mysterious Treasure ×4 / Ultra Ball ×0 (Treasure fills the role) / Evosoda ×3** —
  search: **Mysterious Treasure** (discard a card → any Psychic/Dragon, grabs Zorua/Trubbish/
  Tapu Lele), **Evosoda** evolves without a Supporter, keeping tempo.
- **N ×4 / Brigette ×3 / Guzma ×2 / Sycamore ×1 / Cynthia ×1** — draw + setup + disruption.
  **Brigette** (×3) is the turn-1 board-builder (get 3 Basics down); **N** disrupts and
  refuels; **Guzma** gusts the KO.
- **Field Blower ×3 / Enhanced Hammer ×1 / Rescue Stretcher ×1 / Town Map ×1** — remove
  Tools/Stadiums (and your own Garbotoxin Tool when *you* need Trade back), strip special
  Energy, recover, and prize-fix.
- **Float Stone ×3 / Choice Band ×2 / Parallel City ×2** — free retreat (and Garbotoxin's
  Tool), +30 vs. GX/EX, and a Stadium that shrinks benches / cuts damage.
- **4 DCE + 3 Unit Energy (LPM)** — DCE powers Riotous Beating; **Unit Energy
  Lightning/Psychic/Metal** enables Kartana and off-costs.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for Zorua ×2–3 + Trubbish; ideally a Zorua-heavy
  board. Pass.
- **Turn 2:** evolve into **Zoroark-GX**, start **Trade** (2 cards/turn), and either
  **Riotous Beating** for a KO or set up **Garbotoxin** depending on the matchup.

## The core loop
Trade every turn to churn through the deck, take 2HKOs with **Riotous Beating**, and use
**Puzzle of Time** to recur whatever you need (DCE, Guzma, a KO'd Zoroark). Set up
**Garbotoxin** to lock the opponent's Abilities (crucial in the mirror and vs. Malamar), or
hold **Trashalanche** vs. Item decks. **Guzma + Choice Band** line up the exact KO; **N**
keeps them off-balance.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Brigette turn 1** is the whole setup — a wide Zorua board = big Riotous Beating + more
  Trade next turn.
- **Garbotoxin in the mirror** — turning off the opponent's Trade while keeping yours (via
  Field Blower toggling your own Tool) usually wins the ZoroGarb mirror.
- **Manage your Item count** vs. your *own* Trashalanche isn't a risk, but watch the
  opponent's Trashalanche against you — Puzzle/Mysterious Treasure pile up Items.
- **Puzzle of Time is your recursion** — plan two-Puzzle turns to reclaim the exact pieces.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting (Buzzwole/Lycanroc, Buzz/Shrine)** — Zoroark-GX is Fighting-Weak; Jet Punch +
  Sledgehammer + Beast Ring punishes it. The defining bad matchup.
- **Shrine of Punishment decks** — passive damage on your GX-heavy board adds up.
- **Opposing Garbotoxin** — turns off *your* Trade; you must Field Blower it or race.
- **Big 1HKO decks (Ultra Necrozma/Malamar)** — can out-damage your 2HKO grind.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Trade: **discard 1 → draw 2**, once per turn per Zoroark (Ability).
- Riotous Beating: **20 × your Pokémon in play** for a DCE.
- Garbotoxin: **all Abilities off** while Garbodor holds a Tool.
- Puzzle of Time (×2): **retrieve any 2 cards**; Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
