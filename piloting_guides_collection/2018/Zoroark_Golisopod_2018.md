# How to Pilot — Zoroark Golisopod (2018)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2018 (BKT–FLI). "ZoroPod" — a Tier-1 Zoroark-GX variant
(Masters Worlds Top-8 archetype)._

## One-line identity
Consistency meets efficiency: **Zoroark-GX's Trade** draws you through the deck every turn
while **Riotous Beating** provides scaling damage, and **Golisopod-GX** adds a hyper-
efficient **First Impression (120 for one Grass)** as a second, harder-hitting attacker.
Zoroark's free-switch **Stand In** and Acerola reset Golisopod so First Impression fires
every turn.

## The meta it lived in
ZoroPod was one of the strongest Zoroark-GX pairings of 2018 (Masters Worlds Top-8). It
kept Zoroark's best-in-format consistency but gained a bigger single hit (First Impression)
and a Grass typing to trade into the field. Its natural predator was the same as every
Zoroark deck's — Fighting (Buzzwole) — but its efficiency and Acerola resets let it grind
most of the field.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Zorua (52/73) ×4 → Zoroark-GX (53/73) ×4** — the core. **Trade** (Ability): discard 1 →
  draw 2 each turn; **Riotous Beating**: 20 × your Pokémon; **Trickster-GX** copies an
  attack. Also note the BKT-line **Stand In** isn't on Zoroark-GX — here mobility comes from
  Float Stone + Guzma + Acerola.
- **Wimpod (16/147) ×3 → Golisopod-GX (17/147) ×2** — the hitter. **First Impression**: 120
  for one Grass, *only the turn Golisopod becomes Active*. You reset it into the Active spot
  each turn (Guzma, Acerola, Float Stone) to re-fire.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter (Brigette, Guzma, Acerola,
  Cynthia).
- **Puzzle of Time ×4** — retrieve any 2 cards (DCE, Guzma, Acerola, a KO'd attacker) — the
  recursion that keeps First Impression and Riotous Beating streaming.

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Oranguru (113/149) ×1** — **Resource Management**/Instruct: recycle cards or draw; a
  benched engine that survives N and doesn't rely on Trade.
- **Mew-EX (XY126) ×1** — copies a benched attacker's move (a **Buzzwole-GX counter** — copy
  Riotous Beating/First Impression to 1HKO a Buzzwole that's Psychic-Weak).
- **Acerola ×1 / Max Potion ×1** — the reset/heal package: **Acerola** picks up a damaged
  Golisopod (heal + re-arm First Impression) and denies a prize; Max Potion full-heals.
- **Brigette ×4 / N ×3 / Guzma ×3 / Cynthia ×1 / Sycamore ×1** — setup + draw + gust.
  **Brigette ×4** guarantees the turn-1 board; **Guzma** resets Golisopod and gusts targets.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Evosoda ×3 / Field Blower ×3 / Counter Catcher ×1** — search, Supporter-
  free evolves, Tool/Stadium removal, and **Counter Catcher** (free gust when you're behind
  on prizes).
- **Choice Band ×1 / Float Stone ×2 / Parallel City ×3** — +30 vs. GX/EX, free retreat (reset
  First Impression), and **Parallel City** (×3) to shrink benches / cut Fire & Water damage
  and win the Stadium war.
- **4 DCE + 3 Grass** — DCE powers Riotous Beating; Grass powers First Impression.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** for Zorua ×2–3 + Wimpod; pass with a wide board.
- **Turn 2:** evolve **Zoroark-GX**, **Trade** to dig, and either **Riotous Beating** or move
  a **Golisopod-GX** Active for **First Impression (120)**.

## The core loop
Trade every turn for gas, take 2HKOs with **Riotous Beating**, and drop **First Impression**
whenever you can reset Golisopod Active (Guzma/Acerola/Float Stone). **Acerola** heals +
re-arms Golisopod and denies prizes; **Puzzle of Time** recurs your pieces; **Guzma/Counter
Catcher** pick the KO. **Mew-EX** answers Buzzwole. You out-consistency and out-efficiency
the field.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Brigette turn 1** — wide board = bigger Riotous Beating + more Trade.
- **Reset Golisopod every turn** — First Impression is 120 only on entry; Acerola/Guzma/Float
  Stone are the enablers.
- **Acerola = heal + reload + prize denial** — the deck's grind engine; don't waste it.
- **Mew-EX for Buzzwole** — copy the right attack to 1HKO the Psychic-Weak baby Buzzwole/
  Buzzwole-GX.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting (Buzzwole/Lycanroc, Buzz/Shrine)** — Zoroark-GX is Fighting-Weak; the defining
  bad matchup (Mew-EX + First Impression help, but it's tough).
- **Garbotoxin (opposing Garbodor)** — turns off *your* Trade; Field Blower it or race.
- **Shrine of Punishment** — passive damage on your GX-heavy board.
- **Fire attackers** — Golisopod is Fire-Weak (Parallel City helps).

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Trade: **discard 1 → draw 2** (Ability, once/turn/Zoroark).
- Riotous Beating: **20 × your Pokémon** for a DCE.
- First Impression: **120 for 1 Grass**, only on entering Active (150 w/ Choice Band).
- Puzzle of Time (×2): **retrieve any 2 cards**; reset tools: Guzma/Acerola/Float Stone.
