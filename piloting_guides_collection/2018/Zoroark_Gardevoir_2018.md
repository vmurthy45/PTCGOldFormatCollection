# How to Pilot — Zoroark Gardevoir (2018)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2018 (BKT–FLI). "ZoroGard" — a Tier-1 Zoroark-GX variant
pairing the format's best draw engine with Gardevoir-GX's scaling Fairy attacker._

## One-line identity
Consistency plus a heavy hitter: **Zoroark-GX's Trade** churns through the deck while
**Gardevoir-GX** provides **Infinite Force** (30 × total Energy on both Active Pokémon) and
**Secret Spring** Fairy acceleration. **Gallade** covers the Fighting matchups that wreck
normal Zoroark decks, and **Mew-EX** answers Buzzwole — a flexible, powerful toolbox.

## The meta it lived in
ZoroGard was a Tier-1 pairing that tried to solve Zoroark's biggest problem — its Fighting
Weakness — by teching **Gallade** (a Fighting attacker that hits Buzzwole/Zoroark back) and
**Mew-EX**. Gardevoir-GX's Fairy typing also resists the Dark attacks in the mirror. It
plays the classic Zoroark game (Trade, Riotous Beating) with a bigger late-game ceiling than
the other variants via Infinite Force.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Zorua (52/73) ×4 → Zoroark-GX (53/73) ×4** — the draw/attack core. **Trade** (discard 1 →
  draw 2), **Riotous Beating** (20 × your Pokémon), **Trickster-GX**.
- **Ralts (91/147) ×4 → Gardevoir-GX (93/147) ×2** — the hitter/accelerator. **Secret Spring**
  (Ability): attach a Fairy from hand each turn (on top of your attachment). **Infinite
  Force**: 30 × all Energy on both Active Pokémon — scales into 1HKO range. **Twilight-GX**
  recycles 10 cards. Fairy typing resists Dark (mirror).
- **Gallade (84/162) ×1** (from Kirlia) — a **Fighting** attacker; the tech that lets this
  Zoroark deck punch Buzzwole/Zoroark for Weakness (the matchups that sink other variants).
- **Rare Candy ×3** — jump Ralts → Gardevoir-GX a turn faster (skip Kirlia).
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter (Brigette, Guzma, Mallow).

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Mew-EX (XY126) ×1** — copies a benched attacker (a **Buzzwole counter**: copy Riotous
  Beating / a Fighting move to 1HKO the Psychic-Weak Beasts).
- **Giratina (XY184) ×1** — its Ability blanks effects of some GX/EX attacks / an anti-lock
  disruptive body (tech vs. specific attackers).
- **Puzzle of Time ×4** — retrieve any 2 cards (DCE, Rare Candy, Guzma, a KO'd attacker) — the
  recursion engine.
- **Brigette ×3 / N ×3 / Sycamore ×2 / Mallow ×2 / Guzma ×2** — setup + draw + gust.
  **Mallow** (×2) finds the exact combo piece (Rare Candy + Gardevoir); **Brigette** builds
  the turn-1 board.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Evosoda ×2 / Field Blower ×2 / Enhanced Hammer ×1** — search, Supporter-
  free evolves, Tool/Stadium removal, special-Energy strip.
- **Max Potion ×1 / Choice Band ×1 / Float Stone ×1 / Parallel City ×1** — heal (great with a
  loaded Gardevoir — reset their progress), +30 vs. GX/EX, free retreat, bench control.
- **4 DCE + 4 Fairy** — DCE powers Riotous Beating/Gallade; Fairy feeds Secret Spring +
  Infinite Force.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** for Zorua ×2 + Ralts; wide board, pass.
- **Turn 2:** evolve **Zoroark-GX**, **Trade** to dig for **Rare Candy → Gardevoir-GX**, start
  **Secret Spring** banking Fairy, and take a **Riotous Beating** KO while you build Infinite
  Force.

## The core loop
Trade every turn for gas; take early KOs with **Riotous Beating**; transition to
**Gardevoir-GX** — **Secret Spring** banks Energy so **Infinite Force** climbs into 1HKO
territory, and **Max Potion** resets damage. Bring in **Gallade** vs. Fighting/Zoroark and
**Mew-EX** vs. Buzzwole. **Puzzle of Time** recurs pieces; **Guzma** picks the KO. You out-
consistency the field and out-scale it late.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Brigette turn 1**, then dig for **turn-2/3 Gardevoir** (Rare Candy + Mallow) — Secret
  Spring wants to start early.
- **Gallade is your Fighting answer** — it's why this variant survives the Buzzwole matchups
  that kill other Zoroark decks; set it up when Fighting looms.
- **Bank Energy for Infinite Force**, but spread attachments so an Espeon-GX/Psychic can't
  punish an over-loaded Pokémon.
- **Mew-EX for Buzzwole**; **Max Potion** to erase their damage on a loaded Gardevoir.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting (Buzzwole/Lycanroc)** — still the pressure point, though Gallade/Mew-EX make it
  far more winnable than other Zoroark variants.
- **Fire / Metal (vs. Gardevoir)** — Gardevoir-GX is Metal-Weak; Fire hits it hard.
- **Garbotoxin (opposing Garbodor)** — turns off Trade *and* Secret Spring; Field Blower is
  key.
- **Shrine of Punishment** — chip on your GX-heavy board.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Trade: **discard 1 → draw 2**; Riotous Beating: **20 × your Pokémon**.
- Infinite Force: **30 × total Energy on both Active Pokémon**; Secret Spring: **+1 Fairy/turn**.
- Gallade: Fighting attacker (Buzzwole/Zoroark answer); Mew-EX copies a benched attacker.
- Puzzle of Time (×2): **retrieve any 2 cards**; Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
