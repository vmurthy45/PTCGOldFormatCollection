# How to Pilot — Perfection (Mewtwo & Mew-GX) (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM, the Tag Team / Welder era). "Perfection"
/ "Fire M&M's." Mewtwo & Mew-GX won the 2019 World Championship in Henry Brand's hands and
put **six** players into Worlds Top 8 — each with a different attacker suite._

> **Why this deck is special.** Almost every other deck has one or two attackers.
> Perfection has *a dozen*, because **Mewtwo & Mew-GX's Perfection Ability lets it use the
> attack of ANY Pokémon-GX on your Bench or in your discard pile.** Your whole deck is a
> menu of GX attacks, and you pick the perfect one for the board in front of you — every
> turn, off a single 270-HP body, all as Psychic-type damage. Mastering this deck is
> mastering *when* to reach for each tool. This guide goes card-by-card through the
> toolbox and the niche each one fills.

## One-line identity
A single 270-HP Tag Team that copies the entire format's best GX attacks. **Welder**
accelerates 2 Fire a turn (and draws 3), so even a lightly-charged Mewtwo & Mew-GX can
fire a 270-damage Sky Judgment, a healing Miraculous Duo, a bench-wiping Cross Division,
or a shutdown Tag Purge — whatever the matchup demands.

## The meta it lived in
2019 Standard was the Tag Team GX format: **Reshiram & Charizard-GX** (Fire), **Pikachu &
Zekrom-GX** (Lightning), **Malamar/Giratina** (single-prize Psychic), **Blacephalon**
(single-prize Fire OHKOs), and **Zoroark** variants. Perfection sat above all of them
because it could answer each with a *different* attack from the same card. Its structural
weaknesses were narrow: decks that hit 270 for a 1HKO (Reshiram & Charizard with Ninetales),
**Power Plant** (Stadium that shuts off your GX Abilities — i.e., Dedenne/Jirachi draw),
and being Psychic-Weak in the mirror (which Jirachi-GX fixes).

## The core engine (in every Perfection list)

- **Mewtwo & Mew-GX (71/236) ×4** — the deck. **Perfection** (Ability): its attacks *are*
  the attacks of your Pokémon-GX on the Bench/in the discard. 270 HP means most decks can't
  OHKO it. Why four? It's your only "real" attacker, you burn through them, and prizing one
  hurts — running four keeps your start-with-one odds high (~48%) and lets you discard extras
  to fuel other lines. You typically use 2–3 per game, retreating a damaged one to a fresh one
  to dodge a KO.
- **Welder ×4** — the heart. Attach **2 Fire from hand** to 1 Pokémon **and draw 3**. It's
  energy acceleration *and* your main draw engine in one Supporter — the reason a Fire base
  powers Psychic attacks. You want Welder turn 1 almost every game; the whole Trainer suite
  is built to find it.
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×3** — **Dedechange**: discard your hand, draw 6. Refuels an empty
  hand *without* using your Supporter (so you can Dedechange *then* Welder the same turn — the
  explosive setup turn). Bonus: it discards your evolution-GX attackers so **Perfection can
  copy them**. **Miraculous Duo-GX** (its GX attack) is a fine emergency (damage + heal your
  whole board). Warning: 160 HP and 2 prizes — a bench liability vs. snipe.
- **Jirachi (99/181)** — *not in this exact list, but the archetypal partner* — **Stellar
  Wish**: look at top 5, take a Trainer. (This list leans on Dedenne + heavy Item search
  instead; see Trainers below.)

## The attacker toolbox — and the *exact* niche of each card

Perfection lists sort their GX attackers into four jobs: **heavy hitters, energy
accelerators, bench hitters, and protective/utility attackers.** Here's every attacker in
this build and precisely when you reach for it:

- **Reshiram & Charizard-GX (20/214) ×1 — the flexible heavy hitter.**
  - **Flare Strike (230)**: your bread-and-butter 1HKO on non-Tag-Team Pokémon. Downside
    (can't use it next turn) barely matters — you just copy a *different* attack next turn.
  - **Outrage**: cheap 2-Energy damage that scales with damage on Mewtwo & Mew-GX — punishes
    an opponent who chipped you but didn't KO.
  - **Double Blaze-GX (also copyable!)**: a 6-Energy 1HKO that, boosted, **punches through an
    opposing Tag Purge / damage-prevention** — critical in the mirror and vs. other Tag Teams.
- **Solgaleo-GX (SM104) ×1 — the accelerator/refueler.**
  - **Turbo Strike**: solid damage *and* attach a basic Energy from your deck to a benched
    Pokémon. This is your **"I had to discard a ton of Energy" recovery turn** — the default
    attack when you can't get a clean KO, quietly charging your *next* attacker so you never
    stall. Think "this is a Solgaleo game" whenever you dump Energy early.
- **Magcargo-GX (44/214) ×1 — the uncapped nuke.**
  - **Lava Flow**: scales with Energy — with enough Fire (Welder + Giant Hearth + Fire Crystal)
    it **1HKOs literally anything, including a full-HP Tag Team**. Even un-used, **Burning
    Magma-GX** (its GX attack) threatens damage that forces aggressive decks (Blacephalon, Mad
    Party) to play conservatively. Your ceiling attacker.
- **Latios-GX (78/236) ×1 — the protective attacker & ideal starter.**
  - **Tag Purge (120)**: does damage **and prevents damage from your opponent's Tag Team
    Pokémon next turn** — lets your Mewtwo & Mew-GX *tank* an opposing PikaRom/ReshiZard/mirror
    attacker. The premier anti-Tag-Team tech.
  - **Clear Vision-GX**: shuts off the opponent's GX attacks for the game — stops Tag Bolt-GX
    (PikaRom), Double Blaze-GX (ReshiZard), Cross Division, etc. from *ever going live*.
  - Bonus: **free retreat**, so it's a great card to start with (retreat into your setup).
- **Espeon & Deoxys-GX (72/236) ×1 — the bench hitter / prize-swinger.**
  - **Cross Division-GX**: place **10 damage counters** (20 if you have 3 extra Energy)
    anywhere across the opponent's board. Finish a damaged attacker *and* wipe a bench of
    low-HP Basics (Inkay, Ditto, Pidgey) before they evolve — a multi-prize turn out of
    nowhere. Great vs. evolution decks and Pidgeotto Control.
- **Naganadel-GX (160/236) ×1 — the snipe.**
  - **Venom Shot (170 to ANY Pokémon)**: reaches a benched threat **without needing two Custom
    Catchers** — snipes a Dedenne-GX for 2 prizes, or a damaged attacker they retreated to
    safety. Bodies Malamar (picks off benched pieces). Its inclusion "needs no justification."
- **Jirachi-GX (79/236) ×1 — the mirror/Malamar insurance.**
  - **Psychic Zone** (Ability): **removes Psychic Weakness from all Pokémon in play** — turns
    off the mirror's and PikaRom-teched Mewtwo & Mew-GX's Psychic revenge-KO. Against Malamar,
    keep it benched at all times. In the mirror, drop it the moment you see a Psychic-type
    revenge KO coming (via their Energy Switch/Tag Switch transition). **Tag Bolt-GX-style**
    utility.
- **Cobalion-GX (106/181) ×1 — the clincher & anti-Raichu.**
  - **Iron Rule-GX**: gives you an **uninterrupted extra turn to attach one more Energy** —
    the "one more turn to assemble the KO with no room for error" button in tense endgames
    (a better Altaria-GX). **Metal Symbol** (Ability, with Metal Energy) shines vs. **Raichu &
    Alolan Raichu-GX's Tandem Shock paralysis**.
- **Marshadow (81/214) ×1 — the Stadium answer.**
  - **Resetting Hole** (Ability): discard the Stadium in play. **Your searchable out to Power
    Plant**, which is the single most dangerous counter to this deck (it shuts off Dedenne/
    Jirachi-GX Abilities). Also clears opposing Giant Hearth/Thunder Mountain, etc.

## The Trainer engine (why the odd counts)

- **Pokégear 3.0 ×4** — dig for a Supporter (almost always **Welder**). More outs to the
  turn-1 Welder = a more consistent deck.
- **Acro Bike ×4** — draw 2, keep 1: another way to find Welder and to *dig/discard*, seeding
  attackers and Energy where you want them.
- **Cherish Ball ×4** — search any Pokémon-GX (Mewtwo & Mew-GX, Dedenne-GX, any attacker);
  the primary way to *find* a toolbox piece.
- **Mysterious Treasure ×3** — discard a card → search a Psychic **or** Dragon Pokémon. Doubles
  as a way to **put an evolution-GX attacker into the discard** so Perfection can copy it.
- **Electromagnetic Radar ×1** — discard 2 → search 2 Pokémon-GX (great for Dedenne + a
  discard attacker at once).
- **Custom Catcher ×4** — gust. Needs **two at once** to gust a benched Pokémon, which is why
  the count is high; huge for reaching KOs the opponent tried to protect. (Naganadel-GX's
  Venom Shot covers this when you only have one.)
- **Bill's Analysis ×2** — draw depth (look at 6, take 2) — conserves resources and finds the
  specific piece a random draw would miss.
- **Switch ×2** — pivot out of a bad active or a status/gust trap; also gets a fresh Mewtwo &
  Mew-GX active.
- **Fire Crystal ×1** — return **3 Fire from discard to hand** — combos with Welder and lets
  you be "careless" with Energy earlier (you'll get it back for the big Lava Flow / Sky
  Judgment turn).
- **Giant Hearth ×3** — Stadium: search 2 Fire to hand. "Welder's best friend" — both a
  discard outlet for Perfection *and* Energy fuel; keeps the Fire flowing for Lava Flow.
- **Viridian Forest ×1** — thin/fix Energy (discard a card, get a basic Energy) and a Stadium
  to overwrite an opponent's.
- **8 Fire + 3 Psychic** — Fire powers Welder and every Fire attack; **Psychic** enables the
  Psychic-cost attackers (Naganadel-GX's Venom Shot, Espeon & Deoxys-GX, and Mewtwo & Mew-GX's
  own Miraculous Duo-GX) — don't cut it to zero, those attacks matter.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1 dream:** get **Welder** (via Pokégear/Acro Bike). If your hand's bad,
  **Dedenne-GX Dedechange** for a fresh 6 *then* Welder — attach 2 Fire to Mewtwo & Mew-GX,
  draw 3, and you're online. Meanwhile **Cherish Ball / Mysterious Treasure** put the right
  GX attacker in play or the discard so Perfection can copy it.
- **Starter note:** opening **Latios-GX** is fine (free retreat), and it discards nothing you
  need — retreat into your setup and it's a live Tag Purge later.

## The core loop
Each turn: **read the board → pick the attack.** Need a clean 2-prize KO on a non-Tag-Team?
Flare Strike. On a Tag Team? Load up for Sky Judgment/Double Blaze or Lava Flow. Getting
raced by another Tag Team? Tag Purge (tank it) or Clear Vision-GX (shut their GX off). Facing
a wide low-HP bench or Malamar? Cross Division / Venom Shot for multi-prize or snipes. Dumped
Energy? Turbo Strike to refuel. Power Plant down? Marshadow Resetting Hole. Then **Welder +
attach** to fund next turn, and rotate to a fresh Mewtwo & Mew-GX if the active is nearly
dead.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Welder first, always.** Your consistency, acceleration, and draw all run through it.
  Dedechange → Welder is the explosive turn.
- **Seed the discard.** Perfection copies attackers in the *discard* too — Mysterious
  Treasure/Acro Bike/Giant Hearth aren't just draw, they're how you "install" Flare Strike,
  Turbo Strike, Lava Flow into your menu.
- **Match the tool to the matchup** (the whole skill of the deck):
  - **vs. ReshiZard / big Tag Teams:** Tag Purge to tank, Clear Vision to shut off Double
    Blaze, Double Blaze yourself to punch through *their* Tag Purge.
  - **vs. PikaRom:** Clear Vision-GX (deny Tag Bolt-GX), Jirachi-GX vs. their Psychic tech,
    Cobalion vs. Raichu paralysis.
  - **vs. Malamar/Giratina:** Jirachi-GX benched (kill their Psychic revenge), Venom Shot /
    Cross Division to snipe their fragile pieces, Great-value 1HKOs to win the prize race.
  - **vs. Blacephalon:** race with clean KOs; Magcargo's Burning Magma-GX threat makes them
    play conservatively.
  - **vs. Power Plant decks:** Marshadow Resetting Hole is your out — find it.
- **Protect your bench.** Dedenne-GX (2 prizes, 160 HP) is a snipe magnet — don't over-bench
  GX support you can't afford to lose.
- **Count to 270.** That's the 1HKO number on a Tag Team; know whether this turn's attack +
  Energy gets you there or whether you Turbo Strike/Tag Purge and wait.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Decks that hit 270 (Reshiram & Charizard-GX + Ninetales/Victini)** — Nine Temptations
  drags your damaged Mewtwo & Mew-GX up (negating the "hide on bench" plan) and 1HKOs you;
  the most dangerous matchup.
- **Power Plant** — shuts off Dedenne/Jirachi-GX Abilities (your draw/insurance); you *must*
  find Marshadow.
- **Psychic Weakness in the mirror / vs. Psychic-tech PikaRom** — answered by Jirachi-GX, but
  a mis-sequenced game loses to a revenge KO.
- **Reset Stamp late** — shrinks your hand when you most need Welder; run enough Welder/Pokégear
  and lean on Dedechange to dig back.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Perfection: use the attack of **any of your Pokémon-GX on Bench or in discard**.
- Welder: **attach 2 Fire from hand + draw 3** (Supporter).
- 1HKO benchmarks: **230** Flare Strike (non-Tag-Team), **270** Sky Judgment / big Lava Flow
  (anything).
- Key techs: Tag Purge (tank Tag Teams), Clear Vision-GX (deny their GX attack), Cross
  Division-GX (spread 10–20 counters), Venom Shot (170 anywhere), Resetting Hole (kill Power
  Plant), Psychic Zone (remove Psychic Weakness).
