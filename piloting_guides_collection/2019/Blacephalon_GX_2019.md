# How to Pilot — Blacephalon-GX (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM). Blacephalon-GX + Naganadel — the Fire
"Mind Blown" deck that OHKOs anything by sending Fire Energy to the Lost Zone._

## One-line identity
A Fire OHKO deck built on **Blacephalon-GX's Mind Blown**: send any number of your Fire
Energy **to the Lost Zone** to deal **50 damage each**, scaling to a one-hit KO on
literally anything in the format. **Naganadel's Charging Up** streams Energy back onto the
board so you can keep Mind Blowing, and **Beast Ring** refuels explosively once the
opponent is on the back foot. Louis and I (Vig) played this exact 60 at the 2019 World
Championships in Washington, DC — Louis piloted it to Day 2.

## The meta it lived in
2019 Standard was the Tag Team GX / Welder era — **Reshiram & Charizard-GX**, **Pikachu &
Zekrom-GX**, **Malamar/Giratina**, **Mewtwo & Mew-GX**, and Zoroark variants. Blacephalon-GX
punched into all of it because Mind Blown's damage is effectively **uncapped** — it OHKOs a
270-HP Tag Team as easily as a 120-HP Basic, and Naganadel's Energy acceleration + Beast
Ring meant it could reload fast. The catch that defines how you pilot it: Mind Blown's
Energy goes to the **Lost Zone**, not the discard.

## The Lost Zone rule (the single most important thing)
Mind Blown sends Fire Energy to the **Lost Zone** — a zone you can **never** recover from.
This is completely different from a discard (which Fire Crystal, Energy Retrieval, etc. can
recycle). Consequences for how you play:

- The deck runs a **very high basic Fire Energy count** precisely because every Mind Blown
  permanently removes Energy from the game. You are burning a finite resource.
- **You must plan your whole game around having enough Fire to take all six prizes before
  you fizzle out.** Count it: roughly how many Mind Blowns (and how big) will it take to win,
  and don't Lost-Zone more Energy than a KO actually requires. Over-committing Energy early
  is how this deck loses — it runs dry at 2 prizes and can't close.
- Because you can't get Lost-Zoned Energy back, **Naganadel's Charging Up** (which moves
  Energy from the **discard**, not the Lost Zone) and your Energy-search Items are how you
  keep Energy *flowing onto the board*, but they can't undo the Lost Zone — they only help
  you deploy what you still have.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Blacephalon-GX (52/214) ×4** — the attacker. **Mind Blown**: send any number of your
  Fire Energy to the Lost Zone → 50 damage each (OHKOs anything with enough Energy).
  **Blazer** is a cheap early attack, and **Burst-GX** (the GX attack) can take a quick prize
  / close a game. 180 HP.
- **Poipole (107/214) ×3 → Naganadel (108/214) ×3** — the Energy engine. **Charging Up**
  (Naganadel's Ability): once per turn, attach a Fire Energy from your discard to a benched
  Pokémon — this is how you keep putting Energy onto Blacephalon-GX to fuel the next Mind
  Blown without spending your manual attachment. The Poipole/Naganadel line is the deck's
  backbone.
- **Welder ×4** — attach **2 Fire from hand + draw 3** — the burst that lets you load
  Blacephalon-GX and Mind Blown the same turn, plus your main draw.
- **Beast Ring ×4** — when the opponent has **4 or fewer prizes**, attach **2 Fire from your
  deck** to your Ultra Beasts — an explosive refuel that reloads Blacephalon-GX for a huge
  Mind Blown right when you're closing (helps you hit the "take the last prizes" plan
  without running dry).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×1** — **Dedechange**: discard hand → draw 6; the consistency draw
  (a small prize liability you accept).
- **Ditto Prism Star (154/214) ×1** — flexibly completes a Naganadel line when a piece is
  prized/missing.
- **Meowth (125/181) ×1 → Persian-GX (149/214) ×1** — a Colorless attacker option;
  Persian-GX's **Cat Crash / Catwalk-GX** utility and a body that doesn't cost Fire.
- **Pokémon Communication ×4 / Mysterious Treasure ×4** — search Pokémon (Blacephalon-GX,
  the Naganadel line, techs); Treasure also digs.
- **Custom Catcher ×4** — gust (two at once) — pull up the exact Pokémon you want to Mind
  Blown for a KO, or snipe a support Pokémon.
- **Cynthia ×4 / Lillie ×2** — draw. **Lillie** front-loads early hands; Cynthia refuels
  mid-game.
- **Ultra Space ×1** — search an Ultra Beast (Blacephalon-GX/Naganadel) — speeds setup.
- **Giant Hearth ×2 / Heat Factory Prism Star ×1** — Fire search Stadiums — get basic Fire
  into hand/onto the board to feed Mind Blown.
- **15 Fire + 1 Beast Energy Prism Star** — the high Fire count is mandatory given the Lost
  Zone drain; **Beast Energy** boosts Ultra Beasts (+30) and gives extra when Active. (Note:
  Beast Energy is Special — it can't be moved by Charging Up like a basic Fire can.)

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** set up Poipole → Naganadel and get Fire flowing. A strong turn: attach Fire +
  **Welder** (2 more Fire, draw 3), and either develop or take an early **Mind Blown** if the
  KO is there. Keep Blacephalon-GX safe on the bench until you can make the Energy count.
- **Don't over-commit Fire early** — remember every Mind Blown Energy is gone forever; take
  the KO you need, not the biggest one you can.

## The core loop
Stream Energy with **Naganadel's Charging Up** + Welder, then **Mind Blown** for exactly
enough to KO the target (send only as much Fire to the Lost Zone as the KO requires).
**Custom Catcher** picks off the Pokémon you want; **Beast Ring** reloads once they're at 4
prizes. Throughout, **track your remaining Fire against the prizes you still need** — the
game plan is to have enough Energy left to take all six prizes, not to throw the biggest
possible hit early.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Count your Fire to six prizes** — the defining discipline. Know how many more KOs you
  need and whether you have the Energy (in play + deck + hand, *not* Lost Zone) to get there.
  Don't fizzle.
- **Mind Blown only as big as the KO needs** — every extra Energy Lost-Zoned is one you can't
  use later.
- **Naganadel keeps Energy on the board** — use Charging Up every turn to redeploy discard
  Fire; it's your renewable supply line (the Lost Zone is not).
- **Beast Ring is your closer** — sequence prizes so it's live (opponent ≤4) for the reload
  that finishes the game.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Water attackers** — Blacephalon-GX is Water-Weak (a Water deck 1HKOs it easily).
- **Running out of Fire** — the deck's built-in clock; over-committing Energy to the Lost
  Zone early loses games you were winning. Resource discipline is everything.
- **Sleep / disruption (Darkrai's Abyssal Sleep)** — a sleep-lock or switch-denial stalls
  your Mind Blown cadence; run/hold Switch.
- **Malamar/Giratina spread (Distortion Door + Spell Tag)** — chip that pressures your
  Blacephalon-GX and complicates the race.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Mind Blown: **50 × Fire Energy sent to the Lost Zone** (uncapped — OHKOs anything).
- Blacephalon-GX: **180 HP**; **Burst-GX** for a quick prize.
- Welder: **2 Fire from hand + draw 3**; Beast Ring: **2 Fire from deck** at opponent ≤4
  prizes.
- Naganadel Charging Up: **1 Fire from discard → benched Pokémon** per turn.
- **Lost Zone ≠ discard** — Mind Blown Energy is gone for good; plan your Fire to cover all 6
  prizes.
