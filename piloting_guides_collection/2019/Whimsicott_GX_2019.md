# How to Pilot — Whimsicott GX (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM). A Fairy disruption/toolbox that tries to
win games while taking little or no damage — you wall, tax, and grind rather than race._

## One-line identity
A **Fairy control-beatdown**: **Porygon-Z's Crazy Code** pours Special Energy onto
**Whimsicott-GX** for free, whose **Energy Blow** scales at 30 damage per Energy, while a
wall package — **Fluffy Cotton** (coin-flip to prevent all damage), **Fairy Charm L**, and
**Wondrous Labyrinth** — makes the opponent's attacks whiff, cost more, or do nothing at all.
You out-last, then close with a big Energy Blow.

## The meta it lived in
2019 Standard was defined by hard-hitting 3-prize Tag Teams — above all **Pikachu &
Zekrom-GX (PikaRom)** and the various Reshiram & Charizard / Mewtwo & Mew builds. Whimsicott-GX
attacks that field sideways: a single **Fairy Charm L** blanks every Lightning-GX attack
PikaRom has, **Fluffy Cotton** coin-flips away half of everything else, and **Wondrous
Labyrinth** taxes every non-Fairy attacker in the format one extra Energy while leaving your
own Fairy attacker untouched. It's a knowledge- and patience-heavy deck that punishes
one-dimensional aggro. Its problem: it's inconsistent and coin-flip-reliant, and decks that
ignore the wall (Metal Weakness, spread, ability lock) run it over.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Cottonee (139/214) ×4 → Whimsicott-GX (140/214) ×3** — the attacker and the wall in one.
  **Fluffy Cotton** (Ability): whenever an attack would damage it, flip a coin; on heads,
  prevent *all* of that damage. **Energy Blow** [Y]: 10 + **30 × Energy attached** — a
  scaling single-Energy-cost attack that hits 100 off a Triple Acceleration Energy and climbs
  fast from there. **Toy Box-GX** [Y] searches your deck for up to 5 cards — your one-time
  "find everything" button on a slow start. It's a Stage 1, so no Rare Candy needed.
- **Porygon (155/214) ×3 → Porygon-Z (157/214) ×4** — the acceleration engine. **Crazy Code**
  (Ability): attach **as many Special Energy from your hand as you like** each turn, to any of
  your Pokémon. This is what powers Energy Blow to KO numbers in a single turn. Stage 2, so
  you lean on **Rare Candy** to jump Porygon straight to Porygon-Z.
- **Blitzle (81/214) ×1 → Zebstrika (82/214) ×1** — the draw engine. **Sprint** (Ability):
  once per turn, discard your hand and draw 4 — digs you to Porygon-Z, energy, and your
  disruption pieces (it does **not** knock itself out). A 1-1 line you fetch and evolve as
  needed.
- **The Special Energy suite** — all attach for free off Crazy Code and all feed Energy Blow:
  - **Triple Acceleration Energy (190/214) ×4** — provides **CCC** (3 Energy!) but only while
    on an **Evolution** Pokémon (Whimsicott-GX qualifies); discarded at end of turn. One
    attachment = 100 Energy Blow.
  - **Unit Energy FDF (118/131) ×4** — provides Fairy/Dark/Fighting; the reliable **Fairy**
    for Energy Blow's cost.
  - **Rainbow Energy (151/168) ×1** / **Recycle Energy (212/236) ×4** — flexible Special
    Energy; Recycle returns to hand instead of being discarded, so Crazy Code never runs dry.
  - **Fairy Energy ×1** — the lone basic, a non-Special backup for the [Y] cost.

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Whimsicott (144/236) ×1** — **Prowl** (Ability): when you play it from hand to evolve a
  Cottonee, search your deck for **any card**. A free tutor bolted onto a body — grab the
  exact disruption piece or Energy you need.
- **Mew (76/214) ×1** — **Bench Barrier**: prevents all damage to your Benched Pokémon,
  shutting off spread/snipe against your fragile Porygon-Z and Cottonee lines.
- **Ditto Prism Star (154/214) ×1** — flexibly completes whichever evolution line (Whimsicott
  or Porygon-Z) is missing a piece.
- **Fairy Charm L (172/214) ×1** — Pokémon Tool: prevents **all** damage to the Fairy Pokémon
  it's attached to from the opponent's **Lightning-GX/EX** — a one-card hard wall vs PikaRom.
- **Wondrous Labyrinth ◇ (158/181) ×1** — Prism Star Stadium: the attacks of **non-Fairy**
  Pokémon (both players') cost **[C] more**. Your Whimsicott-GX is Fairy and pays nothing
  extra; every non-Fairy attacker in the format gets taxed. Also can't be affected by Item or
  Supporter effects.
- **Counter Catcher (91/111) ×4** — free gust while you're behind on prizes. Because you often
  give up **zero** early prizes, you're behind almost all game, so Counter Catcher is basically
  a free Item Boss's Orders — drag up something to KO or to strand.
- **Reset Stamp (206/236) ×1** — shuffle the opponent's hand down to a small number when you're
  behind; devastating on a grindy turn where they're rebuilding.
- **Professor Elm's Lecture ×4 / Cynthia ×4 / Tate & Liza ×2** — draw + pivot. Elm's Lecture
  loads Basics (Cottonee/Porygon/Blitzle) early; Cynthia refreshes; **Tate & Liza** draws *and*
  switches, un-sticking a tired attacker without paying retreat.
- **Rare Candy ×4 / Pokémon Communication ×4 / Cherish Ball ×2** — the search/consistency shell
  that assembles Porygon-Z and finds your Pokémon.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** bench **Cottonee**, **Porygon**, and ideally a **Blitzle**; **Professor Elm's
  Lecture** to load more Basics. Get **Fluffy Cotton** online by evolving to Whimsicott-GX ASAP
  so you're already flipping away damage.
- **Turn 2:** **Rare Candy** a Porygon into **Porygon-Z**; now **Crazy Code** can dump Special
  Energy. Drop **Wondrous Labyrinth** to start taxing them, and attach **Fairy Charm L** if
  you're staring down PikaRom. **Toy Box-GX** is your bail-out if the pieces won't come.

## The core loop
Wall behind **Whimsicott-GX** (coin-flip prevention + Charm + Labyrinth) → **Crazy Code** a
pile of Special Energy onto it → **Energy Blow** for a scaling hit → **Counter Catcher** to
pick off whatever wins you the most tempo → **Recycle Energy** keeps the tank full and
**Reset Stamp** strips their rebuild. You're not trying to go fast; you're trying to make
their turns do nothing while yours slowly convert.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Establish the wall before you attack.** A turn where they can't meaningfully damage you is
  worth more than a marginal Energy Blow — Charm L / Labyrinth / Fluffy Cotton first.
- **Know your Energy Blow math.** Triple Acceleration = 3 Energy = 100. Stack a second Special
  Energy (or another Triple Accel after it's discarded) to climb to 130/160+ for the KO you
  need — count it out before committing.
- **Counter Catcher is almost always live** because you're behind on prizes — use it as your
  gust, not as a panic button.
- **Respect the coin flip.** Fluffy Cotton is 50/50, not a guarantee; don't leave Whimsicott-GX
  low on HP assuming it'll hold, and keep a backup Cottonee benched (behind Mew's Bench
  Barrier).

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Metal decks** — Whimsicott-GX is **Metal Weakness ×2**; anything Metal (e.g. Zacian-era /
  Metal Tag Teams) blows through the wall for double.
- **Ability lock (Power Plant / Garbotoxin-style)** — turning off Crazy Code kills your
  acceleration and turning off Fluffy Cotton kills your wall; you have little game under lock.
- **Fast, wide aggro that ignores the wall** — if they can out-tempo the coin flips and take
  prizes before you stabilize, the slow engine loses the race.
- **Consistency** — the deck bricks more than the format's linear aggro; a bad Sprint chain or
  a whiffed Rare Candy can cost the game outright.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Energy Blow: **10 + 30 × Energy attached** (Triple Accel = 3 → **100**; +1 more → **130**).
- Fluffy Cotton: **coin flip to prevent all damage** from each attack.
- Crazy Code: attach **any number of Special Energy** from hand per turn.
- Wondrous Labyrinth: **non-Fairy attacks cost [C] more** (your Fairy attacker is exempt).
- Fairy Charm L: **prevents all damage from opposing Lightning-GX/EX**.
- Setup: **turn-2 Porygon-Z** (via Rare Candy); Whimsicott-GX online turn 1–2.
