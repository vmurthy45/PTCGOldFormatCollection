# How to Pilot — ReshiZard Firebox (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM). The Jirachi/Dedenne-GX ("Firebox")
aggressive variant of Reshiram & Charizard-GX — the most popular deck of the format._

## One-line identity
Fast, consistent Fire beatdown: **Welder** attaches 2 Fire a turn (and draws 3) to power
**Reshiram & Charizard-GX's Flare Strike (230)** from turn 2, while an Ability engine
(**Jirachi + Dedenne-GX**) digs to Welder every game. **Ninetales** gusts benched threats
and **Heatran-GX** 1HKOs opposing Tag Teams. Hit hard, hit early, take prizes.

## The meta it lived in
Reshiram & Charizard-GX was *the* deck of 2019 — a 270-HP Tag Team with a huge, cheap 230
attack. The "Firebox" (Jirachi/Dedenne) build trades the defensive Green's Exploration
package for raw speed and an Ability-based draw engine, making it more consistent at getting
the turn-1/2 Welder. **Kaya Lichtleitner won the Senior Division World Championship** with a
ReshiZard build; the deck was everywhere.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Reshiram & Charizard-GX (20/214) ×2** — the core. **Flare Strike**: 230 (can't use next
  turn — pivot or use another attacker). **Outrage**: cheap scaling damage. **Double
  Blaze-GX**: a 1HKO GX attack that, boosted, punches through damage-prevention. 270 HP.
- **Welder ×4** — attach **2 Fire from hand + draw 3**. Acceleration and draw in one — the
  engine of every Fire deck. Turn-1 Welder is the goal.
- **Jirachi (99/181) ×4** — **Stellar Wish**: look at top 5, take a Trainer (usually Welder or
  a Ball). Consistency spine; sleeps active, so pivot with Escape Board/Switch.
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×3** — **Dedechange**: discard hand, draw 6 — refuels without your
  Supporter, so Dedechange → Welder is the explosive turn.
- **Vulpix (15/181) ×2 → Ninetales (16/181) ×2** — **Nine Temptations** (Ability): gust a
  benched opposing Pokémon Active — a repeatable, Item-free Catcher that also negates the
  opponent hiding damaged Pokémon (crucial vs. Mewtwo & Mew-GX). One of the best support
  Pokémon in the format.

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Heatran-GX (25/236) ×1** — **Hot Burn-GX**: a GX attack that **1HKOs any Tag Team** and
  only gives up 2 prizes when KO'd. Your answer to the mirror and other Tag Teams.
- **Turtonator (50/70) ×1** — **Explosive Jet**: scales with Energy discarded; a non-GX
  attacker (single prize) that hits hard using excess Energy (e.g., after Double Blaze-GX).
- **Victini Prism Star (7/70) ×1** — **Victory Ball**: shuffle all Fire from discard into deck
  + damage per Energy — recover your resource *and* a 2-Energy attacker that can 1HKO a Tag
  Team. Great vs. single-prize decks (recycle for the long game).
- **Cherish Ball ×4 / Pokémon Communication ×3** — search Pokémon-GX / trade for any Pokémon.
- **Acro Bike ×4** — dig for Welder / discard-and-draw.
- **Switch ×3 / Escape Board ×2** — pivot the sleeping Jirachi and reset Flare Strike's "can't
  attack next turn" clause.
- **Fiery Flint ×1 / Pal Pad ×1** — dig 3 Fire to hand / recover 2 Supporters (get Welder
  back).
- **Giant Hearth ×3 / Heat Factory Prism Star ×1** — Fire acceleration Stadiums (search/attach
  Fire), the fuel for Welder turns.
- **18 Fire** — a high count so Welder and every attack are always live.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** find **Welder** (Jirachi Stellar Wish / Dedechange / Acro Bike). Bench Jirachi +
  a Vulpix.
- **Turn 2:** **Welder** 2 Fire onto Reshiram & Charizard-GX (+ your attach) → **Flare Strike
  for 230** — a 2-prize KO on most things.

## The core loop
Welder every turn to keep a ReshiZard charged; **Flare Strike (230)** for KOs, pivoting to a
second ReshiZard (or Turtonator/Victini) to dodge the "can't attack next turn" clause.
**Ninetales** gusts the benched Pokémon you actually need to KO; **Heatran-GX** answers Tag
Teams. Play around **Reset Stamp** by taking KOs in a way that keeps your options open.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Turn-2 Flare Strike** is the tempo baseline — get Welder online turn 1.
- **Ninetales targets the right Pokémon** — don't just attack the Active; gust the threat/
  support that matters (and deny the mirror's bench-hiding).
- **Reset the Flare Strike lock** with a pivot (Switch/second attacker) so you never skip a
  turn of damage.
- **Heatran-GX / Victini in the tank** for Tag Teams and single-prize grinds respectively —
  reach for them at the right moment.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Water attackers** — Reshiram & Charizard-GX is Water-Weak (a Water deck 1HKOs it).
- **Single-prize decks (Malamar, Blacephalon)** — trade prizes efficiently against your 3-prize
  Tag Team; you must take clean KOs and use Victini/Turtonator to keep pace.
- **Reset Stamp + gust** — disruption that strands you off Welder.
- **Mind Blown / Fireball Circus mirror-race** — Blacephalon can out-race if you stumble.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Flare Strike: **230** (can't attack next turn); Double Blaze-GX: 1HKO GX attack.
- Welder: **2 Fire from hand + draw 3**; Nine Temptations: **gust a benched Pokémon**.
- Heatran-GX (Hot Burn-GX): **1HKOs a Tag Team** (gives up 2 prizes).
- Setup: **turn-2 Flare Strike**; **18 Fire** base.
