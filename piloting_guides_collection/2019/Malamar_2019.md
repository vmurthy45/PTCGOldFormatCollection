# How to Pilot — Malamar (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM). The premier single-prize deck of the
format — the "gatekeeper" that kept other non-GX decks in check._

## One-line identity
A resilient Psychic engine: **Malamar's Psychic Recharge** accelerates Psychic Energy from
the discard to your bench every turn, powering an **infinitely-recurring Giratina** (which
comes back from the discard each turn) plus a toolbox of Psychic attackers. You give up one
prize at a time, recur your attacker forever, and win the prize race.

## The meta it lived in
2019 Standard was full of 3-prize Tag Teams, and Malamar punished them: a non-GX deck with
an attacker (**Giratina**) that literally can't be permanently removed is incredibly stable
in a format with almost no Pokémon recovery. It was the deck to beat among single-prize
strategies and a strong, proactive choice into an unknown field. Its kryptonite: healing
Supporters (Mallow & Lana) that negate a 2HKO turn, and fast decks that win the race before
the engine stabilizes.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Inkay (50/131) ×4 → Malamar (51/131) ×4** — the engine. **Psychic Recharge** (Ability):
  once per turn per Malamar, attach a Psychic from the discard to a benched Psychic Pokémon.
  Two–three Malamar = a flood of Energy every turn. The whole deck exists to get Malamar
  online.
- **Giratina (97/214) ×2** — the main attacker. **Shadow Impact**: 130 (put 2 damage counters
  on one of your own) — and crucially it can **return itself from the discard to your hand**
  via its own Ability loop, so you re-play and re-attack it every turn. A single-prize 130
  that never runs out. **Distortion Door** (Ability) spreads 2 counters to set up KOs.
- **Jirachi (99/181) ×4** — **Stellar Wish**: look at top 5, take a Trainer. The consistency
  engine that finds your setup/disruption pieces (it falls asleep active — pivot with Switch/
  Escape Board).
- **Mysterious Treasure ×4 / Pokémon Communication ×4** — search Psychic Pokémon (Inkay,
  Giratina, techs); Communication trades a Pokémon in hand for any in deck.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Mimikyu (112/181) ×1** — **Copycat** / its Ability punishes Tag Teams; a tech attacker
  that walls or answers specific threats.
- **Espurr (44/131) ×1** — a support Basic (search/utility) that feeds the Psychic engine.
- **Mew (76/214) ×1** — **Psypower**: place a damage counter (combos with Distortion Door/Spell
  Tag to reach exact KO numbers on 70-HP targets like Jirachi/Marshadow); also **Bench
  Barrier**-style protection in some builds.
- **Latios-GX (78/236) ×1** — **Tag Purge** anti-Tag-Team attacker / a bigger hit when you need
  to go over a Giratina's ceiling.
- **Spell Tag ×4** — Tool: when the holder is KO'd (or via its effect) place damage — turns
  Giratina's 130 into "170" over a turn cycle and sets up spread KOs.
- **Cynthia ×3 / Lillie ×4** — draw. **Lillie** (draw to 6/8 early) is huge on setup turns;
  Cynthia refuels mid-game.
- **Acro Bike ×3 / Switch ×3 / Escape Board ×2** — dig/discard (seed Psychic Energy for
  Recharge), and pivot the sleeping Jirachi / a stuck attacker out.
- **Reset Stamp ×2** — shrink the opponent's hand to 4 (or fewer as prizes dwindle) — a
  comeback/disruption button when you're behind.
- **Power Plant ×2 / Viridian Forest ×2** — **Power Plant** shuts off opposing Abilities
  (Dedenne/Jirachi-GX draw, Zoroark's Trade) — a huge Stadium vs. the field; Viridian Forest
  fixes/discards Energy for Recharge.
- **9 Psychic** — the only Energy; Recharge recycles it endlessly.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** bench Inkay(s) + Jirachi; **Stellar Wish** for setup pieces, **Lillie** to
  load your hand, and get Psychic into the discard (Acro Bike/Viridian Forest) so Recharge is
  live. Evolve **Malamar** turn 2.
- **Turn 2–3:** with 2 Malamar online, Recharge floods Energy → promote **Giratina** →
  **Shadow Impact** for 130, then re-loop it next turn.

## The core loop
Recharge Energy onto the bench every turn, attack with **Giratina** (130, single prize), and
**recur it from the discard** so it never stops. **Distortion Door + Spell Tag + Mew** place
extra counters to reach KOs on bigger Pokémon over a turn cycle. **Power Plant** turns off
their draw engines; **Reset Stamp** disrupts. You trade 1 prize for their 3 (Tag Teams) and
grind the race.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **2–3 Malamar is the setup goal** — one isn't enough acceleration; protect them (they're the
  engine, not the attacker).
- **Giratina is un-killable** — lean on the loop; you rarely "run out" of attacker, which wins
  attrition.
- **Stack spread damage** (Distortion Door/Spell Tag/Mew) a turn ahead so Shadow Impact's 130
  becomes lethal on 200+ HP targets.
- **Power Plant is a weapon** — shutting off Dedenne/Jirachi-GX/Trade cripples the GX decks;
  keep it down.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Healing (Mallow & Lana, Great Potion)** — negates a 2HKO turn and stalls your race; a real
  problem for a 130-damage attacker.
- **Fast aggro (Blacephalon, PikaRom)** — can win the prize race or 1HKO before the engine
  stabilizes.
- **Reset Stamp / hand disruption against you** — your setup is hand-reliant early.
- **Chip-immune / high-HP walls** — anything that out-heals or out-sizes 130-per-turn.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Psychic Recharge: **1 Psychic from discard → benched Psychic Pokémon**, per Malamar, per
  turn.
- Shadow Impact (Giratina): **130** (+2 counters on your own), single prize, self-recurring.
- Distortion Door / Spell Tag / Mew: place counters to reach exact KO numbers.
- Setup: **turn-2 Malamar**, **2–3 Malamar** for full acceleration.
