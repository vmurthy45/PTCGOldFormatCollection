# How to Pilot — Green's ReshiZard (2019)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard 2019 (TEU–UNM). The **Green's Exploration** control/tank
variant of Reshiram & Charizard-GX — a slower, grindier take on the format's best Fire deck._

## One-line identity
A tanky, Trainer-driven ReshiZard: **Green's Exploration** searches your deck for **2
Trainer cards** — playable only while you have **no Pokémon with Abilities in play** — and
because **not a single Pokémon in this list has an Ability**, Green's is **always live**.
That turns the deck into a Trainer-toolbox engine (healing, gust, disruption) that makes a
270-HP Tag Team nearly impossible to KO. **Welder** still powers **Flare Strike**; the plan
is to out-grind, not out-race.

## The meta it lived in
2019 had two ReshiZard schools: the aggressive **Firebox** (Jirachi/Dedenne) and this one —
the **Green's Exploration** control build. Instead of an Ability draw engine, it chains
Green's Exploration + Pokégear to assemble Trainers, leaning on **Mixed Herbs / Great
Potion** healing to make the 270-HP TAG TEAM nearly impossible to KO. It trades speed for
resilience and a grindy, disruptive game.

## Why Green's Exploration is always on (the deck's whole trick)
Green's Exploration reads: *"You can play this card only if you have no Pokémon with
Abilities in play. Search your deck for up to 2 Trainer cards, reveal them, and put them
into your hand. Then, shuffle your deck."* The build is designed so that clause is **never a
problem**:

- **No Pokémon in the list has an Ability.** Reshiram & Charizard-GX has none, and
  **Volcanion's Flare Starter is an *attack*, not an Ability** — so a benched Volcanion does
  *not* turn Green's off. There's simply nothing on your board that can ever disable Green's.
- That means every turn you can reliably play Green's Exploration to grab exactly the two
  Trainers the matchup calls for. Consistency is the deck's identity — you tutor your way to
  the perfect answer each turn.
- It also means you avoid the Firebox draw engines (Jirachi/Dedenne-GX) on purpose: adding an
  Ability Pokémon would break Green's. Your draw/consistency comes from Green's + Pokégear +
  Welder instead.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Reshiram & Charizard-GX (20/214) ×3** — the core/wall. **Flare Strike (230)**,
  **Outrage**, **Double Blaze-GX**. 270 HP is the whole point — with healing, opponents
  struggle to ever KO it. No Ability (never blocks Green's).
- **Volcanion (25/214) ×4** — **Flare Starter** (an *attack*): does damage and accelerates
  Fire — a Fire attacker/accelerator that doesn't touch your Ability count, so it plays
  freely alongside Green's. (No Ability = no conflict with Green's Exploration.)
- **Green's Exploration ×4** — the engine: search **2 Trainer cards** (always live here). With
  the deck's huge Trainer count it assembles exactly the piece you need (heal, gust, disrupt,
  Stadium) — and it grabs **any** Trainers (Items *and* Supporters, e.g. fetching Welder), not
  just Items.
- **Welder ×4** — attach 2 Fire + draw 3; still your acceleration/draw. (You often want
  Green's *or* Welder depending on the turn — Pokégear finds either.)

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Pokégear 3.0 ×4** — dig for Green's Exploration or Welder — the Supporter you need each
  turn.
- **Custom Catcher ×4** — the gust (two at once), fetchable in a pair with a single Green's —
  reach the KO target.
- **Great Potion ×4 / Mixed Herbs ×4** — the healing package: **Great Potion** heals 50 off a
  Pokémon with 3 Energy (turns 2HKOs into 3HKOs); **Mixed Herbs** heals cheaply. This is how
  the wall never dies — all Trainers, all searchable with Green's.
- **Fire Crystal ×3 / Energy Spinner ×1 / Fiery Flint ×1** — Energy recovery and fixing so
  you never run dry (all Trainers → Green's-able).
- **Acro Bike ×4** — dig/discard toward the empty-ish hands and setups you want.
- **Cherish Ball ×2** — search Pokémon-GX (a Trainer, so Green's can even find it).
- **Lusamine ×1** — recover 2 Supporters (Green's/Welder) from the discard for the long game.
- **Reset Stamp ×1** — hand disruption when ahead/behind.
- **Giant Hearth ×1 / Heat Factory Prism Star ×1 / Power Plant ×1 / Shrine of Punishment ×1 /
  Lysandre Labs ×1** — a flexible Stadium/Tool suite (all Trainers, all searchable):
  **Power Plant** shuts off opposing Abilities, **Shrine** chips GX boards, **Lysandre Labs**
  turns off opposing Tools. (Note: your own Power Plant is fine — you have no Abilities to
  lose.)
- **11 Fire** — enough for Welder + attacks (Green's finds the Energy-recovery Trainers so you
  can run a leaner Energy count than Firebox).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** **Pokégear → Welder** to charge a ReshiZard (Volcanion's Flare Starter can
  chip + accelerate Fire meanwhile). **Green's Exploration** is live from the start, so begin
  chaining the Trainers you need.
- **Turn 2+:** **Flare Strike (230)** while you build the Trainer toolbox; from here you heal
  and grind.

## The core loop
Charge ReshiZard (Welder + Volcanion), **Flare Strike (230)** for KOs, and use **Green's
Exploration** every turn (it's always on) to grab exactly the two Trainers you need — **Great
Potion/Mixed Herbs** to heal the wall back up, **Custom Catcher** to gust a KO, or a
disruptive Stadium/Tool. The opponent has to KO 270 HP repeatedly while you heal it — an
attrition war you're built to win.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Green's every turn** — it's never turned off (no Abilities in the deck), so treat it as
  your reliable per-turn tutor; plan which two Trainers win the turn.
- **Heal proactively** — Great Potion turns their 2HKO into a 3HKO; the extra turn is the
  plan.
- **Search the answer, not just healing** — Green's finds the disruption (Power Plant/
  Lysandre Labs/Custom Catcher) that swings a matchup, not only Potions.
- **Volcanion keeps the Active safe** — use its Flare Starter to accelerate Fire from the
  bench early so a KO doesn't cost your loaded attacker.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Water attackers** — ReshiZard is Water-Weak; a Water deck 1HKOs the wall regardless of
  healing.
- **Single-prize decks that out-tempo the setup** — Blacephalon/Malamar can race before the
  heal-lock stabilizes.
- **Item/Trainer lock (Chaotic Swell, anti-Item)** — Green's + Custom Catcher rely on
  Trainers; shutting them off hurts more than it hurts Firebox.
- **Reset Stamp against you** — disrupts the Trainer-chaining hand.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Green's Exploration: search **2 Trainer cards** — playable only with **no Ability Pokémon
  in play**, which is **always** the case here (this deck runs zero Abilities).
- Flare Strike: **230**; Great Potion: **heal 50** (Pokémon with 3 Energy).
- Volcanion Flare Starter: an **attack** (damage + Fire acceleration) — *not* an Ability, so
  it never disables Green's.
- ReshiZard: **270 HP** — the tank the whole deck protects.
