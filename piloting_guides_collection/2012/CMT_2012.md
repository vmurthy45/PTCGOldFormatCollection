# How to Pilot — CMT (2012)

_Format: HGSS-on 2012. "CMT" = **C**elebi Prime / **M**ewtwo-EX / **T**ornadus. A
top-tier speed deck of the format (multiple Regional/Championship results)._

## One-line identity
Pure speed and consistency: **Celebi Prime** accelerates Grass Energy by hand, so you
power a hard-hitting **Mewtwo-EX** (X-Ball) or a **Tornadus** in a single turn — often
hitting for 60–100 as early as turn 1. No gimmicks, just fast, flexible beatdown with
a deep tech pool.

## The meta it lived in
An extremely fast Basic-EX format. CMT thrived because it could **donk**, win the
all-important Mewtwo war, and tech for everything (Terrakion, Shaymin-EX, Regigigas).
It repeatedly put multiple copies into Regional top cuts and won the European
Challenge Cup; it was a defining "big three" deck alongside Darkrai and Eelektrik.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Celebi Prime (92/102) ×3** — the engine. **Forest Breath**: attach a Grass Energy
  from hand to one of your Pokémon each turn (on top of your normal attachment). With
  Skyarrow Bridge giving it free retreat, you can Forest Breath multiple times a turn
  and pour Energy onto Mewtwo.
- **Mewtwo-EX (54/99) ×3** — **X-Ball** scales with total Energy on both Mewtwos;
  attacks for Colorless, so Grass fuels it with no drawback. The deck's main attacker
  and the reason it wins the Mewtwo mirror (more Energy = you 1HKO first).
- **Tornadus-EX (90/108) ×1** — **Power Blast**: 100 (discards an Energy); a heavy
  hitter that, unlike Mewtwo, is hard to 1HKO and near-impossible to Catcher-lock
  (retreat 1 under Skyarrow). Fighting-resistant.
- **Smeargle (8/90) ×2** — starter; **Portrait** borrows the opponent's Supporter,
  free-retreat under Skyarrow.
- **Terrakion (73/101) ×2** — Fighting tech / revenge attacker; can be loaded fast
  with Celebi + Energy Retrieval and swings the mirror.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Skyarrow Bridge (91/99) ×2** — Stadium that gives Basics with retreat 1 (Celebi,
  Tornadus, Smeargle) *free* retreat. Central to the whole engine — it's what lets you
  loop Forest Breath.
- **Switch ×3** — "frosting" on Skyarrow: play Switch *and* still retreat the same
  turn, letting you Forest Breath then swap back into your attacker (huge in the
  Tornadus war).
- **Dual Ball ×4 / Random Receiver ×3 / PONT ×4 / Juniper ×3 / N ×1** — the speed and
  consistency package; you want to open Celebi or Tornadus almost every game.
- **Junk Arm ×4 / Pokémon Catcher ×3 / PlusPower ×2 / Eviolite ×2** — reach and
  tanking; PlusPower pushes X-Ball over KO lines, Eviolite makes your attackers
  survive a hit.
- **Energy Retrieval / Energy Search / Revive ×1 each** — utility: recur Energy for
  Terrakion, fix Energy, get back a prized/KO'd Mewtwo.
- **6 Grass / 3 Fighting / 4 DCE** — Grass for Forest Breath + X-Ball fuel, Fighting
  for Terrakion, DCE for fast Mewtwo/Tornadus.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** open Celebi (best) or Tornadus. Forest Breath a Grass onto Mewtwo or
  Tornadus, use PONT/Juniper to dig for DCE + Skyarrow Bridge, and if it comes
  together, hit for 60–100 immediately (Hurricane / early X-Ball).
- **Setup achieved** = Celebi(s) online with Skyarrow down, giving you two Energy
  attachments a turn to load a Mewtwo.

## The core loop
Every turn: Forest Breath (+ manual attach, + Switch tricks) to keep Energy flowing
onto the active attacker, then X-Ball / Power Blast for a KO. In the Mewtwo war, get
*more* Energy than they do so you win the exchange; in the Tornadus war, use Eviolite
+ Switch to trade favorably. Bring up Celebi/Smeargle after a KO (free retreat) so you
never waste a turn.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Win the Mewtwo war** — it's the deck's whole reason to exist; don't bench a Mewtwo
  you aren't ready to attack with (it's Catcher/Mewtwo bait).
- After a KO, **promote Celebi or Smeargle** if Skyarrow is down — even if you don't
  plan to use them, it keeps your attackers safe.
- Watch **Celebi's 60 HP** — a lone Celebi start can be donked; don't over-expose it.
- Use PlusPower to force whiffs — making the opponent extend for an extra Energy is a
  real edge.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Six Corners / big Dragon decks (Outrage)** — Tornadus is useless into them and
  Mewtwo gets answered by their own Mewtwo + DCE; a genuinely hard matchup.
- **Lightning decks** — Tornadus is Lightning-Weak (Zekrom/Eelektrik punish it).
- **Item lock / early disruption** (Vileplume, Zebstrika Disconnect) can cut off the
  Item-heavy engine.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Forest Breath: **+1 Grass attachment** per turn (stacks with your normal attach).
- X-Ball: **×20 per Energy** on both Mewtwos combined.
- Power Blast (Tornadus-EX): **100**, discard 1 Energy.
- Turn-1 damage of **60–100** is realistic and often the plan.
