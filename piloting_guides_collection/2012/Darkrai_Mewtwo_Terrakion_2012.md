# How to Pilot — Darkrai Mewtwo (2012)

_Format: HGSS-on 2012 (BLW–DRX, "Big Basics" era). This build closely mirrors Igor
Costa's 2012 World Championship-**winning** list._

## One-line identity
The premier "Big Basics" toolbox: a wall of powerful Basic EXs where **Darkrai-EX**
gives the whole team free retreat and **Night Spear** snipes the bench, while
**Mewtwo-EX** and **Terrakion** are matchup-specific hammers. You pick the right
attacker for what's across the table and grind out prizes with superior mobility.

## The meta it lived in
2012 was defined by fast, hard-hitting Basics — Mewtwo-EX was in nearly every deck,
and Darkrai-EX became just as ubiquitous after Dark Explorers. Because Terrakion was
being teched everywhere to punish Darkrai, this deck answered its own weakness by
running Mewtwo and Terrakion itself. It won Worlds and multiple National
Championships; it was the deck to know.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Darkrai-EX (63/108) ×3** — the core. Ability **Dark Cloak**: every Pokémon with
  a Darkness Energy has *no retreat cost*, making the whole clunky team mobile.
  **Night Spear**: 90 to the Active + 30 to a benched Pokémon — the snipe wins prize
  races by softening two targets at once.
- **Mewtwo-EX (54/99) ×3** — **X-Ball** scales with total Energy on both Mewtwos; the
  format's best attacker and your answer to opposing Mewtwo and to Fighting decks.
- **Terrakion (73/101) ×2** — Fighting tech: **Retaliate** hits big the turn after a
  KO, and it 1HKOs opposing Darkrai (Fighting Weakness). The anti-meta card.
- **Dark Patch (93/108) ×4** — accelerator: attach a Darkness from the discard to a
  benched Dark Pokémon. With Shaymin, you can fully load a Darkrai in one turn.
- **Shaymin (8/95) ×2** — **Celebratory Wind**-style energy movement / support to
  concentrate Energy where you need it fast (helps the one-turn setups).
- **Smeargle (8/90) ×2** — starter: **Portrait** uses a Supporter from the opponent's
  hand; free-retreat under Dark Cloak so it's not a liability.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Junk Arm ×4** — reuse a spent Item (Catcher, Dark Patch, PlusPower) from the
  discard by pitching two cards; huge consistency + late-game reach.
- **Pokémon Catcher ×3** — drag up a target to Night Spear or to remove a support
  Basic. Often the prize-winning play.
- **Eviolite ×3** — −20 from attacks on the holder; makes Darkrai a 180-HP wall and
  forces opponents to find PlusPower to hit KO numbers (esp. vs. Terrakion).
- **Random Receiver ×2 / PONT ×4 / Juniper ×3 / N ×2** — the draw/consistency spine.
  N doubles as disruption when you're ahead.
- **Super Scoop Up ×2** — reuse Smeargle's Portrait, or pick up a damaged Darkrai to
  reset it (with Shaymin, replay it fresh).
- **Prism ×2 / DCE ×3 / 7 Darkness / 2 Fighting** — Prism enables Terrakion's
  colored costs; DCE fuels Mewtwo; Darkness feeds Dark Patch and Night Spear.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** ideally start Smeargle (free retreat under Darkrai), Portrait for a
  Supporter, and set up a Darkrai on the bench. Discard Darkness (Juniper/Ultra Ball)
  so Dark Patch is live.
- **Turn 2:** Dark Patch + attachment (+ Shaymin) to fully load a Darkrai, then
  **Night Spear** — 90 to the Active and 30 onto their most important benched Basic.

## The core loop
Night Spear every turn, placing the 30 snipe to set up future KOs; use free retreat
(Dark Cloak) to rotate the *right* attacker in — Mewtwo into the Mewtwo war, Terrakion
into Darkrai/Landorus, Darkrai into everything else. Catcher + snipe to pick off
support Basics. Junk Arm keeps your Catchers/Patches/PlusPowers flowing.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Know your prized Darkrai count** on your first search — prizing multiple is scary;
  three copies is the number that survives it.
- **Match the attacker to the matchup** rather than defaulting to Darkrai.
- **Eviolite on the wall** early forces the opponent to over-invest to KO you.
- Place the **Night Spear snipe** deliberately — it's how you take a 6th prize a turn
  early.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Pure Fighting decks** (Landorus/Terrakion, Fighting boxes) — Darkrai is
  Fighting-Weak; even Mewtwo doesn't fully cover it.
- **Blastoise/Keldeo-EX** — Keldeo 1HKOs Darkrai while Darkrai can't 1HKO Keldeo, and
  Squirtle BCR blunts the Night Spear snipe, losing you the prize race.
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — shuts off Dark Cloak (your free retreat) and Dark Patch;
  Tool Scrapper helps but this list is light on it.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Night Spear: **90 active + 30 bench snipe** (no Weakness on the bench damage).
- Dark Cloak: **free retreat** for all your Darkness-carrying Pokémon.
- X-Ball: scales with **total Energy on both Mewtwos** (×20 per Energy).
- Setup: **turn-2 Night Spear** is the goal, via Dark Patch + Shaymin.
