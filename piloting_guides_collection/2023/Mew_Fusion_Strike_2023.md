# How to Pilot — Mew (Fusion Strike) (2023)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet-on Standard 2023 (post-rotation). The Fusion Strike **Mew VMAX**
deck carried into the SV format — still fast, consistent, and combo-driven._

## One-line identity
The engine deck that survived rotation: **Genesect V's Fusion Strike System** draws you a
fresh hand once per Genesect every turn, **Mew VMAX's Cross Fusion Strike** copies **Genesect's
Techno Blast (210)** for 2 Energy, and **Power Tablet** (×4 in a turn) pushes it to OHKO
numbers. Post-rotation it leans harder on **Forest Seal Stone**, **Judge/Iono** disruption, and
Lost-Zone tools (Lost Vacuum / Lost City) for the prize-trade race.

## The meta it lived in
2023 Standard was a varied field — Gardevoir ex, Lugia VSTAR, Lost Zone Box, Giratina VSTAR,
Miraidon ex. Mew VMAX remained a top pick because its **draw engine is still elite** and it's a
turn-2 attacker that races anything. The Fusion Strike Energy variant "fell out of favor late
last year" then came back as the format opened up; its counters are the same as ever — **Path
to the Peak** (shuts off Fusion Strike System) and **Darkness** attackers (Weakness).

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Genesect V (185/264) ×4** — the heart. **Fusion Strike System** (Ability): once per Genesect
  per turn, draw until your hand equals your **Fusion Strike Pokémon in play** — a huge,
  repeatable draw with a wide bench. **Techno Blast**: 210 (can't attack next turn, but Mew
  copies it). Bench 2–3.
- **Mew V (113/264) ×4 → Mew VMAX (114/264) ×3** — the attacker. **Cross Fusion Strike**: copy a
  benched Fusion Strike Pokémon's attack (usually **Techno Blast 210**) for 2 Colorless. **Free
  retreat** — retreat Mew VMAX and return it to reset the "can't attack" clause. **Max Miracle**
  (130, ignores effects) bypasses damage-prevention.
- **Meloetta (124/264) ×1** — **Melodious Echo**: 70 × Fusion Strike Energy on all your Pokémon —
  a secondary attacker that helps the **prize trade** vs. single-prize decks.
- **Elesa's Sparkle ×2** — attach **2 Fusion Strike Energy from deck** to 2 Fusion Strike
  Pokémon — turn-1 acceleration.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Oricorio (42/264) ×1** — **its Ability** protects your bench from spread/snipe (a specific
  answer, e.g. vs. Lost Zone Sableye's Lost Mine spread) — a key SV-era tech.
- **Boss's Orders ×2 / Iono ×1 / Judge ×1** — gust + disruption. **Iono/Judge** shrink both
  hands (disruption that the engine recovers from better than the opponent does); **Boss's
  Orders** pulls the KO target.
- **Power Tablet ×4** — +30 to a Fusion Strike attack; stacking all four (enabled by the draw)
  turns 210 into 330 — OHKO on a VSTAR/VMAX.
- **Battle VIP Pass ×4** — turn-1 only: bench 2 Basics (Genesect + Mew V) — the ideal opener.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Cram-o-matic ×4 / Nest Ball ×2** — search + dig (Cram-o-matic: discard an
  Item, flip → search any card; also pitches a dead Battle VIP Pass).
- **Lost Vacuum ×2 / Switch Cart ×2 / Escape Rope ×1 / Pal Pad ×1** — remove a Tool/Stadium
  (their Path to the Peak / a Manaphy-style Tool), pivot/reset Mew VMAX, recover Supporters.
- **Forest Seal Stone ×3** — once-per-game "search any card" on a Pokémon — the SV-era
  consistency boost (grab Boss/Power Tablet/the missing piece).
- **Choice Belt ×2** — +30 vs. V/VSTAR (another OHKO number-fixer).
- **Box of Disaster ×1** — a Tool with disruptive/Lost-Zone utility (tech).
- **Stadium suite: 2 Lost City + 1 Path to the Peak + 1 Crystal Cave** — **Lost City** (KO'd
  Pokémon go to the Lost Zone, not discard — denies opponents recovery *and* helps your prize
  trade); **Crystal Cave** (heal); **Path to the Peak** (deny opposing Abilities — and win the
  Stadium war so *their* Path doesn't stick).
- **4 Fusion Strike Energy + 3 Double Turbo Energy** — Fusion Strike Energy fuels Meloetta/the
  system; **Double Turbo** powers Cross Fusion Strike cheaply.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Battle VIP Pass** → Genesect V + Mew V; attach Energy to Mew V. **Fusion Strike
  System** to dig; **Elesa's Sparkle** if going second.
- **Turn 2:** evolve **Mew VMAX**, **Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast (210)** — +Power Tablet
  for the OHKO.

## The core loop
Fill the bench with Fusion Strike Pokémon → **Fusion Strike System** (multiple Genesect = draw
your deck) → **Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast (210)** every turn → **Power Tablet** to reach
OHKO numbers → **retreat + return Mew VMAX** to reset the clause. **Boss's Orders** picks the
target, **Iono/Judge** disrupt, **Lost City** grinds the prize trade, **Forest Seal Stone**
fetches the missing piece. You out-draw and out-tempo the field.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Bench Fusion Strike Pokémon early** — the draw scales with your count; a wide board = a huge
  hand.
- **Don't over-Genesect** — 2–3 is right; too many and you draw fewer *new* cards.
- **Reset Techno Blast with free retreat** — Mew VMAX's 0 retreat means you attack every turn.
- **Answer Path to the Peak** — Lost Vacuum it / win the Stadium war; without your Abilities the
  deck stalls.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Darkness attackers** — Mew VMAX is Dark-Weak; a dedicated Dark deck 1HKOs it.
- **Path to the Peak** — shuts off Fusion Strike System (your engine); you must remove it.
- **Single-prize aggro (Lost Zone Sableye)** — trades favorably against your 3-prize VMAX and
  attacks first; Meloetta/Oricorio/Lost City help the trade.
- **Being teched against** — its popularity means opponents pack Drapion V / Dark / Path
  specifically for you.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Fusion Strike System: **draw up to your Fusion Strike Pokémon count**, once per Genesect per
  turn.
- Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast: **210** for 2 Energy (330 with 4 Power Tablet).
- Elesa's Sparkle: **2 Fusion Strike Energy from deck** → 2 Pokémon; Forest Seal Stone: **search
  any card** (once per game).
- Mew VMAX: **310 HP, free retreat** (reset Techno Blast by retreating).
