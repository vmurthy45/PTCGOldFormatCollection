# How to Pilot — Urshifu VMAX (2022)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2022 (BST–on). "Rapid Strike Urshifu" — a Fighting/Water
bench-snipe deck built on the Inteleon engine; a top-tier archetype since Battle Styles._

## One-line identity
The premier spread/snipe deck: **Rapid Strike Urshifu VMAX's G-Max Rapid Flow** does **120 to
two of the opponent's Pokémon** for just 3 Energy (efficient thanks to Rapid Strike Energy),
picking off support Pokémon and setting up multi-prize turns. The **Inteleon (Shady Dealings +
Quick Shooting) engine** searches your Trainers *and* pings extra damage, and **Octillery**
finds any Rapid Strike card on demand.

## The meta it lived in
Rapid Strike Urshifu has "stood the test of time" — Azul Garcia Griego won Players Cup III
with it, and it stayed top-tier through 2022 with a fantastic matchup spread (it preys on the
low-HP support Pokémon every deck runs). Its edge is **snipe + prize math**: G-Max Rapid Flow
+ Quick Shooting pings line up KOs across the board that single-target attackers can't. Its
kryptonite is **Manaphy** (bench protection) and Psychic decks (Weakness).

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Rapid Strike Urshifu V (87/163) ×3 → Rapid Strike Urshifu VMAX (88/163) ×2** — the core.
  **G-Max Rapid Flow**: **120 to two** of the opponent's Pokémon for 2 Fighting + 1 Colorless
  (discard all Energy — but Rapid Strike Energy re-arms fast). **Gale Thrust**: 150 for one
  Energy when it moves into the Active spot — an efficient single hit. A 2-2 line is enough
  (you rarely need two at once).
- **Sobble (41/198) ×4 → Drizzile (56/202) ×3 → Inteleon (58/202) ×2 + Inteleon (43/198) ×1** —
  the dual engine: **Shady Dealings** (Drizzile 1 / Inteleon-CRE 2 Trainers) searches your
  toolbox, and **Inteleon (43/198) Quick Shooting** pings **20 to any Pokémon** each turn —
  the extra damage that turns G-Max Rapid Flow's 120 into KOs.
- **Remoraid (36/163) ×2 → Octillery (37/163) ×2** — **Rapid Strike Search** (Ability): search
  **any Rapid Strike card** (Urshifu, Rapid Strike Energy, Tower of Waters, Korrina's Focus) —
  the consistency glue; grabbing your Energy and pieces in one Ability.
- **Irida ×4** — search a Water Pokémon + a Water Item (the Inteleon line + Bucket/Energy); the
  main setup Supporter.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Manaphy (41/172) ×1** — **Wave Veil**: protects *your* bench from snipe (mirror / Inteleon
  pings) — and note the opponent's Manaphy is your worst enemy, so pack ways around it.
- **Medicham V (83/203) ×1** — **Yoga Loop**: after a KO with Psycho Punch, take a turn cycle
  advantage — a soft-lock engine (KO a benched Pokémon / their Manaphy and chain turns).
- **Cheryl ×2** — heal **all damage** off one Pokémon — resets a chipped Urshifu VMAX so it
  can't be 2HKO'd (a grind-winning heal).
- **Boss's Orders ×1 / Marnie ×1 / Roxanne ×1** — gust + draw/disruption; **Roxanne** (after
  they take a prize) shrinks their hand to 4 + draws you 6.
- **Scoop Up Net ×4** — re-trigger Shady Dealings / pick up a chipped Pokémon / reset Manaphy —
  a key toolbox loop.
- **Evolution Incense ×3 / Level Ball ×3 / Quick Ball ×2 / Energy Search ×2 / Ultra Ball ×1 /
  Hisuian Heavy Ball ×1** — search the lines and Energy.
- **Escape Rope ×1 / Ordinary Rod ×1 / Pal Pad ×1 / Rescue Carrier ×1 / Tool Jammer ×1** —
  pivot, recover Pokémon/Energy/Supporters, and **Tool Jammer** (shut off an opposing Tool).
- **Tower of Waters ×2** — Stadium: free retreat for Rapid Strike Pokémon (pivot Urshifu into
  the Active for Gale Thrust / out to reset).
- **4 Rapid Strike Energy + 3 Fighting Energy** — **Rapid Strike Energy** provides 2 Energy at
  once (re-arms G-Max Rapid Flow after the discard); Fighting for costs/Martial-Arts-Dojo math.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** bench **Sobble** (Keep Calling widens it) + Urshifu V + Remoraid; attach Energy.
- **Turn 2:** evolve **Octillery** (search Rapid Strike Energy/pieces) and an **Inteleon**
  (Shady Dealings), and start **Gale Thrust / G-Max Rapid Flow** — pinging with Quick Shooting.

## The core loop
Set up the Inteleon engine (search + Quick Shooting pings), then **G-Max Rapid Flow (120×2)**
to snipe support Pokémon and spread — combine the pings + snipe to take **multi-prize turns**
(e.g. KO a Sobble *and* a Crobat V). **Rapid Strike Energy** re-arms the attack; **Cheryl**
heals your VMAX to grind; **Octillery** keeps you stocked. You dismantle their board from every
angle and win the prize race.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Aim the snipe to line up KOs** — G-Max Rapid Flow + Quick Shooting is spread math; place
  damage where it converts to prizes (kill their engine before it evolves).
- **Re-arm with Rapid Strike Energy** — the discard on G-Max Rapid Flow is fine because 1 Rapid
  Strike Energy = 2 Energy back.
- **Cheryl to reset a chipped Urshifu** — denies their 2HKO and keeps your attacker alive.
- **Play around / remove Manaphy** — an opposing Manaphy blanks your whole snipe plan; force it
  Active (Boss) or attack around it.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Psychic (Mew VMAX)** — Urshifu is Psychic-Weak; Mew 1HKOs it and races — the toughest
  matchup.
- **Manaphy (opposing)** — Wave Veil blanks G-Max Rapid Flow/Quick Shooting snipe; you must
  answer it.
- **3-prize liability** — VMAX gives up 3; decks that OHKO VMAX (Giratina/Mew) trade up on you.
- **Consistency wobble** — infamous for a great on-paper spread but occasionally clunky starts;
  Octillery mitigates but doesn't erase it.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- G-Max Rapid Flow: **120 to two Pokémon** for 2 Fighting + 1 Colorless (discard all Energy).
- Gale Thrust: **150** for 1 Energy when Urshifu moves into the Active spot.
- Quick Shooting (Inteleon): **20 to any Pokémon** per turn; Octillery: search **any Rapid
  Strike card**.
- Rapid Strike Energy: provides **2 Energy** (re-arms the attack).
