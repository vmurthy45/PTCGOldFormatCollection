# How to Pilot — Fusion Strike Mew (2022)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2022 (FST–on). "Mew VMAX" — the fast, consistent
Fusion Strike combo deck; a perennial best-deck-in-format contender._

## One-line identity
The engine deck of the format: **Genesect V's Fusion Strike System** draws you a full new
hand **once per Genesect, every turn** (up to four times), so you see a huge chunk of your
deck each turn. **Mew VMAX's Cross Fusion Strike** copies **Genesect's Techno Blast (210)**
for just 2 Energy, and **Power Tablet** (×4 in a turn) pushes it to OHKO numbers. Fast,
flexible, and combo-driven.

## The meta it lived in
Mew VMAX was arguably the strongest, most consistent deck in 2022 Standard — widely regarded
as the **best deck in the format (BDIF)**, and its draw engine is one of the best ever printed. It's a turn-2-attacker that races anything, held in check
mainly by **Path to the Peak** (shuts off Fusion Strike System), Single Strike Dark decks
(Weakness), and its own popularity (everyone teched against it). It has "the DNA of a
World-Championship-winning deck."

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Genesect V (185/264) ×4** — the heart. **Fusion Strike System** (Ability): once per
  Genesect per turn, **draw until your hand equals your number of Fusion Strike Pokémon in
  play** — with a wide bench that's a massive, repeatable draw. **Techno Blast**: 210 (can't
  attack next turn — but Mew copies it, so that clause rarely matters). You usually bench 2–3.
- **Mew V (113/264) ×4 → Mew VMAX (114/264) ×3** — the attacker. **Cross Fusion Strike**: copy
  a benched Fusion Strike Pokémon's attack (almost always **Techno Blast 210**) for 2 Colorless.
  **Free retreat** on both — retreat a Mew VMAX and return it to reset the "can't attack"
  clause, then Techno Blast again. **Max Miracle** (130, ignores effects) bypasses Zamazenta/
  damage-prevention.
- **Meloetta (124/264) ×2** — **Melodious Echo**: 70 × Fusion Strike Energy on all your Pokémon
  — a big secondary attacker (and a target for Elesa's Sparkle). Also just a Fusion Strike body
  to grow Fusion Strike System.
- **Elesa's Sparkle ×3** — attach **2 Fusion Strike Energy from deck** to 2 Fusion Strike
  Pokémon — turn-1 acceleration (Elesa + 1 manual = a powered Mew VMAX in one turn).

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Pumpkaboo (76/203) ×1** — **Pumpkin Pit**/utility (bench control / a small disruptive body).
- **Boss's Orders ×3** — gust the KO target (a benched Pokémon you can Techno Blast for the win).
- **Power Tablet ×4** — +30 to a Fusion Strike attack; stacking all four in one turn (enabled by
  the huge draw) turns 210 into 330 — OHKO on a VMAX.
- **Battle VIP Pass ×4** — turn-1 only: bench **2 Basics** — the ideal opener to set up multiple
  Genesect + Mew V fast.
- **Cram-o-matic ×4** — discard an Item, flip a coin; heads → search any card — extra digging /
  finds the piece (or discards a dead Battle VIP Pass).
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Rotom Phone ×4** — search Pokémon and dig; **Rotom Phone**
  (draw/dig) supplements the engine.
- **Switch ×2 / Escape Rope ×2 / Echoing Horn ×1** — pivot/reset Mew VMAX; **Echoing Horn**
  recovers a KO'd Basic (rebuild the bench for Fusion Strike System).
- **Choice Belt ×2** — +30 vs. V/VSTAR (another OHKO number-fixer alongside Power Tablet).
- **Rose Tower ×2** — Stadium: draw until you have 3 (combos with the engine) *and* a counter to
  the opponent's **Path to the Peak** (bounce it so Fusion Strike System comes back on).
- **4 Fusion Strike Energy + 3 Double Turbo Energy** — Fusion Strike Energy fuels Meloetta/the
  system; **Double Turbo** powers Cross Fusion Strike cheaply (−20 damage, but Techno Blast is
  copied so it doesn't matter).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Battle VIP Pass** → bench Genesect V + Mew V; attach an Energy to Mew V. Use
  **Fusion Strike System** to dig, and **Elesa's Sparkle** for acceleration if going second.
- **Turn 2:** evolve **Mew VMAX**, **Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast (210)** — often +Power
  Tablet for the OHKO.

## The core loop
Fill the bench with Fusion Strike Pokémon → **Fusion Strike System** (multiple Genesect =
draw your deck) → **Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast (210)** every turn → **Power Tablet**
to reach OHKO numbers → **retreat Mew VMAX and return** to reset the attack clause. **Boss's
Orders** picks the target; **Rose Tower** answers their Path to the Peak. You out-draw and
out-tempo the field.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Bench Fusion Strike Pokémon early** — Fusion Strike System draws equal to your Fusion Strike
  count; a wide board = a huge hand.
- **Don't over-Genesect** — 2–3 is usually right; too many and you draw fewer *new* cards (your
  hand's already full).
- **Reset Techno Blast with free retreat** — Mew VMAX's 0 retreat means you attack every turn.
- **Answer Path to the Peak** — Rose Tower/Stadium war is mandatory; without your Abilities the
  deck stalls.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Darkness (Single Strike Houndoom/Umbreon VMAX)** — Mew VMAX is Dark-Weak; a dedicated Dark
  deck 1HKOs it.
- **Path to the Peak** — shuts off Fusion Strike System (your whole engine); you must bounce it.
- **Fan of Waves / Special-Energy denial** — the deck leans on Special Energy (Fusion Strike/
  Double Turbo); stripping it slows you.
- **Being teched against** — its popularity means opponents pack specific counters; a metagame
  problem more than a card problem.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Fusion Strike System: **draw up to your Fusion Strike Pokémon count**, once per Genesect per
  turn.
- Cross Fusion Strike → Techno Blast: **210** for 2 Energy (330 with 4 Power Tablet).
- Elesa's Sparkle: **2 Fusion Strike Energy from deck** → 2 Pokémon.
- Mew VMAX: **310 HP, free retreat** (reset Techno Blast by retreating).
