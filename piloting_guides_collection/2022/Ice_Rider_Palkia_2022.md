# How to Pilot — Ice Rider Palkia (2022)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2022 (BRS–on, the VSTAR/Inteleon era). This build
mirrors Rikuto Ohashi's 2022 World Championship-**winning** Ice Rider Palkia deck._

## One-line identity
A Water toolbox with two big hitters and the format's best engine: **Origin Forme Palkia
VSTAR** accelerates Water (Star Portal) and hits up to **~290 with Subspace Swell + Choice
Belt off a packed bench**, while **Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX** OHKOs for **250** on just 2
Energy. The **Inteleon (Shady Dealings) engine** searches the exact Trainers you need every
turn — making it consistent, flexible, and brutal.

## The meta it lived in
2022 Standard revolved around **Arceus VSTAR** (the Starbirth toolbox), **Mew VMAX** (Fusion
Strike), **Origin Forme Palkia VSTAR/Inteleon**, and **Rapid Strike Urshifu**. Ice Rider
Palkia sat at the top: Palkia VSTAR's Star Portal Energy acceleration plus the Inteleon
engine gave it both power and the best consistency in the format. Rikuto Ohashi took it all
the way to the 2022 World Championship in London — Subspace Swell + Choice Belt off a full
bench one-shots almost anything.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Origin Forme Palkia VSTAR (40/189) ×3** (from Origin Forme Palkia V ×3) — the core.
  **Subspace Swell**: 60 + 20 per Pokémon on **both** benches (up to ~200, +30 Choice Belt =
  ~230–290) for 2 Water — scales huge with a packed board. **Star Portal** (VSTAR Power, once
  per game): attach **3 Water from discard** to your Water Pokémon — the acceleration that
  powers everything.
- **Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX (46/198) ×1** (from Ice Rider Calyrex V ×1) — the second hitter.
  **Max Lance**: **250** for 2 Water (discard 2 Water) — OHKOs most V/VSTAR on a tiny Energy
  cost. A one-of you set up when you want a big clean KO.
- **Sobble (41/198) ×4 → Drizzile (56/202) ×3 → Inteleon (58/202) ×2** — the engine.
  **Shady Dealings** (Drizzile searches 1 Trainer on evolve; **Inteleon searches 2**) — grabs
  the exact card you need (Boss's Orders, Path to the Peak, an Energy card, Irida). **Sobble's
  Keep Calling** fills the bench (which also grows Subspace Swell).
- **Irida ×3 / Melony ×2** — Water support Supporters: **Irida** searches a Water Pokémon + a
  Water Item (Capacious Bucket); **Melony** draws 3 + attaches a Water from discard to a
  benched Water V/VSTAR — turn-by-turn acceleration.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Manaphy (41/172) ×1** — **Wave Veil** (Ability): protects your bench from snipe (crucial vs.
  Rapid Strike Urshifu's G-Max Rapid Flow and Inteleon's Double Gunner).
- **Radiant Greninja (46/189) ×1** — **Concealed Cards** (discard an Energy → draw 2): a draw/
  Energy-cycling engine; **Moonlight Shuriken** as an emergency two-target attack.
- **Boss's Orders ×2 / Leon ×1 / Marnie ×1 / Professor's Research ×1 / Roxanne ×1** — gust +
  draw + disruption. **Boss's Orders** pulls the KO target; **Leon** buffs Subspace Swell to
  reach numbers; **Roxanne** (after they take a prize) shrinks their hand to 4 + draws you 6 —
  a strong comeback button.
- **Evolution Incense ×3 / Level Ball ×3 / Quick Ball ×3 / Capacious Bucket ×2 / Battle VIP Pass
  ×1 / Ultra Ball ×1 / Rare Candy ×1** — search the Inteleon line and Water Energy; **Battle
  VIP Pass** (turn-1 only) benches 2 Basics for an explosive start; **Rare Candy** jumps
  Sobble→Inteleon.
- **Scoop Up Net ×2** — pick up an Inteleon/Sobble to **re-trigger Shady Dealings** (more
  searches) or reset Manaphy.
- **Escape Rope ×1 / Pal Pad ×1 / Choice Belt ×2 / Path to the Peak ×1 / Training Court ×1** —
  pivot/disrupt, recover Supporters, **Choice Belt** (+30 vs. V/VSTAR — the number-fixer),
  **Path to the Peak** (shut off opposing Abilities — huge vs. Mew/Arceus), **Training Court**
  (recover Water Energy).
- **8 Water Energy** — the only Energy; Star Portal/Melony/Bucket keep it flowing.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** ideally **Battle VIP Pass** → bench Sobble(s) + a Palkia V; **Sobble Keep Calling**
  to widen the bench. Attach Water.
- **Turn 2:** **Rare Candy / evolve** an Inteleon (Shady Dealings → grab what you need), **Melony
  or Star Portal** to load Water, and start **Subspace Swell**.

## The core loop
Widen your bench (Sobble Keep Calling) to pump **Subspace Swell**, accelerate Water (Star
Portal once, then Melony/Bucket/Irida), and swing for 200+ (Choice Belt for the OHKO number).
Use the **Inteleon engine** to fetch exactly the Trainer each turn — **Boss's Orders** for the
KO target, **Path to the Peak** to shut off Mew/Arceus Abilities, **Manaphy** to blank their
snipe. Bring in **Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX (Max Lance 250)** for a big clean KO when you want it.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Fill your bench** — Subspace Swell scales with total benched Pokémon; a wide board is your
  damage.
- **Spend Star Portal at the right time** — it's once per game; use it on the turn it enables a
  KO or sets up two attackers.
- **Inteleon fetches the answer** — search the specific out (Path to the Peak vs. Ability decks,
  Boss's Orders for the KO, Choice Belt for the number). This flexibility is the deck's edge.
- **Manaphy down vs. snipe decks** — Urshifu/Inteleon VMAX punish an unprotected bench.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Lightning (Palkia is Lightning-Weak)** — Flying Pikachu VMAX / Lightning attackers hit
  Palkia for Weakness.
- **Path to the Peak against *you*** — it doesn't stop Palkia's attacks, but it shuts off your
  own Inteleon/Radiant Greninja Abilities; you run your own to win the Stadium war.
- **Fast OHKO decks (Mew VMAX)** — can race the setup; you rely on Manaphy + consistency to
  keep pace.
- **Bench snipe (Urshifu/Inteleon VMAX)** — without Manaphy down, your Sobble/Drizzile get
  picked off.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Subspace Swell: **60 + 20 × Pokémon on both benches** (+30 Choice Belt) for 2 Water.
- Star Portal (VSTAR Power): **attach 3 Water from discard** (once per game).
- Max Lance (Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX): **250** for 2 Water (discard 2 Water).
- Shady Dealings: Drizzile **1 Trainer** / Inteleon **2 Trainers** on evolve.
