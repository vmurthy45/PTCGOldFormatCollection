# How to Pilot — ADP Flying Pikachu (2022)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2022 (BRS–on). An **Arceus VSTAR** toolbox paired with
**Flying Pikachu VMAX** + **Hisuian Decidueye VSTAR** and a **Path to the Peak** Ability-lock._

## One-line identity
An Arceus VSTAR control-beatdown: **Starbirth** (Arceus VSTAR's VSTAR Power) searches **any 2
cards** for perfect consistency, **Trinity Nova (200)** accelerates Energy to set up the next
attacker, and you close with **Flying Pikachu VMAX** (a Lightning VMAX that dodges damage) and
**Hisuian Decidueye VSTAR**. All under a **Path to the Peak** lock that shuts off the format's
Ability engines.

## The meta it lived in
**Arceus VSTAR** was the most dominant, flexible card of 2022 — its Colorless typing pairs with
any V, and Starbirth's search made every variant hyper-consistent. This build weaponizes
**Path to the Peak** (×4): most 2022 decks (Mew's Fusion Strike System, Palkia/Urshifu's
Inteleon, Bibarel) live and die by Abilities, and turning them off while you attack with
low-Ability-dependence Pokémon is a powerful plan. **Flying Pikachu VMAX** adds a Lightning
attacker that answers the Water decks (Palkia/Urshifu).

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Arceus V (122/172) ×4 → Arceus VSTAR (123/172) ×3** — the core. **Trinity Nova**: 200 for 3
  Energy **and attach 3 basic Energy from deck to your Colorless V** — damage + acceleration in
  one, powering your next attacker. **Starbirth** (VSTAR Power, once per game): search **any 2
  cards** — the best consistency tool in the format.
- **Flying Pikachu V (6/25) ×3 → Flying Pikachu VMAX (7/25) ×2** — a closer. **Thunderbolt** hits
  hard, and Flying Pikachu VMAX's **Fly** (attack + can't be damaged/affected next turn in some
  lines) makes it a slippery attacker; Lightning typing answers Water decks.
- **Hisuian Decidueye V (83/189) ×2 → Hisuian Decidueye VSTAR (84/189) ×2** — a Fighting/utility
  attacker; **its VSTAR Power / attack** covers matchups Arceus/Pikachu can't and adds reach.
- **Bidoof (120/172) ×2 → Bibarel (121/172) ×2** — **Industrious Incisors** (Ability): draw until
  you have 5 — the draw engine. (Note: it's an Ability, so under *your own* Path to the Peak it's
  off — you draw *before* dropping Path, or lean on Supporters; sequencing matters.)

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Crobat V (104/189) ×1** — **Dark Asset**: draw until you have 6 — a burst draw to set up
  (again, mind your own Path to the Peak timing).
- **Lumineon V (40/172) ×1** — **Luminous Sign**: search a Supporter on entry — grabs Boss's
  Orders/Marnie/Research when you need it (a great turn-1 body).
- **Pumpkaboo (76/203) ×1** — bench control / small disruptive utility.
- **Marnie ×4 / Boss's Orders ×4 / Professor's Research ×2 / Raihan ×1** — a heavy Supporter suite
  (this deck leans on Supporters *because* Path to the Peak turns off Ability draw). **Marnie**
  disrupts + refills; **Boss's Orders** pulls the KO; **Raihan** accelerates 2 Lightning + draws.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Evolution Incense ×1 / Switch ×1** — search and pivot.
- **Path to the Peak ×4** — the lock: **no Pokémon with a Rule Box can use Abilities**. Shuts off
  Mew's Fusion Strike System, Palkia/Urshifu's Inteleon, Bibarel — a game-warping Stadium. You
  time your own Ability use around it.
- **4 Double Turbo Energy + 4 Lightning + 3 Fighting + 1 Capture Energy** — **Double Turbo**
  powers Arceus's Trinity Nova / attackers cheaply; Lightning/Fighting for Pikachu/Decidueye;
  **Capture Energy** doubles as a Pokémon-search on attach.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Quick Ball / Lumineon V** to set up; get an **Arceus V** down with Energy. If you
  have Bibarel/Crobat, draw *before* you'll want Path to the Peak.
- **Turn 2:** **Arceus VSTAR → Trinity Nova (200 + accelerate)**, ideally dropping **Path to the
  Peak** to lock the opponent while your board is set.

## The core loop
**Starbirth** for the exact 2 cards you need → **Trinity Nova** for 200 + Energy acceleration onto
your next attacker → drop **Path to the Peak** to shut off their engine → close with **Flying
Pikachu VMAX** (into Water decks) or **Hisuian Decidueye VSTAR**. **Boss's Orders** picks the KO;
**Marnie** keeps them disrupted under the lock. You out-consistency and out-control the field.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Sequence around your own Path to the Peak** — draw with Bibarel/Crobat *before* Path is down,
  then lock; under the lock you rely on Supporters (Marnie/Research/Lumineon).
- **Starbirth for the missing piece** — it's once per game; use it to guarantee a key turn
  (the KO enabler or the second attacker).
- **Trinity Nova sets up the next attacker** — always accelerate to a benched Colorless V so you
  stream attacks.
- **Path to the Peak vs. Ability decks is the game** — establish it against Mew/Palkia/Urshifu
  and win the Stadium war.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fighting (Arceus is Fighting-Weak)** — Fighting attackers hit Arceus for Weakness.
- **Non-Ability decks** — Path to the Peak does nothing to decks that don't rely on Abilities;
  you have to out-attack them straight up.
- **Your own Ability reliance** — Bibarel/Crobat draw is off under your own Path; a mis-sequenced
  turn strands you (Supporter-light hands hurt).
- **Fast OHKO decks (Mew VMAX if it sets up first)** — can race before the lock takes hold.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Trinity Nova: **200 + attach 3 basic Energy from deck** to your Colorless V (3 Energy cost).
- Starbirth (VSTAR Power): search **any 2 cards** (once per game).
- Path to the Peak: **Rule-Box Pokémon can't use Abilities** (both players).
- Industrious Incisors (Bibarel): **draw until 5** (off under your own Path).
