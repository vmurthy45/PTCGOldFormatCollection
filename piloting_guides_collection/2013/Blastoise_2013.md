# How to Pilot — Blastoise (2013)

_Format: NXD-on / BW-era (2013 Worlds format). This exact 60 is essentially James
Good's 3rd-place 2013 World Championship list._

## One-line identity
A Stage-2 "toolbox" deck: get **Blastoise** out fast, use its **Deluge** Ability to
dump a pile of Water Energy onto an attacker in a single turn, and take
knockouts with **Keldeo-EX** (scaling damage) or **Black Kyurem-EX** (flat 200).

## The cards that matter (your engine)
- **Blastoise (31/149)** — Ability **Deluge**: attach as many Water Energy as you
  like from your hand each turn. This is the whole deck. It's a Stage 2, so you run
  4 and lean on Rare Candy to skip Wartortle.
- **Squirtle (29/149)** — the base you want on the bench turn 1.
- **Keldeo-EX (49/149)** — **Secret Sword** scales with Energy attached; ~7 Energy
  1HKOs most Basic EXs. **Rush In** (Ability) lets it swap into the Active spot for
  free — your pivot/retreat button.
- **Black Kyurem-EX (95/135)** — **Black Ballista**: 200 damage for WWLC, discarding
  3 Energy on use. 1HKOs virtually every EX in the format, but is totally reliant on
  Blastoise to re-fuel.
- **Water Energy ×10 / Lightning Energy ×2** — Lightning exists purely to pay the
  `L` in Black Ballista's cost.

## Support you'll lean on
- **Rare Candy ×4 + Ultra Ball ×4** — the Squirtle → Blastoise shortcut.
- **Tropical Beach ×4** — Stadium; on a turn you can't do much, draw up to 7. This is
  the consistency backbone for setup turns.
- **Skyla ×4** — search any Trainer; grab the Rare Candy, Ultra Ball, or Catcher you
  need to lock in a turn-2 Blastoise.
- **Superior Energy Retrieval ×4** — return 4 basic Energy from discard to hand;
  this is what keeps Black Ballista firing every turn.
- **N ×4 / Professor Juniper ×3 / Colress ×2** — draw. N doubles as disruption; the
  deck is on the slower side, so N against faster decks is strong.
- **Pokémon Catcher ×3** — drag up a weak/benched target for a knockout. Often the
  literal wins-the-game card.
- **Computer Search (ACE SPEC) ×1 / Heavy Ball ×1 / Level Ball ×1** — extra search.

## The game plan (turn by turn)

**Turn 1 (ideal):**
- Bench a **Squirtle** (and ideally a second). Play **Tropical Beach** to draw 7 if
  you have nothing better. Aim to end the turn with the pieces for Blastoise next
  turn (Rare Candy + Blastoise, or an Ultra Ball / Skyla to find them).

**Turn 2 (the key turn):**
- **Rare Candy** the Squirtle straight into **Blastoise**. Now Deluge is online.
- Load your attacker: dump Water Energy from hand onto **Keldeo-EX** (for Secret
  Sword) or a **Black Kyurem-EX** (for Black Ballista).
- Start attacking. Black Ballista only needs ~4 Energy to 1HKO an EX, which is why
  it's the faster kill; Keldeo wants ~7 for the same.

**Turn 3 onward (the loop):**
- Attack → **Superior Energy Retrieval** to pull the discarded Energy back → Deluge
  it onto the next attacker → attack again. That's the rhythm.
- Use **Keldeo's Rush In** to pivot a damaged/exhausted attacker to the bench and
  promote a fresh one without paying retreat.
- Use **Pokémon Catcher** to pick off a benched EX (or a low-HP retreater) for a
  clean prize when you can't 1HKO the Active.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Two Blastoise on board is the goal**, not one. If your only Blastoise gets KO'd,
  the energy engine dies — that's how you lose. Set up a backup.
- **Blastoise treats Energy as expendable.** Seeing a Water in your opening hand with
  an Ultra Ball? Discard it — you'll get it back with SER. Setup > hoarding.
- **Black Kyurem for EXs, Keldeo for the rest** (and for non-EX attacking, since
  Keldeo/Blastoise itself doesn't give up two prizes as easily as the EXs do).
- Don't be shy with N when you're behind or when the opponent is comboing off.

## Weaknesses to respect
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** shuts off Abilities — including Deluge. If they land it
  with a Tool attached, your whole engine stops. (Historically the reason lists
  carried Tool Scrapper; this build doesn't, so play around it: race, or force them
  to remove the Tool.)
- **Slow starts / lone Blastoise.** Prize a Blastoise or whiff the turn-2 evolution
  and you can fall badly behind.
- **Weakness:** Black Kyurem-EX is Dragon-Weak; opposing Dragons can 1HKO it back.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Blastoise setup target: **turn 2** (via Rare Candy).
- Black Ballista: **200 dmg**, cost **WWLC**, discard **3 Energy**.
- Secret Sword: **~7 Energy** to 1HKO a Basic EX.
- Prize math: you have EX attackers (worth 2 prizes to the opponent), so avoid
  handing over easy multi-prize KOs — pivot with Rush In.
