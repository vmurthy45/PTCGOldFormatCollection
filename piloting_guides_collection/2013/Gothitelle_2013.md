# How to Pilot — Gothitelle (2013)

_Format: HGSS/BW-on 2013 (Boundaries Crossed–Plasma era). This build closely mirrors
Edmund Kuras's US Nationals 2013-**winning** Accelgor/Gothitelle list._

## One-line identity
A lock deck: **Gothitelle** stops the opponent from playing Item cards while
**Accelgor** (copied endlessly by **Mew-EX**) paralyzes and poisons their Active every
turn. Set it up and the opponent can't retreat, can't use Items, and can't attack —
you win at your leisure while chipping them down.

## The meta it lived in
2013 was full of Item-reliant setup decks (Blastoise, RayEels, Plasma, Darkrai), all
of which fold if their Items get shut off early. Gothitelle exploited exactly that. It
was a Nationals-winning, Worlds-relevant deck — feared enough that some players teched
specifically against it. Its kryptonite was Keldeo-EX's Rush In (the one clean out to
paralysis + item lock).

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Gothitelle (47/98) ×3** (3-1-3 line w/ Gothita ×4, Gothorita ×1) — Ability
  **Magic Room**: while it's Active, neither player can play Item cards. This is the
  lock. It has to sit Active and take hits, so you protect it with Float Stone (free
  retreat) and keep spares.
- **Accelgor (11/108) ×2** (from Shelmet ×3) — **Deck and Cover**: 10 damage, paralyze
  + poison, then shuffle Accelgor (and itself) back into the deck. Paralysis every
  turn = they never attack.
- **Mew-EX (46/124) ×2** — **Versatile**: copies Accelgor's Deck and Cover, then Mew
  shuffles *itself* back in (better than shuffling Accelgor, since Mew is a Basic and
  Accelgor is an evolution you'd have to rebuild). This is the loop's heart.
- **Dusknoir (63/149)** (1-0-1 via Rare Candy) — **Sinister Hand**: move damage
  counters. Pull damage off the opponent's Active so it can't die/leave (staying
  locked), and pile it onto benched Pokémon for KOs. Enables the "un-loseable" lock.
- **Musharna (59/99)** (1-1) — **Forewarn**-style draw to keep the DCE + Mew loop fed.
- **Keldeo-EX (49/149)** — utility retreat / a Water attacker vs. mirror-ish spots.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Rare Candy ×4** — jump Gothita → Gothitelle (and Duskull → Dusknoir) a turn
  faster; hitting **T2–3 Gothitelle** is the whole game.
- **Skyla ×4 / Ultra Ball ×3 / Level Ball ×2 / Pokémon Communication ×2** — heavy
  search to assemble the multi-piece lock (2 Stage-2s + Stage-1s).
- **Float Stone ×3** — free retreat for Gothitelle so you can send Mew out to attack
  and pull Goth back Active each turn.
- **Tropical Beach ×2** — Stadium; draw 7 on setup turns you can't do much else.
- **N ×3 / Juniper ×4 / Colress ×2** — draw; N also resets the opponent to a small
  hand while they're locked (with Dusknoir keeping the loop, you can N them repeatedly).
- **Tool Scrapper ×1** — strip a Float Stone/Dark Claw (or shut off Garbodor's Tool);
  after a Goth is up they can't replay Tools anyway.
- **Super Rod / Town Map / Computer Search / Pokémon Catcher ×3** — recovery, prize
  fixing, and dragging the right target under the lock.
- **DCE ×4** — the only Energy; powers Deck and Cover via Mew.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** bench Gothita + Shelmet; ideally Tropical Beach, Juniper, drop your
  Basics. Use Mew or Shelmet so you have a way out of the Active spot.
- **Turn 2 (critical):** Rare Candy → **Gothitelle** and get Magic Room online.
  Getting Goth up T2 shields your fragile benched evolutions from Catcher/Laser.

## The core loop
With Goth + Float Stone Active, each turn: retreat Goth free, promote a Mew, **Deck and
Cover** (paralyze/poison, shuffle Mew back), then Goth returns Active — so the opponent
is Item-locked *and* paralyzed permanently. Use **Dusknoir** to move their Active's
damage away (so it can't be KO'd out of the lock) and dump it on their bench. Add **N**
to keep their hand empty. That's the "un-loseable" state: Goth + Float Stone, Dusknoir,
Musharna, Accelgor, Mew/DCE, and all their N in the discard.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **T2–3 Gothitelle is the priority** above all else — the lock protects the rest of
  your slow setup.
- **Shuffle Mew, not Accelgor**, when you have the choice (keep Accelgor rebuilt on the
  bench; Mew re-loops as a Basic).
- **Don't waste Float Stone on the wrong Pokémon** (e.g. a stuck Munna/Duskull start) —
  guard your Goth's free retreat.
- Against **Keldeo-EX** (the main out), get Goth up fast and Catcher + Deck-and-Cover
  loop it to death before it can Rush In and reset.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Keldeo-EX / Rush In** — the clean answer to paralysis + item lock; respect it.
- **Donks / bad starts** — the deck is slow and setup-reliant; a Munna/Duskull opening
  or a prized key piece can cost the game before the lock lands.
- **Fast decks that race the setup** (Plasma T2 Blizzard Burn, aggressive Blastoise)
  can KO your Duskull/Goth pieces before Magic Room is stable.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Magic Room (Gothitelle): **no Item cards** for either player while it's Active.
- Deck and Cover (Accelgor, copied by Mew): **10 + paralyze + poison**, shuffle back.
- Lock target: **T2–3 Gothitelle**.
- Energy: **4 DCE only**.
