# Raging Bolt (2025)

*Raging Bolt ex / Teal Mask Ogerpon ex — Yuya Okita's Tera-Ancient toolbox. 2025 Standard (JTG/BLK-on, the "ex" era).*

> **New to this deck? Read this first.**
> Raging Bolt is a **one-shot nuke deck**. Its whole plan is to pile Grass Energy onto **Raging Bolt ex**, then fire **Bellowing Thunder**, which *discards all of that Energy at once* to hit for **70 damage per Energy** — three Energy is 210, four is 280, five is 350. Because the attack empties the tank every time, the deck is built to **re-load Energy every single turn** so you can nuke again next turn. The refueling machine is **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex**, whose Ability attaches a Grass Energy from hand *and* draws you a card for free each turn; you then shuttle that Grass onto Raging Bolt with **Energy Switch** and **Professor Sada's Vitality** (which also heals). So each turn you do the same thing: draw and attach with Ogerpon, move Energy onto Raging Bolt, and swing for a huge number. **Noctowl** is the glue that fetches the Trainers you need to keep the loop running.

---

## One-line identity

A Tera-Ancient turbo deck that reloads Grass Energy every turn to fire **70-per-Energy** one-shots with Raging Bolt ex, backstopped by Noctowl's Trainer-searching consistency.

## The meta it lived in

2025 Standard's top tier was **Gardevoir ex** (Worlds Masters champion), **Dragapult ex / Dusknoir**, **Gholdengo ex**, and **Raging Bolt ex**. Raging Bolt was the format's premier **hit-hard-and-fast** option: it doesn't grind, it *removes* threats with 280–350 one-shots before they set up. Yuya Okita's **Raging Bolt / Teal Mask Ogerpon** build (per the Coded Yellow Worlds deck-product writeup) is the reference list — it leans on the Tera engine (Area Zero Underdepths bench expansion, Ogerpon acceleration) and Noctowl for consistency.

## The engine — exactly how Bellowing Thunder works (read carefully)

**Raging Bolt ex — Bellowing Thunder (attack):** *Discard all Energy from this Pokémon. This attack does 70 damage for each Energy card you discarded.*
- 3 Energy = **210**, 4 = **280**, 5 = **350**. It scales with how much you've loaded.
- The catch: it **discards everything from Raging Bolt**, so after every Bellowing Thunder your attacker is empty. The entire deck exists to refill it before your next turn.

**Raging Bolt ex — Thunderburst Storm (attack):** does damage **for each Energy attached** but does **NOT discard** the Energy. This is the lower-commitment option: when you want to chip a target while *keeping* your board loaded, you use Thunderburst Storm; when you want the full nuke, you use Bellowing Thunder and dump it all. The two attacks work together — chip and hold, or empty the tank.

**The worked example:** You've loaded 4 Grass onto Raging Bolt ex over two turns (Ogerpon + Sada + Energy Switch). You fire **Bellowing Thunder**: discard all 4 → **280 damage**, one-shotting a Gardevoir ex or Dragapult ex. Raging Bolt is now empty — but **Professor Sada's Vitality** next turn attaches 2 back, **Ogerpon + Energy Switch** add more, and **Night Stretcher** recovers any discarded Energy, so you're reloaded to nuke again.

## The reload machine

- **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex — Teal Dance (Ability):** Once during your turn, **attach a Grass Energy from your hand to Ogerpon**, then **draw a card.** Acceleration *and* card draw in a single free Ability — this is the heartbeat of the deck. You use it every turn.
- **Energy Switch (Item):** move a Basic Energy from one of your Pokémon to another — specifically, shuttle the Grass Ogerpon just attached **onto Raging Bolt ex**.
- **Professor Sada's Vitality (Supporter):** attach **2 Basic Energy from hand to your Ancient Pokémon** and **heal 40 damage** from them. Raging Bolt ex and Koraidon are **Ancient**, so this is your primary acceleration *and* it keeps the attacker out of KO range. (4 copies — it's the most important Supporter.)
- **Crispin (Supporter):** search your deck for **2 Basic Energy of different types**, attach 1 to a Pokémon and put the other in hand. Fixes/accelerates when Sada isn't in hand.
- **Night Stretcher** (3 copies): recover a Pokémon **or** a Basic Energy from discard → hand. Refuels the Energy you keep discarding with Bellowing Thunder.

## Consistency — the Noctowl engine

**Noctowl — Jewel Seeker (Ability):** *Once during your turn, when you play this Pokémon from your hand to evolve 1 of your Pokémon, if you have any Tera Pokémon in play, you may search your deck for up to 2 Trainer cards, reveal them, and put them into your hand.*
- The trigger has **two conditions**: (1) you're **evolving Hoothoot → Noctowl this turn**, and (2) you have a **Tera Pokémon in play**. Ogerpon ex and Raging Bolt ex are **Tera**, so the condition is almost always met once you're set up.
- Reward: **search 2 Trainers** — usually Professor Sada's Vitality, Energy Switch, or ball/recovery — the exact pieces that keep the reload loop flowing. **Hoothoot (114/142)** is the Basic you start; you run 3 Hoothoot / 3 Noctowl to fire this repeatedly.
- **Gotcha:** Jewel Seeker only fires on the *evolution turn*. Playing Noctowl from hand onto an already-in-play Hoothoot is what triggers it — you can't re-use an already-evolved Noctowl for it.

## Setup & tech (why each count)

- **Fan Rotom — Fan Call (attack, typically Turn 1):** benches multiple Basic Colorless/support Pokémon at once, building your board fast. **Fan Rotom + Squawkabilly ex is the ideal turn-1 setup.** (2 copies.)
- **Squawkabilly ex — Squawk and Seize (Ability):** turn-1 discard-your-hand-and-draw-5 to dig for your setup pieces; also a Colorless attacker in a pinch.
- **Koraidon** — Ancient support/attacker; benefits from Sada's Vitality acceleration.
- **Raging Bolt (111/142, non-ex)** — a **single-prize** attacker option; useful when you want to attack without giving up 2 prizes on a KO.
- **Ditto (132/165)** — flexible evolution filler: via its rule it can stand in as an evolving Basic to smooth lines.
- **Latias ex — Skyliner (Ability):** your Basic Pokémon retreat for free — the pivot.
- **Fezandipiti ex — Flip the Script (Ability):** after one of your Pokémon was KO'd last turn, draw 3 — bounce-back draw.
- **Area Zero Underdepths (Stadium):** lets **Tera Pokémon expand your Bench to 8**, giving room for extra Ogerpon/attackers/support. A core Tera-deck engine. (2 copies.)
- **Jamming Tower (Stadium):** turns off **both players' Pokémon Tools** — disruption vs. Tool-reliant decks; also contests Underdepths.
- **4 Nest Ball / 4 Ultra Ball / 3 Earthen Vessel** — search density: Nest Ball for Basics, Ultra Ball for anything, Earthen Vessel for Basic Energy.
- **2 Boss's Orders** — gust the KO that finishes the prize race.
- **1 Iono** — hand disruption + refresh when you're ahead on prizes.
- **1 Professor Turo's Scenario** — pick up a damaged/liability Pokémon of yours **without giving up a prize**.
- **1 Energy Switch** — the Ogerpon-to-Raging-Bolt shuttle.
- **1 Prime Catcher (ACE SPEC)** — free gust; saved for the lethal turn.

## Ideal early game

**Turn 1:** You want the board wide and the hand deep. Ideal: **Squawkabilly ex → Squawk and Seize** (dump a clunky hand, draw fresh) and/or **Fan Rotom → Fan Call** to bench multiple support Basics (Hoothoot, Ogerpon, a second attacker). Get **Teal Mask Ogerpon ex** down so Teal Dance can start every turn. Drop **Area Zero Underdepths** if you need the bench space.

**Turn 2:** Begin the loop. **Teal Dance** (attach Grass to Ogerpon + draw). Evolve **Hoothoot → Noctowl** with a Tera Pokémon in play → **Jewel Seeker** for 2 Trainers (grab Sada / Energy Switch). **Sada's Vitality** loads 2 Energy onto Raging Bolt ex; **Energy Switch** moves Ogerpon's Grass over. Now you likely have 3 on Raging Bolt → **Bellowing Thunder for 210**, and you're set to escalate.

## The core loop

1. **Teal Dance** (Ogerpon: attach Grass + draw) — every turn.
2. **Noctowl (Jewel Seeker)** on evolution turns → fetch the 2 Trainers that keep it flowing.
3. **Sada's Vitality / Energy Switch / Crispin** move and add Energy onto **Raging Bolt ex** (Sada also heals 40).
4. **Bellowing Thunder** — discard it all for **70 × N** — one-shot the threat.
5. **Night Stretcher / Sada** reload the discarded Energy for next turn → repeat.

## The core combo (express clearly)

**Fan Rotom / Squawkabilly** turn-1 board → **Teal Dance** each turn (Grass on Ogerpon + draw) → **Energy Switch / Sada's Vitality** move+add that Energy onto **Raging Bolt ex** → **Bellowing Thunder** discards it all for a 210–350 one-shot on almost anything → **Night Stretcher / Sada** reload for the next nuke. **Noctowl** continuously fetches the Trainers (Sada, Energy Switch, recovery) that keep the machine fed. The deck's power is that it can do this **every turn from turn 2**, out-tempoing setup decks.

## Priorities / rules of thumb

- **Fire Teal Dance every turn** — free Energy + a card is the engine; never skip it.
- **Only evolve into Noctowl when a Tera Pokémon is in play** — otherwise Jewel Seeker whiffs. Keep an Ogerpon or Raging Bolt ex out before you evolve.
- **Choose the right attack:** Bellowing Thunder to *one-shot and reset*; Thunderburst Storm to *chip while keeping Energy* when a full dump isn't lethal and you'd rather stay loaded.
- **Use the non-ex Raging Bolt (111/142) to deny prizes** — against decks racing you, a single-prize attacker changes the prize math.
- **Sada's heal 40 matters** — it can drag Raging Bolt back out of an opponent's KO range, buying you the turn you need to nuke.
- **Bench math with Area Zero Underdepths** — expanding to 8 lets you keep spare Ogerpon/attackers so a gust-KO doesn't stall your loop.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups

- **Stall / control** — the deck must keep discarding and reloading Energy; if it's denied Energy or forced to whiff a reload, the loop stalls. Control decks that blunt your one-shot cadence are its worst matchup.
- **Gardevoir ex** — noted as a tough matchup; Gard grinds and its Munkidori-fueled spread can pick off your **Hoothoot** before it becomes Noctowl, choking your consistency engine.
- **Spread damage (Dragapult ex / Munkidori)** targets your low-HP setup Basics (Hoothoot, Ogerpon) before they contribute.
- **Energy denial** hurts disproportionately because every Bellowing Thunder empties the tank — anything that slows the refill slows your whole plan.
- **Weakness exposure** — mind type Weakness on your attackers when choosing what to send Active.

## Quick reference numbers

- **Bellowing Thunder:** 70 × (Energy discarded from Raging Bolt ex). 3 = 210, 4 = 280, 5 = 350.
- **Thunderburst Storm:** damage per Energy attached, **no discard** (keep your board loaded).
- **Teal Dance:** attach 1 Grass from hand to Ogerpon + **draw 1**, every turn.
- **Sada's Vitality:** +2 Energy to an Ancient Pokémon and **heal 40** (Raging Bolt ex & Koraidon are Ancient).
- **Noctowl — Jewel Seeker:** on evolve, with a **Tera** in play, search **2 Trainers**.
- **Area Zero Underdepths:** Tera Pokémon bench expands to **8**.
- **Energy base:** 6 Grass / 3 Fighting / 3 Lightning.
