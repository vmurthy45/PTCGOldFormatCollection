# How to Pilot — Dragapult (2025)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet Standard 2025 (JTG/BLK-on). The **Dragapult ex / Dusknoir**
"Cursed Blast" build — the 2025 World Championship **finalist** archetype (2nd place,
Justin Newdorf/Jose Cruz Galindo-Resendiz), and one of the most balanced, skill-rewarding
decks in the format._

> **New to this deck? Read this first.** Dragapult is a *spread-damage prize-mapping* deck.
> Its attack (Phantom Dive) does 200 to the Active **and** lets you drop **6 damage counters
> anywhere on their bench** — so every turn you're doing two jobs at once: taking a KO up
> front *and* pre-damaging benched Pokémon for future KOs. **Dusknoir** then converts that
> spread into extra knockouts by dropping **13 damage counters** on a single Pokémon on
> demand. The skill is *sequencing*: placing counters this turn so that next turn (or the
> same turn, with Dusknoir) you take **multiple prizes at once**. This guide walks through
> the counter math so you can plan those swing turns.

## One-line identity
A Ghost/Dragon spread deck: **Dragapult ex's Phantom Dive** does **200 to the Active + 6
damage counters spread** on the opponent's bench, softening their board while you take KOs;
**Dusknoir's Cursed Blast** then places **13 damage counters** on one Pokémon (a manual KO on
almost anything) to convert the spread into multi-prize turns. **Drakloak's Recon Directive**
draws you through the deck. Fast, flexible, and disruptive.

## The meta it lived in
2025 Standard's top tier was Gardevoir ex, Dragapult ex, Gholdengo ex, Raging Bolt ex.
Dragapult/Dusknoir was prized for being **the most balanced deck in the format** — "almost no
completely unfavorable matchups," bringing most games close to 50-50. It excels at **punishing
wide boards of low-HP support Pokémon** (Gardevoir's Ralts, Lost Zone Comfey) by wiping their
engine with spread. Its trouble is **single high-HP attackers** (Gholdengo ex) that Phantom
Dive's 200 can't 1HKO — that's exactly what Dusknoir is there to fix.

## The engine — exactly how the damage works (read carefully)
1. **Dragapult ex — Phantom Dive (attack):** *"200 damage. Put 6 damage counters on your
   opponent's Benched Pokémon in any way you like."* So each attack = **200 to the Active** (a
   KO on most 2-prize ex Pokémon) **plus 6 counters (60 damage) spread however you choose**
   across their bench. You use those 6 counters to (a) snipe a low-HP Basic (e.g. a 70-HP
   Dreepy/Ralts before it evolves), or (b) **pre-load damage** on a benched threat so you can
   finish it next turn.
2. **Dusknoir — Cursed Blast (Ability):** *"Once during your turn (before your attack), you may
   put 13 damage counters on 1 of your opponent's Pokémon. Then, this Pokémon [Dusknoir] is
   Knocked Out."* 13 counters = **130 damage** placed instantly on any one Pokémon — enough to
   finish a big benched target *or* combine with Phantom Dive's spread for a huge multi-KO turn.
   **Dusknoir KOs itself** when you use it (you give up a prize), so time it when the payoff is
   worth it.
3. **Dusclops — Cursed Blast (Ability):** the Stage-1 version places **5 damage counters** (50)
   and also KOs itself — a smaller, earlier version you can use while building toward Dusknoir.
4. **Munkidori (95/167) ×1 — Adrena-Brain:** move up to 3 counters (if it has Darkness) — here
   it's used to **nudge damage** onto exactly the right Pokémon to line up a KO, or to heal a
   counter off your own board.

**The core skill:** count HP. Phantom Dive's 6 counters + Dusknoir's 13 + attack damage let
you pre-map knockouts. Example: Phantom Dive 200 KOs their Active *and* drops 6 counters onto
a benched 130-HP ex (now at 60 damage); next turn Dusknoir's 13 counters (130) finishes it —
two ex KOs across two turns, or one giant turn if you have both online.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Dreepy (128/167) ×4 → Drakloak (129/167) ×4 → Dragapult ex (130/167) ×3** — the core.
  **Drakloak's Recon Directive** (Ability): look at top 2, put 1 in hand and 1 on the bottom —
  smooth draw/dig every turn (a wide Drakloak bench = lots of digging). **Dragapult ex's
  Phantom Dive** (above) is your engine attack. **Note:** this build **cuts Rare Candy to a
  single copy** because **Budew** (below) can shut Candy off — you mostly evolve Dreepy →
  Drakloak → Dragapult the natural way, which the Drakloak draw supports.
- **Duskull (18/64) ×2 → Dusclops (19/64) ×2 → Dusknoir (20/64) ×1** — the finisher line.
  **Cursed Blast** (Dusknoir 13 / Dusclops 5, above). You build this over a few turns; against
  slower decks (Gardevoir/Dragapult mirror) you set it up patiently.
- **Budew (4/131) ×2** — **Itchy Pollen** (Ability): while Budew is your Active, **neither
  player can play Item cards**. A turn-1 **Item lock** that cripples setup decks (no Ultra Ball/
  Rare Candy/Nest Ball for them) — a huge tempo tool. It's a Basic you lead with.

## The tech attackers / support (why the 1-ofs)
- **Genesect (40/64) ×1** — a support/attacker tech (draw or a specific attack) — flexibility.
- **Hawlucha (118/198) ×1** — **Flying Entry**: chip the bench for free — combos with Phantom
  Dive's spread to reach KO counts on benched Pokémon.
- **Munkidori (95/167) ×1** — Adrena-Brain (above): fine-tune counter placement for exact KOs /
  heal a counter off your board (needs a Darkness Energy — hence Luminous/Neo Upper flexibility).
- **Fezandipiti ex (38/64) ×1** — **Flip the Script**: draw 3 after a KO last turn — comeback
  draw.

## Support / tech (why the counts)
- **Iono ×4 / Professor's Research ×3 / Boss's Orders ×2 / Brock's Scouting ×1 / Hilda ×1** —
  draw + disruption + gust. **Iono** disrupts + refuels (great when ahead); **Boss's Orders**
  drags the target you want to Phantom-Dive/Cursed-Blast; **Hilda** is a newer draw Supporter
  that smooths setup.
- **Buddy-Buddy Poffin ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Nest Ball (via Poffin role) / Rare Candy ×1** —
  **Buddy-Buddy Poffin** benches two ≤70-HP Basics (Dreepy/Duskull) — the key setup Item;
  Ultra Ball searches + discards.
- **Counter Catcher ×3 / Night Stretcher ×3** — free gust when behind; recover Pokémon/Energy
  (recur your lines).
- **Jamming Tower ×1 / Air Balloon ×2** — **Jamming Tower** (Stadium) turns off both players'
  Tools (disruption; note you then can't use your own Tools); **Air Balloon** gives free retreat
  to pivot Dragapult/Budew.
- **Psychic Energy ×2 + Fire Energy ×1 + Luminous Energy ×3 + Neo Upper Energy ×1 (ACE SPEC)** —
  Dragapult's Phantom Dive costs Psychic + Fire; **Luminous Energy** provides any 1 type (fixes
  costs); **Neo Upper Energy** (ACE SPEC) provides 2 Energy of any type to an evolved Pokémon —
  a fast way to arm Dragapult.

## Ideal early game (turn by turn)
- **Turn 1:** ideally lead **Budew** Active (**Itchy Pollen** = Item lock) to strangle a setup
  deck, while you **Buddy-Buddy Poffin** out Dreepy/Duskull. Evolve toward Drakloak.
- **Turn 2:** get a **Dragapult ex** online (natural evolution, or Rare Candy if Budew isn't
  needed) and **Phantom Dive** — 200 to the Active + place the 6 counters where they set up
  future KOs (snipe a low-HP Basic, or pre-damage a benched ex).

## The core loop (every turn once set up)
1. **Phantom Dive**: KO the Active (200) *and* place 6 counters to snipe/soften their bench.
2. **Recon Directive** (each Drakloak) to dig for your next piece.
3. When the payoff is there, **Cursed Blast** (Dusknoir 13 / Dusclops 5) to finish a benched
   threat or set up a **multi-prize turn** — remember Dusknoir KOs itself (give up 1 prize, take
   2+).
4. **Munkidori** to nudge counters for the exact KO; **Boss's Orders/Counter Catcher** to drag
   the target Active; **Iono** to disrupt when ahead.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Sequence the counters** — the whole skill is placing Phantom Dive's 6 (and Dusknoir's 13)
  so you take **multiple prizes over the next turn or two**. Always ask: "where do these
  counters set up a KO?"
- **Budew turn 1 vs. setup decks** — Item-locking Gardevoir/Gholdengo on turn 1 can win the game
  before it starts; lead Budew when you see a setup deck.
- **Watch the opponent's prize count with Cursed Blast** — Dusknoir KOs itself, giving up a
  prize; if the opponent is close to winning, that extra prize can hand them the game. Don't
  Cursed Blast into your own loss.
- **Phantom Dive the *right* Stage 2** — in the mirror, the player who attacks the opponent's
  Stage 2 (not just the Active) first is often ahead; snipe their Drakloaks/setup.
- **Air Balloon to pivot** — free-retreat Dragapult/Budew so you're never stuck Active.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Straight Dragapult (mirror)** — the non-Dusknoir version is a documented unfavorable matchup
  (they're faster; you committed deck space to the Dusknoir line).
- **Single high-HP attackers (Gholdengo ex)** — Phantom Dive's 200 can't 1HKO them; you *must*
  lean on Dusknoir/Munkidori math to win the prize race (slightly unfavorable).
- **Dragon Weakness / Lillie's Clefairy** — Gardevoir's Lillie's Clefairy makes its Psychic
  attacks 1HKO your Dragapult (double damage to Dragons); a real threat.
- **N's Zoroark ex** — slightly unfavorable (around 40-60), but more winnable than Gardevoir's.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Phantom Dive: **200 to the Active + 6 damage counters (60) spread** on their bench.
- Cursed Blast: **Dusknoir 13 counters (130)** / **Dusclops 5 counters (50)** on 1 Pokémon —
  then that Pokémon KOs **itself**.
- Recon Directive (Drakloak): look at top 2, **1 to hand, 1 to bottom** (draw/dig).
- Itchy Pollen (Budew): **both players can't play Items** while Budew is Active.
