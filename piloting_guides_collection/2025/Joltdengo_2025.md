# Joltdengo (2025)

*Gholdengo ex / Joltik Box hybrid — "An Unlikely Alliance." 2025 Standard (JTG/BLK-on, the "ex" era). Won the 2025 World Championship Seniors division.*

> **New to this deck? Read this first.**
> Joltdengo is two decks stapled together. One half is a **Joltik Box** — a swarm of cheap single-attack Basics (Iron Hands ex, Miraidon ex, Pikachu ex) that you power up instantly with Joltik's energy-search attack and use to take fast, opportunistic prizes early. The other half is **Gholdengo**, a late-game hammer whose attack, Make It Rain, hits for 50 damage per Basic Energy you throw away from your hand — so the whole deck is built to *hoard Basic Energy in hand*, then dump it for a 200–300 nuke. Each turn you're doing one of two things: (1) setting up and swinging with a Box attacker while stockpiling Energy, or (2) once you have Gholdengo ex out and a fat hand of Energy, unloading Make It Rain to close games. The magic trick that ties it together: Energy you spend on Iron Hands early comes *back* to your hand (via Superior Energy Retrieval) and becomes Make It Rain damage later. Same Energy, two jobs.

---

## One-line identity

A hybrid toolbox that has one of the format's **best early games** (Joltik Box) welded to one of its **best late games** (Gholdengo), letting it apply pressure from turn 2 and still have a lethal top-end.

## The meta it lived in

2025 Standard was the mature "ex" format. The top tier was **Gardevoir ex** (the eventual Worlds Masters winner, Riley McKay), **Dragapult ex / Dusknoir**, **Gholdengo ex**, and **Raging Bolt ex**. Straight Gholdengo was the **most-played Day 1 deck** at Worlds (~22%, climbing to ~30% Day 2) but none of its pilots cracked Top 4 in Masters — the pure list was consistent but linear. The **Joltik Box hybrid**, by contrast, took the **Seniors division title** (Gabriel Semedo, per PokéBeach): grafting the Box's explosive early game onto Gholdengo's late game fixed the pure deck's biggest weakness — falling behind early.

## The engine — exactly how Make It Rain works (read carefully)

Everything in this deck orbits **Gholdengo ex** and the goal of keeping your hand stuffed with Basic Energy.

**Gholdengo ex — Coin Bonus (Ability):** Once during your turn you draw a card. **If Gholdengo ex is in the Active Spot, draw 2 instead.** With 3 copies of Gholdengo ex, at least one Coin Bonus fires most turns, keeping your hand full so you always have Energy and options.

**Gholdengo ex — Make It Rain (attack):** *Discard any number of Basic Energy cards from your hand. This attack does 50 damage for each card you discarded.*
- 4 Energy discarded = **200 damage** (KOs most ex Basics).
- 5 Energy = **250**; 6 = **300** (KOs the fattest attackers).
- Crucially, the Energy is discarded **from your hand, not from Gholdengo** — Gholdengo itself never needs Energy attached to attack. That's why the deck is a Basic-Energy stockpiling machine.

**The worked example:** You have 5 Basic Energy in hand and Gholdengo ex active. You attack: discard all 5 → **250 damage**, one-shotting almost anything. Next turn, **Superior Energy Retrieval** pulls up to 4 of those Energy back from the discard, and Coin Bonus + Gimmighoul refill the rest — so you can do it again.

## The stockpiling + refuel loop

- **Gimmighoul (SSP 97) — Minor Errand-Running (attack):** Search your deck for **2 Basic Energy** and put them in your hand. This both thins your deck and loads the hand for Make It Rain. (This is the specific SSP "Minor Errand-Running" print — 4 copies.)
- **Superior Energy Retrieval (Item):** Discard 2 other cards from your hand, then **return up to 4 Basic Energy from your discard pile to your hand.** This is the refuel button — after you dump Energy for Make It Rain (or spend it on Iron Hands), this brings it right back. 4 copies because the whole engine depends on recycling.
- **Earthen Vessel:** discard 1 card, search 2 Basic Energy → hand. More hand-loading. (2 copies.)

## Consistency — the Genesect line replaces Ultra Ball

**Genesect ex — Metallic Signal (Ability):** When you play Genesect ex from hand to your Bench, search your deck for **up to 2 Metal Evolution Pokémon** and put them in your hand. **Gholdengo ex is a Metal Stage-1**, so Metallic Signal pulls Gholdengo ex straight out of the deck. This is why the list runs **no Ultra Ball** — Genesect *is* your Gholdengo-finder, and it does it without discarding cards. (2 copies.)

## The Box attackers (the toolbox)

- **Joltik (50/142) — Jolting Charge (attack):** Search your deck for **up to 2 Basic Grass Energy and up to 2 Basic Lightning Energy** and attach them to your Pokémon **in any way you like.** This is the acceleration that powers up the toolbox attackers in a single turn. (2 copies; note the deck runs 4 Grass + 4 Lightning + 3 Metal Energy precisely so Joltik always has targets.)
- **Iron Hands ex — Amp You Very Much (attack):** 120 damage, **and if this attack Knocks Out a Pokémon, take 1 more Prize card** (so a KO gives you 2 prizes, or 3 on a 2-prize ex). The prize-swinger — Iron Hands is how you steal tempo early.
- **Miraidon ex — Tandem Unit (Ability):** Once during your turn (when you play it from hand), search for **2 Basic Lightning Pokémon** and put them on your Bench — e.g. fetch Joltik plus another attacker to build your board instantly.
- **Pikachu ex (57/191):** enables the Area Zero Underdepths–style bench expansion (more attackers/support on board).
- **Latias ex — Skyliner (Ability):** your Basic Pokémon retreat for free — the pivot that lets you move a spent attacker out and a fresh one in without paying Energy.
- **Fezandipiti ex — Flip the Script (Ability):** once, after one of your Pokémon was Knocked Out last turn, draw 3 cards. Bounce-back draw after you take a hit.

## Support & tech (why each count)

- **4 Arven** — your best Item-tutor turn after turn (grabs Nest Ball + a Tool, or the Item you need).
- **3 Boss's Orders** — gust the opponent's benched liability into the Active for a prize. High count because targeting the right Pokémon wins prize races.
- **2 Brock's Scouting** — draw/search Supporter to smooth setup.
- **1 Team Rocket's Petrel** — disruptive search Supporter (situational hand attack / tutor).
- **1 Professor Turo's Scenario** — return one of *your* damaged or liability Pokémon (a low Genesect ex or Fezandipiti ex) to hand **without giving up a prize**. Great for un-benching a fragile ex before it's KO'd.
- **2 Levincia** (Stadium) — Lightning support Stadium; also contests the opponent's Stadium.
- **4 Nest Ball** — free Basic search; the deck is almost entirely Basics, so this is prime setup.
- **3 Buddy-Buddy Poffin** — bench two low-HP Basics (Joltik, Gimmighoul) at once. Explosive turn-1 setup.
- **2 Pokégear 3.0** — dig for the Supporter you need.
- **1 Prime Catcher (ACE SPEC)** — the free-gust Item; your one-of ACE SPEC, saved for a lethal gust.
- **2 Air Balloon** — free retreat so you can keep **Gholdengo ex active for the 2-card Coin Bonus** and pivot freely.
- **1 Bravery Charm** — +50 HP Tool to push a key attacker out of a KO range.

## Ideal early game

**Turn 1:** Get a Basic (ideally Joltik and/or Gimmighoul) down via **Nest Ball / Buddy-Buddy Poffin**. If you can, play **Gimmighoul → Minor Errand-Running** to bank 2 Basic Energy, or **Joltik → Jolting Charge** to load Energy onto an attacker. Bench **Genesect ex** if you have it (Metallic Signal → grab Gholdengo ex). Aim to have a threat online by turn 2.

**Turn 2:** This is the fork.
- **Aggro line:** **Joltik → attach Grass/Lightning to Iron Hands ex → Amp You Very Much** for 120 and a prize swing. Keep stockpiling Basic Energy in hand.
- **Late-game setup line:** get **Gholdengo ex active**, fire **Coin Bonus** (draw 2), keep hoarding Energy, and start swinging **Make It Rain** once you have 4+ Energy in hand.

## The core loop

1. **Coin Bonus** (draw, 2 if Gholdengo active) + **Gimmighoul / Earthen Vessel** to keep Basic Energy flowing into hand.
2. Take early prizes with **Iron Hands ex** (Joltik-fueled) while the hand fills.
3. When you have 4–6 Basic Energy in hand and Gholdengo ex active, **Make It Rain** for 200–300 to close.
4. **Superior Energy Retrieval** brings the discarded Energy back → repeat.
5. **Boss's Orders / Prime Catcher** to gust the exact KO that finishes the prize race.

## The signature combo (the thing that makes the hybrid work)

**Joltik** searches Energy → attach to **Iron Hands ex** → **Amp You Very Much** takes an early KO (and a bonus prize). When Iron Hands is later Knocked Out, **Superior Energy Retrieval** pulls those *same* Energy cards back to your hand → they now become **Make It Rain damage** on Gholdengo. The Energy does double duty: first it powers Iron Hands, then it *is* Gholdengo's damage. That recycling is why the deck has a strong early **and** late game instead of running out of gas.

## Priorities / rules of thumb

- **Keep Basic Energy in your hand, not on your board.** Gholdengo's damage lives in your hand. Resist over-attaching.
- **Keep Gholdengo ex active when you can** (with Air Balloon to pivot) for the 2-card Coin Bonus draw.
- **Genesect ex, not Ultra Ball, finds Gholdengo.** Plan your Genesect drops around when you need the Stage-1.
- **Use Iron Hands' bonus prize to break parity** — a well-timed Amp You Very Much can put you a prize ahead in a race you'd otherwise lose.
- **Turo's Scenario your own fragile ex** (a low Genesect/Fezandipiti) before the opponent can gust-and-KO it for easy prizes.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups

- **Fire attackers** 1HKO Gholdengo ex cheaply — Gholdengo is Metal and **Fire-Weak**, so a Fire deck trades up on your main late-game piece.
- **Linearity when behind on prizes** — the deck wants to be even or ahead; if it drops the first two prizes it can struggle to stabilize.
- **Toedscruel (Trickened Garden / Energy-lock effects)** can block **Superior Energy Retrieval** and choke the refuel loop.
- **Gardevoir ex late** — hard to reliably KO a well-set Gardevoir line, and Gard's Munkidori-fueled damage can pick off your low-HP Joltik/Gimmighoul before they contribute.

## Quick reference numbers

- **Make It Rain:** 50 × (Basic Energy discarded from hand). 4 = 200, 5 = 250, 6 = 300.
- **Coin Bonus:** draw 1, or **2 if Gholdengo ex is Active**.
- **Iron Hands ex — Amp You Very Much:** 120, **+1 Prize on KO**.
- **Bellowing** targets: aim for **4+ Energy in hand** before committing to a Make It Rain turn.
- **Energy base:** 4 Grass / 4 Lightning / 3 Metal (Joltik wants the Grass + Lightning; Gholdengo eats any Basic).
- **Genesect ex Metallic Signal:** grabs up to **2 Metal Evolution Pokémon** (Gholdengo ex counts).
