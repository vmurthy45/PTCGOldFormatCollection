# How to Pilot — Flutter Devo Gardevoir (2025)

_Format: Scarlet & Violet Standard 2025 (JTG/BLK-on, the "ex" era). This build mirrors the
Munkidori/Scream Tail Gardevoir ex archetype — Riley McKay **won the 2025 World
Championship** with a Gardevoir ex deck, beating Dragapult/Dusknoir in the final._

> **New to this deck? Read this first.** Gardevoir ex is a *damage-counter engine*, not a
> straightforward beatdown. Its Ability (Psychic Embrace) accelerates Energy but **hurts
> your own Pokémon on purpose** — and the whole deck is built to turn that self-damage into
> a weapon. You'll be doing three things every turn: (1) attaching Energy from your discard
> with Psychic Embrace, (2) moving the resulting damage onto the opponent with Munkidori,
> and (3) attacking with single-prize Pokémon (Scream Tail/Drifloon) whose damage *scales
> with damage counters*. You trade your 1-prize attackers for their 2-prize ex Pokémon — a
> prize race that's very hard to lose once you understand the loop. Take it slow; this guide
> spells out every interaction.

## One-line identity
A single-prize damage-counter deck: **Gardevoir ex's Psychic Embrace** attaches **any number
of basic Psychic Energy from your discard** to your Psychic Pokémon (placing **2 damage
counters** on each Pokémon you fuel), **Munkidori's Adrena-Brain** moves that self-damage
onto the opponent, and **Scream Tail / Drifloon** hit for more the more damage counters are
in play. You out-trade the format's 2-prize ex decks with cheap single-prize attackers.

## The meta it lived in
2025 Standard was a Stage-2 ex format — Gardevoir ex, Dragapult ex/Dusknoir, Gholdengo ex,
Raging Bolt ex. Gardevoir ex was **the deck to beat all season** and cemented it by winning
Worlds 2025. Its plan (trade single-prize attackers for 2-prize ex Pokémon, healing your own
board with Munkidori) is almost impossible to out-race once set up. Its hardest matchups are
**Darkness attackers** (N's Zoroark ex) that hit Gardevoir for Weakness, and fast decks that
KO you before the engine comes online.

## The engine — exactly how the loop works (read carefully)
1. **Gardevoir ex — Psychic Embrace (Ability):** *"As often as you like during your turn, you
   may attach a basic Psychic Energy from your discard pile to 1 of your Psychic Pokémon. If
   you do, put 2 damage counters on that Pokémon."* So you can pour many Energy out of the
   discard in one turn — but every Energy you attach this way puts **2 damage counters (20
   HP) on the Pokémon you attached it to.** That self-damage is the "cost."
2. **Munkidori — Adrena-Brain (Ability):** *"Once during your turn, if this Munkidori has any
   Darkness Energy attached, you may move up to 3 damage counters from 1 of your Pokémon to 1
   of your opponent's Pokémon."* This is how you **turn the self-damage into offense** — move
   the counters Psychic Embrace put on your board onto *their* Pokémon. Each Munkidori with a
   Darkness Energy can do this **once per turn**, so 2–3 Munkidori = 6–9 counters moved per
   turn. **Munkidori needs a Darkness Energy attached to work** — that's why the deck runs
   Darkness Energy alongside Psychic.
3. **Scream Tail / attackers:** these deal more damage the more damage counters exist (see
   below), so the counters you generate feed your attacks *and* your movement.

The beautiful part: Psychic Embrace's "downside" (self-damage) is the deck's *fuel*. Munkidori
moves it onto the opponent, and if a counter would be wasted, Scream Tail's damage scales off
it anyway.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Ralts (84/198) ×3 → Kirlia (85/198) ×2 → Gardevoir ex (86/198) ×2** — the core.
  **Psychic Embrace** (above) is the Energy engine; **Miracle Force** (its attack) does solid
  damage and can be boosted by Munkidori counters. You run **Rare Candy** to jump Ralts →
  Gardevoir ex, skipping Kirlia, for a turn-2 setup. 190 HP.
- **Munkidori (95/167) ×4** — **Adrena-Brain** (above). Four copies because moving damage is
  the whole plan — you want 2+ online with Darkness attached. Also **heals your own board**
  (moving counters *off* your Pokémon keeps them alive — this is how you "undo" a Phantom
  Dive). Its **Mind Bend** attack can confuse, too.
- **Scream Tail (86/182) ×1** — the main attacker. **Slap Whip**: does more damage per damage
  counter on the opponent's Pokémon (with Bravery Charm + Munkidori setup it can exceed 330 —
  enough to KO *anything*). A **single-prize** attacker: they take only 1 prize for KO'ing it,
  but it hits like a 2-prize threat.
- **Psychic Energy ×7 + Darkness Energy ×4** — Psychic fuels Psychic Embrace/attacks; the 4
  Darkness are **exclusively to turn on Munkidori's Adrena-Brain** (Munkidori needs Darkness
  attached). Rule to remember: **you can't Psychic Embrace onto Munkidori** (it's a Darkness
  Pokémon, not Psychic) — attach its Darkness manually or via other means.

## The tech attackers (why the 1-ofs)
- **Flutter Mane (78/162) ×1** — a fast Basic that can attack turn 1 and **slow the opponent
  down** early (buys time to set up your Stage-2 line). Also a cheap Psychic attacker you *can*
  Psychic Embrace onto.
- **Lillie's Clefairy ex (56/159) ×1** — **Fairy Zone** (Ability): your Psychic Pokémon's
  attacks do **double damage to Dragon Pokémon**. This is the **Dragapult answer** — it lets
  Scream Tail/Miracle Force/Mew-copied attacks 1HKO the opposing Dragapult ex (Dragons usually
  have no Weakness, so this is huge).
- **Mew ex (151/165) ×1** — **Restart** (Ability): draw until you have 3 cards — your draw
  refuel now that Kirlia's draw Ability is gone. **Genome Hacking** (attack): copy an attack of
  the opponent's Active — most importantly **copy Phantom Dive** and, with **Lillie's Clefairy
  in play**, 1HKO an opposing Dragapult ex *and* spread counters. (Note: you can't Psychic
  Embrace onto Mew ex either — it's Psychic, so actually you *can*. Mew ex is Psychic-type.)
- **Fezandipiti ex (38/64) ×1** — **Flip the Script** (Ability): once, after a Pokémon was KO'd
  last turn, draw 3 — comeback draw. A single-prize-focused deck triggers this often.

## Support / tech (why the counts)
- **Iono ×4 / Professor's Research ×4 / Arven ×2** — draw + disruption + search. **Iono**
  (shuffle both hands, draw to prize count) disrupts *and* refuels — great when you're ahead
  (they draw fewer). **Arven** searches a Tool (Bravery Charm) + an Item.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / Nest Ball ×2 / Earthen Vessel ×3 / Rare Candy ×2** — search + Energy
  discard. **Earthen Vessel** (discard 1 → 2 Basic Energy) both finds Energy *and* seeds the
  discard so Psychic Embrace has fuel; **Rare Candy** jumps to Gardevoir ex.
- **Counter Catcher ×2 / Secret Box ×1 (ACE SPEC)** — **Counter Catcher** (free gust when
  behind on prizes) drags up a support Pokémon to KO; **Secret Box** searches 4 cards (Pokémon
  + Item + Tool + Supporter) for an explosive turn.
- **Night Stretcher ×2 / Super Rod ×1** — recover Pokémon/Energy (recur attackers + refill
  Psychic for Embrace).
- **Technical Machine: Evolution ×2** — a Tool-TM that lets a Basic evolve the turn it's played
  / an extra evolution — speeds your Stage-2 setup (helps get Gardevoir/attackers online).
- **Technical Machine: Devolution ×1** — a TM attack that **devolves** an opponent's Pokémon —
  combos with your spread damage to KO an evolved Pokémon (devolve a damaged Stage 2 → the
  Basic can't survive), and disrupts Rare-Candy decks.
- **Bravery Charm ×1** — Tool: +50 HP. On **Scream Tail/Drifloon** it lets them survive a hit
  and hold more Energy/counters (bigger attacks); also protects Fezandipiti/Munkidori from
  snipe.
- **Artazon ×2** — Stadium: search a non-Rule-Box Basic (more Ralts/Munkidori/Scream Tail) —
  consistency, and a counter to opposing Stadiums.

## Ideal early game (turn by turn)
- **Turn 1:** bench **Ralts** (+ Munkidori, Flutter Mane). Play **Artazon** to grab more
  Basics. Use **Earthen Vessel/Ultra Ball** to get **Psychic Energy into the discard** (Psychic
  Embrace pulls from there). If you have Flutter Mane charged, attack to slow them.
- **Turn 2:** **Rare Candy → Gardevoir ex** (skip Kirlia). Attach a **Darkness Energy to a
  Munkidori** (turns on Adrena-Brain). **Psychic Embrace** several Psychic onto an attacker
  (Scream Tail), accepting the 2-counters-each self-damage. **Move that damage onto their board
  with Munkidori.** Attack.

## The core loop (every turn once set up)
1. **Psychic Embrace** to load your attacker with Energy from the discard (each attach = 2
   counters on that Pokémon).
2. **Adrena-Brain** with each Darkness-carrying Munkidori: move up to 3 counters per Munkidori
   *off your board and onto theirs* — this both **heals your attacker** and **pre-damages their
   Pokémon**.
3. **Attack with Scream Tail** (scales with the counters you just placed) — often KO'ing a
   2-prize ex while giving up only 1 prize back.
4. Use **Lillie's Clefairy + Mew ex's Genome Hacking (copy Phantom Dive)** to answer Dragapult;
   **Counter Catcher/Boss** to pick the target; **Iono** to disrupt when ahead.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Turn-2 Gardevoir ex** (via Rare Candy) is the goal — Psychic Embrace needs to start ASAP,
  and you need Psychic in the discard to fuel it (seed it with Earthen Vessel/Ultra Ball).
- **Always have a Darkness Energy on a Munkidori** — Adrena-Brain is dead without it; this is
  the #1 beginner mistake. Match your Darkness count to your Munkidori.
- **Use Adrena-Brain to *heal* as much as to damage** — moving counters off your attacker keeps
  it alive through their attack; you can undo an entire Phantom Dive across a few Munkidori.
- **Stay single-prize** — the deck wins the prize trade by giving up 1 for their 2. Don't over-
  expose Gardevoir ex/Mew ex/Fezandipiti (2-prize liabilities) unless necessary.
- **Lillie's Clefairy before you fight Dragapult** — its Fairy Zone (double damage to Dragons)
  is how you 1HKO their Dragapult ex; get it down early in that matchup.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **N's Zoroark ex / Darkness attackers** — Gardevoir ex is **Metal-Weak** and Darkness decks
  (N's Zoroark, N's Reshiram) KO it and don't leave counters for you to exploit — the toughest
  matchup.
- **Fast KO decks before setup** — if they take the first two prizes before Gardevoir ex is
  online, the single-prize trade can fall behind (Flutter Mane/Budew-style early disruption
  helps).
- **Charizard ex** — initiates the prize trade using your Dark Weakness; a careful matchup.
- **Being unable to attach Darkness (Itchy Pollen / Item lock)** — if you can't get Darkness on
  Munkidori, the engine stalls; plan around it.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Psychic Embrace: attach **any number of basic Psychic from discard** to Psychic Pokémon; **2
  damage counters per attach** on that Pokémon.
- Adrena-Brain (Munkidori, needs Darkness attached): move **up to 3 damage counters** from your
  Pokémon to the opponent's, **once per Munkidori per turn**.
- Scream Tail: damage **scales with damage counters** (330+ possible with Bravery Charm +
  Munkidori setup) — single prize.
- Rare Candy: **Ralts → Gardevoir ex**; Lillie's Clefairy: your Psychic attacks do **double to
  Dragons**; Mew ex Genome Hacking: **copy the opponent's attack** (e.g. Phantom Dive).
