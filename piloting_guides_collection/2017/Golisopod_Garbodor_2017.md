# How to Pilot — Golisopod/Garbodor (2017)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard, **late 2017 / Worlds era** (Burning Shadows). "Golisodor"
— this build mirrors Naoto Suzuki's 2017 World Championship **finalist** list._

## One-line identity
An efficient two-pronged deck: **Golisopod-GX** swings for **First Impression (120 for
one Grass)** every time it re-enters the Active spot, while **Garbodor** provides both a
punishing attacker (**Trashalanche** scales with the opponent's Items in the discard) and
a disruptive Ability (**Garbotoxin** shuts off all Abilities). Cheap, consistent, and
hard to play around.

## The meta it lived in
Late-2017 Standard rewarded efficiency and disruption. Golisodor delivered both: First
Impression is the most Energy-efficient big hit in the game, and Garbodor punishes the
Item-reliant decks while Garbotoxin cripples Ability-based ones (Gardevoir's Secret
Spring, Metagross, Zoroark's Trade). It reached the Masters final at Worlds. Its edge
over pure Golisopod was the split Garbodor line — two different Garbodors for two jobs.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Wimpod (16/147) ×4 → Golisopod-GX (17/147) ×3** — the hitter. **First Impression**:
  120 for a single Grass, *only the turn Golisopod becomes Active*. You must move it in
  each turn (Guzma, Acerola, Float Stone) to re-trigger it. **Armor Press** for a smaller,
  defensive hit when you can't reset.
- **Trubbish (56/122) ×4 → Garbodor** — the split line:
  - **Garbodor (57/122, "Trashalanche") ×2** — **Trashalanche**: 20 × the number of Item
    cards in the opponent's discard. In an Item-lighter format it's situational, but
    against Item-heavy decks it 1HKOs.
  - **Garbodor (51/145, "Garbotoxin") ×2** — **Garbotoxin** (with a Tool attached): all
    Abilities off, both players. The disruption that beats Gardevoir/Metagross/Zoroark.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×2** — Wonder Tag for any Supporter (Brigette, Guzma, Acerola).
- **Float Stone ×4** — free retreat: reset Golisopod's First Impression *and* give
  Garbotoxin Garbodor its required Tool + mobility.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Tapu Koko (SM30) ×1** — Flying Flip snipe + a free-retreat promote body so Golisopod
  can be reset each turn.
- **Guzma ×2 / Acerola ×2** — the reset engine: **Guzma** pulls Golisopod Active and gusts
  the target; **Acerola** picks Golisopod up (heal + re-arm First Impression) and denies a
  prize.
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×3 / Brigette ×1 / Teammates ×1 / Hex Maniac ×1** — draw + setup.
  **Teammates** (after a KO) fetches two cards; **Hex Maniac** is a second Ability-lock
  angle when Garbotoxin isn't set.
- **VS Seeker ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Rescue Stretcher ×2 / Heavy Ball ×1 / Field Blower ×1**
  — Supporter recursion, search, recover Golisopod/Garbodor, and remove opposing Tools/
  your own (to toggle Garbotoxin off when *you* need an Ability).
- **Choice Band ×2** — First Impression to 150 (KOs 150-HP GX) / Trashalanche math.
- **4 Rainbow + 3 Grass + 3 DCE** — Rainbow enables Trashalanche's Psychic cost and off-
  types; Grass powers First Impression; DCE flexibility.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** for Wimpod ×2 + Tapu Koko (ideal), or bench Trubbish. Get a
  promotable body so turn 2 you First Impression immediately.
- **Turn 2:** **First Impression for 120** (150 with Choice Band); start shaping whether
  you want Trashalanche or Garbotoxin online based on the matchup.

## The core loop
Move a loaded **Golisopod-GX** Active each turn (Guzma/Acerola/Float Stone) for **First
Impression**, using **Acerola** to heal + re-arm and **Guzma** to pick the KO. Decide per
matchup: set up **Garbotoxin** (vs. Ability decks) or hold **Trashalanche** as a finisher
(vs. Item decks). You take efficient 2HKOs and trade favorably with Acerola resets.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Reset Golisopod every turn** — First Impression is 120 only on entry; never let it sit
  and do 30.
- **Pick your Garbodor** — Garbotoxin vs. Gardevoir/Metagross/Zoroark; Trashalanche vs.
  Item-heavy decks. The split gives you the choice — use the right one.
- **Field Blower toggles your own Garbotoxin** off when *you* need Abilities (rare, but
  matters).
- **Acerola trades up** — heal + re-arm + prize denial is the deck's grind engine.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire (Ho-Oh/Salazzle)** — Golisopod is Grass/Fire-Weak; fast Fire races you.
- **Gardevoir-GX** — can out-scale even through chip; you lean on Garbotoxin + Choice Band
  math and hope to trade with Acerola.
- **Big Basic 1HKO decks** — anything that consistently one-shots Golisopod before you
  reset.
- **Field Blower decks** — opponents removing your Garbotoxin Tool blunts the disruption.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- First Impression: **120 for 1 Grass**, only on entering Active (150 w/ Choice Band).
- Trashalanche: **20 × opponent's Items in the discard**.
- Garbotoxin: **all Abilities off** while Garbodor holds a Tool.
- Reset tools: **Guzma / Acerola / Float Stone**.
