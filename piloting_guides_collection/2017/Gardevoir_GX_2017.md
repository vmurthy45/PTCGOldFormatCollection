# How to Pilot — Gardevoir-GX (2017)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard, **late 2017 / Worlds era** (Burning Shadows). "Infinite
Force" — this build mirrors Diego Cassiraga's 2017 World Championship-**winning** list._

## One-line identity
The premier attrition deck: **Gardevoir-GX's Infinite Force** does 30 × the total Energy
on *both* Active Pokémon, so it scales into a 1HKO of anything, while **Secret Spring**
(Gardevoir-GX's Ability) accelerates Fairy Energy by hand every turn. Pair it with
**Gallade** as a Fighting attacker and **Max Potion** to erase damage, and you out-grind
the entire format.

## The meta it lived in
Gardevoir-GX was the hyped BDIF going into 2017 Worlds and delivered — it won the whole
event. In a Supporter-driven, slower format (VS Seeker still legal, but no item-turbo),
its combination of Energy acceleration, scaling damage, healing, and Fairy typing
(resisting the popular Dark decks) made it the deck to beat. Its main predators were the
things that could 1HKO it or ignore its bulk: Fire (Ho-Oh/Salazzle) and Metal.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Ralts (91/147) ×4 → Kirlia (92/147) ×3 → Gardevoir-GX (93/147) ×3** — the core.
  **Secret Spring** (Ability): attach a Fairy Energy from hand to a Pokémon each turn (on
  top of your normal attachment) — free, stacking acceleration. **Infinite Force**: 30 ×
  all Energy on both Active Pokémon — scales to 1HKO territory. **Twilight-GX** (the GX
  attack) shuffles 10 cards from your discard back into the deck (recur Energy/Pokémon,
  and answer Trashalanche).
- **Gallade (84/162) ×1** (evolves from Kirlia) — a **Fighting** attacker (**Sensitive
  Blade** hits harder as you take damage / **Premonition** to set up); covers matchups
  Gardevoir can't, and hits the Metal/Colorless-weak Pokémon that wall Gardevoir.
- **Rare Candy ×3** — jump Ralts → Gardevoir-GX a turn faster (skip Kirlia); speed is
  everything for the setup.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: any Supporter on demand (Brigette, N, Guzma,
  Acerola). Also a body that benefits from Fairy acceleration.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Remoraid (31/162) ×2 → Octillery (33/162) ×1** — **Abyssal Hand** draw engine, so you
  aren't wholly Supporter-reliant.
- **Diancie (94/147) ×1** — **Princess's Curtain**-style boost: raises your Fairy Pokémon's
  damage (+20 to Fairy attackers), nudging Infinite Force over KO lines.
- **Alolan Vulpix (21/145) ×1** — **Beacon** search / a splashable body.
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×3 / Guzma ×2 / Brigette ×1 / Hex Maniac ×1 / Acerola ×1** — the
  Supporter suite. **Guzma** gusts + switches; **Hex Maniac** shuts off the opponent's
  Abilities for a turn (huge vs. Golisopod's setup, Metagross, etc.); **Acerola** picks up
  a damaged Gardevoir to reset it (heal + replay).
- **VS Seeker ×4 / Ultra Ball ×4 / Field Blower ×2 / Rescue Stretcher ×1 / Super Rod ×1** —
  Supporter recursion, search, Tool/Stadium removal, recovery.
- **Choice Band ×2** — +30 vs. GX/EX to hit exact KO numbers.
- **7 Fairy + 4 DCE + 1 Wonder Energy** — Fairy feeds Secret Spring/Infinite Force; DCE
  bulks the count fast; **Wonder Energy** protects the holder from GX/EX attack effects.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for Ralts ×2–3; drop a Fairy. Bench Remoraid.
- **Turn 2:** **Rare Candy → Gardevoir-GX**, use **Secret Spring** + your attachment to
  bank Energy, and start attacking / building toward a big Infinite Force.

## The core loop
Every turn: **Secret Spring** an extra Fairy (stacking Energy on Gardevoir), attack with
**Infinite Force** (which grows as your Energy grows and as *they* attach), and **Max
Potion**-style reset via **Acerola** / heal to undo their progress. Use **Guzma** to pull
up the KO you want; **Twilight-GX** to recycle Energy in the late game so you never run
dry. You win the attrition war — they can't KO you fast enough while your damage only
climbs.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Turn-2 Gardevoir** (Rare Candy) is the goal — Secret Spring needs to start ASAP.
- **Bank Energy across turns** — Infinite Force scales with total Energy; a fully-loaded
  Gardevoir 1HKOs anything. But don't over-load one target into an Espeon-GX Psychic.
- **Acerola to reset** damaged Gardevoirs (heal + re-load) — it's your Max-Potion-style
  attrition.
- **Hex Maniac / Guzma** are your tempo tools — deny their setup or pull the exact KO.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire (Ho-Oh/Salazzle, Turtonator)** — Gardevoir is Metal-Weak *and* Fire hits hard/
  fast; Ho-Oh can 1HKO before you stabilize. A real problem.
- **Espeon-GX (Psychic)** — punishes over-loaded Gardevoirs by KO'ing them off their own
  Energy; spread your attachments.
- **Metal / Cobalion** — Metal attackers exploit the Weakness.
- **Fast 1HKO decks** — anything that can consistently one-shot a Gardevoir before Secret
  Spring snowballs.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Infinite Force: **30 × total Energy on both Active Pokémon**.
- Secret Spring: **+1 Fairy attachment per turn** (stacks with normal attach).
- Twilight-GX: shuffle **10 cards** from discard into the deck (once-per-game GX).
- Setup: **turn-2 Gardevoir** via Rare Candy; Choice Band **+30 vs. GX/EX**.
