# How to Pilot — Decidueye/Golisopod (2017)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard, **late 2017 / Worlds era** (Burning Shadows — note
Guzma/Acerola). A Grass toolbox pairing Decidueye-GX's snipe with Golisopod-GX's
efficient First Impression._

## One-line identity
Two Grass attackers that cover each other: **Decidueye-GX's Feather Arrow** puts free
damage on any Pokémon *every turn* (an Ability, not an attack), softening the board,
while **Golisopod-GX** swings for a huge **First Impression (120 for one Energy)** each
time it comes into the Active spot. Snipe with the owl, hit hard with the bug, and
recycle with Acerola.

## The meta it lived in
Late-2017 Standard was defined by Gardevoir-GX, Golisopod/Garbodor, Zoroark, Metagross,
and Ho-Oh. Decidueye/Golisopod thrived because **Feather Arrow** ignores the format's
high HP — it stacks damage the opponent can't heal off fast enough — and Golisopod's
First Impression is the most Energy-efficient big hit in the game. **Forest of Giant
Plants** made the multi-Stage-2 setup fast enough to compete.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Rowlet (9/149) ×3 → Dartrix (10/149) ×3 → Decidueye-GX (12/149) ×3** — the snipe
  engine. **Feather Arrow** (Ability): once per turn, put 2 damage counters on any
  opposing Pokémon — no Energy, no attack needed. Multiple Decidueye = multiple arrows
  per turn. **Feather Arrow + Guzma/First Impression** sets up KOs.
- **Wimpod (16/147) ×3 → Golisopod-GX (17/147) ×3** — the hitter. **First Impression**:
  120 for a single Grass Energy, *but only the turn Golisopod becomes Active*. So you
  must move it into the Active spot each turn (Guzma, Acerola, Float Stone) to re-trigger
  it.
- **Forest of Giant Plants ×4** — Stadium: evolve the turn a Pokémon is played. Essential
  to get two Stage-2 lines (Decidueye) online quickly.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×2** — Wonder Tag: search any Supporter (Brigette, Guzma,
  Acerola) the turn it's played.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Tapu Koko (SM30) ×2** — **Flying Flip** snipe + a free-retreat body to promote so
  Golisopod can be Guzma'd/Acerola'd back for another First Impression; also softens
  targets alongside Feather Arrow.
- **Espeon-EX (52/122) ×1** — **Miraculous Shine**: devolve the opponent's Pokémon (wreck
  a Metagross/Gardevoir setup) — a powerful disruption tech.
- **Sudowoodo (66/145) ×1** — anti-Stage-2 / bench tech.
- **Guzma ×2 / Acerola ×2** — the two ways to reset First Impression: **Guzma** pulls
  Golisopod Active (and gusts their target), **Acerola** picks Golisopod up to hand
  (healing it *and* re-arming First Impression next drop). Acerola also denies prizes.
- **Sycamore ×3 / N ×3 / Brigette ×1** — draw + turn-1 setup.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×3 / Revitalizer ×2 / Rescue Stretcher (via Revitalizer
  role)** — search, Supporter recursion, and **Revitalizer** to get Grass Stage-1/2s back
  from the discard.
- **Field Blower ×2 / Choice Band ×3 / Float Stone ×2** — remove Garbodor's Tool/Parallel
  City, push First Impression to 150 (KOs 150-HP GX), and free-retreat to reset
  Golisopod.
- **5 Grass + 3 DCE** — Grass powers both attackers; DCE for flexibility.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Brigette** (via Tapu Lele) for Rowlet(s), Wimpod, and a Tapu Koko; drop
  **Forest of Giant Plants**. Ideal: two Wimpod + a promotable body so turn 2 you can
  First Impression immediately.
- **Turn 2:** evolve Decidueye online (Feather Arrow starts), and **First Impression**
  for 120 (150 with Choice Band).

## The core loop
Every turn: use **Feather Arrow** from each Decidueye to place free damage, then move a
loaded **Golisopod-GX** Active (Guzma/Acerola/Float Stone) for a **First Impression**.
Arrows + First Impression combine to KO things a plain 120 couldn't. **Acerola** recycles
a damaged Golisopod (heal + re-arm); **Guzma** picks the target. You grind the board down
from two angles.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Move Golisopod Active every turn** — First Impression only hits 120 the turn it
  arrives; a Golisopod that sat there does 30. Guzma/Acerola/Float Stone are non-
  negotiable enablers.
- **Aim Feather Arrows to set up KOs** — 2 counters/turn/owl adds up; place them where
  First Impression or Tapu Koko finishes the job.
- **Acerola = heal + reload** — its dual purpose (deny a prize, re-arm First Impression)
  is the deck's engine; don't waste it.
- **Field Blower vs. Garbodor/Parallel City** — protect your Abilities and Stadium.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire (Ho-Oh/Salazzle, Volcanion)** — both your attackers are Grass (Fire-Weak); a fast
  Fire deck races you.
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — shuts off **Feather Arrow** (half your damage) and Tapu Lele;
  lean on Field Blower.
- **Gardevoir-GX** — out-heals/out-scales; you need arrows + Choice Band math to keep up.
- **Slow starts** — a multi-Stage-2 deck; a Forest-less or Brigette-whiff open falls
  behind.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Feather Arrow: **2 counters on any Pokémon, per Decidueye, per turn** (Ability).
- First Impression: **120 for 1 Grass**, *only the turn Golisopod becomes Active* (150 w/
  Choice Band).
- Setup: **turn-1 Forest of Giant Plants** + Brigette.
- Reset tools: **Guzma / Acerola / Float Stone** to re-arm First Impression.
