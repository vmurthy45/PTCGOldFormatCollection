# How to Pilot — Alolan Ninetales (2017)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard, **late 2017 / Worlds era** (through Burning Shadows —
note the Guzma). A Water toolbox built on Aqua Patch acceleration and Alolan Ninetales-GX._

## One-line identity
A flexible Water toolbox: **Aqua Patch** pours Water Energy from the discard onto your
bench, letting **Alolan Ninetales-GX** fire **Ice Blade** (a 2-Energy bench snipe) or
its bulky attacks while **Blizzard Edge** and the **Ice Path-GX** attack lock the game
up. **Tapu Lele-GX** finds any Supporter, and a small tech package covers the field.

## The meta it lived in
This is the Burning Shadows-on Standard that led into 2017 Worlds — a slower,
Supporter-driven format (VS Seeker still legal but the item-turbo of prior years gone)
defined by **Gardevoir-GX**, **Golisopod/Garbodor**, **Ho-Oh/Salazzle**, **Metagross-GX**,
and **Zoroark**. Alolan Ninetales-GX was a consistent, evasive Water deck that punished
the field with snipe damage and free-retreat mobility, with **Rough Seas** healing to
grind out the mirror and Fire matchups.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Alolan Vulpix (21/145) ×4 → Alolan Ninetales-GX (22/145) ×3** — the core. **Ice
  Blade** snipes the bench cheaply; **Blizzard Edge** hits hard for a Water + DCE; the
  **Ice Path-GX** attack can shut down damage from your opponent's Pokémon-GX/EX for a
  turn — a game-swinging wall. Alolan Vulpix's **Beacon** (search 2 Pokémon) sets up
  turn 1.
- **Aqua Patch ×4** — the accelerator: attach a basic Water from the discard to a
  benched Water Pokémon. This is what lets Ninetales attack a turn early and stream.
- **Remoraid (31/162) ×2 → Octillery (33/162) ×2** — **Abyssal Hand**: draw until you
  have 5 — the Ability draw engine that keeps the deck flowing without burning
  Supporters.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×2** — **Wonder Tag**: search any Supporter on the turn it's
  played (Brigette turn 1, Guzma for the KO, N to disrupt). The consistency glue of the
  whole format.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Tapu Koko (SM30) ×1** — **Flying Flip** snipes 20 to a benched Pokémon *and* his
  **Electropace-GX**/attack spreads; free retreat pivot that also softens targets for
  Ice Blade.
- **Sudowoodo (66/145) ×1** — **Roadblock**: opponents can't play Stage-2 evolutions
  from hand as easily (a tech vs. Metagross/Decidueye); also a bench-snipe answer.
- **Giratina (XY184) ×1** — its Ability blocks effects of your opponent's Pokémon-GX/EX
  attacks in some lines (anti-lock tech / disruptive body).
- **Sycamore ×4 / N ×3 / Guzma ×2 / Pokémon Center Lady ×1 / Karen ×1 / Brigette ×1 /
  Professor Kukui ×1** — the Supporter suite. **Guzma** drags up a target *and* switches
  (the format's premier gust); **Karen** shuffles an opponent's discarded Pokémon back
  (anti-Vespiquen/Night March); **Pokémon Center Lady** heals 60 + clears status;
  **Brigette** sets up your evolutions turn 1.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×3 / Field Blower ×1** — search, Supporter recursion, Tool/
  Stadium removal (answers Garbodor/Choice Band).
- **Choice Band ×3 / Float Stone ×2 / Rough Seas ×3** — +30 vs. GX/EX to hit KO numbers,
  free retreat, and heal 30 off your Water Pokémon each turn.
- **7 Water + 4 DCE** — Water feeds Aqua Patch/attacks; DCE powers Blizzard Edge and
  off-costs.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** Alolan Vulpix **Beacon** (or Brigette via Tapu Lele) to grab your line,
  bench Remoraid, drop Water so Aqua Patch is live.
- **Turn 2:** evolve to **Alolan Ninetales-GX**, **Aqua Patch** onto the bench, and start
  **Ice Blade** sniping or set up a Blizzard Edge.

## The core loop
Accelerate with Aqua Patch, snipe with **Ice Blade** / hit hard with **Blizzard Edge**,
and use **Guzma** to pull up the Pokémon you want to KO. Drop **Ice Path-GX** at the key
moment to blank a turn of GX/EX damage. **Rough Seas** + free retreat keeps your board
healthy; **Octillery** keeps you drawing; **Choice Band** pushes attacks over GX KO
lines.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Turn-2 Aqua Patch** is the tempo goal — it's what makes Ninetales attack early and
  stream.
- **Save Ice Path-GX** for a turn that blanks a big attacker (Gardevoir, Ho-Oh) — timing
  it well can win the game outright.
- **Guzma is your reach** — the snipe + gust combination sets up KOs the opponent can't
  protect against.
- **Field Blower the Garbodor / Choice Band** when it matters; don't let Garbotoxin sit.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Lightning attackers** — Alolan Ninetales-GX is Lightning-Weak (Tapu Koko-GX/Vikavolt
  punish it).
- **Gardevoir-GX** — out-tanks and out-scales you late (Max Potion + huge Infinite Force);
  a grind you can lose.
- **Garbodor (Garbotoxin)** — shuts off Octillery/Tapu Lele draw; lean on Field Blower and
  Supporters.
- **Decidueye-GX** — Feather Arrow chip + Grass pressure can outpace the snipe game.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Aqua Patch: **basic Water from discard → benched Water Pokémon** (Item, multiple/turn).
- Ice Blade: cheap **bench snipe**; Blizzard Edge: heavy hit for **Water + DCE**.
- Ice Path-GX: **prevents damage from opponent's GX/EX** for a turn (once-per-game GX).
- Choice Band: **+30 vs. GX/EX**; Rough Seas: **heal 30** off Water Pokémon.
