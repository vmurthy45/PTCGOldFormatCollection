# How to Pilot — Ho-Oh Salazzle (2017)

_Format: Sun & Moon-on Standard, **late 2017 / Worlds era** (Burning Shadows). A fast
Fire deck; first played at 2017 Worlds by Takuya Yoneda (started 6-0, finished 9th)._

## One-line identity
Explosive Fire aggression: **Kiawe** attaches **4 Fire Energy** in one Supporter (your
turn ends), instantly powering **Ho-Oh-GX's Phoenix Burn** for 180 — a turn-1/2 haymaker.
Take early prizes with Ho-Oh, then close with **Salazzle-GX's Diabolical Claws**, which
scales with the prizes you've already taken. Simple, fast, and punishing.

## The meta it lived in
Post-rotation Standard slowed down — which made a fast, aggressive Fire deck valuable.
Ho-Oh/Salazzle exploited the metagame's high-HP GX Pokémon (many Fire-Weak) with big,
early Phoenix Burns, and it was especially strong into **Metagross-GX** and **Zoroark**
while beating many rogues. It's meta-dependent (Energy-acceleration decks and Gardevoir
can outpace it), but in the right field it races anything.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Ho-Oh-GX (21/147) ×3** — the main attacker. **Phoenix Burn**: 180 damage (discard all
  Energy on it) — but Kiawe reloads it. **Sacred Fire**: attach Fire from the discard,
  usable to follow up. **Rebirth-GX** heals/recovers. Note: Phoenix Burn *can't* be used
  the turn you play Kiawe on the same Ho-Oh unless set up right — plan attachments.
- **Kiawe ×3** — the engine: attach **4 basic Fire from the deck** to 1 Pokémon, then your
  turn ends. Perfect for Phoenix Burn's cost; ideally played turn 1 (you can't attack
  first turn anyway).
- **Salandit (15/145) ×2 → Salazzle-GX (25/147) ×2** — the closer. **Diabolical Claws**:
  damage scales with the number of prizes you've taken (huge after Ho-Oh takes 3–4). Cheap
  2-Energy cost. **Callous Comet-GX** for a big finish.
- **Volcanion-EX (26/114) ×2 + Volcanion (25/114) ×1** — **Steam Up** (Volcanion-EX
  Ability): discard a Fire to give a Fire attacker +30 this turn (stackable) — pushes Ho-Oh
  to 1HKO numbers. Baby Volcanion (**Power Heater**) accelerates Fire to the bench.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Turtonator-GX (18/145) ×1** — **Nitro Tank-GX**: refuel your attackers from the discard
  mid-game (answers Phoenix Burn's self-discard); a secondary attacker.
- **Tapu Lele-GX (60/145) ×3** — Wonder Tag: grab **Kiawe** turn 1 (the whole plan); also N/
  Guzma.
- **Shaymin-EX (77/108) ×1** — Set Up burst draw to find the Ho-Oh + Kiawe opener.
- **Sudowoodo (66/145) ×1** — tech vs. Mega Rayquaza / bench-snipe.
- **Sycamore ×4 / Guzma ×3 / N ×2** — draw + gust. **Guzma** pulls up a benched target for
  a Phoenix Burn KO (and switches your own to reset). Note the higher Guzma / lower N: this
  is a fast deck that doesn't want to N itself off a lead.
- **Ultra Ball ×4 / VS Seeker ×3 / Switch ×2 / Max Elixir ×2 / Super Rod ×2 / Float Stone
  ×1** — search, recursion, reset attackers, extra Fire acceleration, and recover Energy/
  Pokémon (crucial with all the discarding).
- **Choice Band ×4** — +30 vs. GX/EX; Phoenix Burn 180 + Band = 210 (1HKOs Gardevoir-GX).
- **14 Fire** — the whole deck runs on basic Fire (Kiawe, Sacred Fire, Steam Up, Max
  Elixir all want a lot).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** **Ho-Oh-GX** active, **Wonder Tag (Tapu Lele) → Kiawe** to dump 4 Fire onto
  Ho-Oh (turn ends). Bench a Salandit with an Energy so Salazzle is a threat later.
- **Turn 2:** **Phoenix Burn for 180** (210 with Choice Band / Steam Up) — an early
  2-prize KO.

## The core loop
Kiawe → Phoenix Burn for 180 → next turn, Guzma the target and either **Sacred Fire**-
reload another Ho-Oh or fire the next **Phoenix Burn**; use **Steam Up** to hit exact 1HKO
numbers. Once you're up ~3–4 prizes, swing with **Salazzle-GX's Diabolical Claws** for a
massive, cheap finish. **Turtonator/Super Rod** refuel so the self-discard never stalls
you.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Turn-1 Kiawe is the dream** — map your attachments so Phoenix Burn comes online turn 2;
  you often plan several turns ahead.
- **Don't wait to arm Salandit** — put an Energy on it early so Salazzle is ready to close;
  whiffing the Energy hands the opponent a turn.
- **Steam Up to exact KO numbers** — 180 → 210 lets Ho-Oh 1HKO Gardevoir/big GX.
- **Manage Fire in the discard** — Turtonator/Super Rod recycle; you can run out if
  careless.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Water attackers** — Ho-Oh/Salazzle/Volcanion are Fire (Water-Weak); Alolan Ninetales/
  Water decks punish you.
- **Energy-acceleration / attrition decks (Gardevoir-GX)** — can out-ramp and out-heal your
  burst; a documented rough matchup.
- **Getting N'd off a lead** — as a fast deck, a well-timed N to a small hand with no Kiawe/
  Ho-Oh can strand you.
- **Parallel City / damage reduction** — cuts your Fire damage below KO lines.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Phoenix Burn: **180** (discard all Energy on Ho-Oh) → 210 with Choice Band.
- Kiawe: attach **4 basic Fire from the deck** to 1 Pokémon; your turn ends.
- Diabolical Claws (Salazzle-GX): scales with **prizes you've taken** (2 Fire cost).
- Steam Up: **+30** to a Fire attacker per use (discard a Fire), stackable.
