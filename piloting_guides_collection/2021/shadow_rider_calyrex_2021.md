# How to Pilot — Shadow Rider Calyrex (2021)

_Format: Standard 2021 (TEU–CRE, the Players Cup IV card pool). This build mirrors **Dan
Hugar's 9th-place** Players Cup IV list._

## One-line identity
A VMAX that **accelerates its own Energy and draws cards for doing it**. **Underworld Door**
attaches a Psychic Energy from hand to a Benched Psychic Pokémon and **draws 2** — once per
copy, every turn — and **Max Geist** does **10 + 30 for every Psychic Energy attached to all of
your Pokémon**. The Energy you're accelerating *is* your damage, so the deck snowballs: more
Energy on board means more damage and more cards.

## The meta it lived in
Shadow Rider was the defining deck of the Players Cup IV era — the fastest, highest-ceiling
VMAX in a format full of them, and the reason **Path to the Peak** was everywhere. It preys on
Rapid Strike Urshifu (Psychic Weakness ×2) and out-scales most fair decks by turn three. Its
own Weakness to Darkness is what kept **Eternatus VMAX** honest at the same events.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Shadow Rider Calyrex V (74/198) ×4 → Shadow Rider Calyrex VMAX (75/198) ×4** — the deck,
  four-of on both halves because you want a VMAX down on turn two and *more than one* in play
  after that. **Underworld Door** (Ability): *once during your turn, attach a Psychic Energy
  from your hand to one of your **Benched** Psychic Pokémon; if you do, **draw 2 cards***. Each
  copy in play is a separate use — **two VMAX on board is two attachments and four cards a
  turn**. **Max Geist** [C][C][C]: **10 + 30 for each Psychic Energy attached to all of your
  Pokémon** (not just the attacker) — so Energy sitting on the Bench still counts. 320 HP.
- **14 Psychic Energy** — an unusually high count, and correct. Underworld Door needs Energy
  **in hand** every turn to trigger, and Max Geist scales off Energy **on board**. Energy is
  simultaneously this deck's fuel, its draw engine, and its damage formula; you can't run a
  normal 10–11.
- **Fog Crystal (140/198) ×4** — searches **either a Psychic Energy or a Basic Psychic
  Pokémon**. Perfectly targeted: it finds Shadow Rider Calyrex V early and Energy later, so it's
  never a dead card.
- **Shadow Rider Calyrex V's Astral Barrage** [C][C][C] — *put 5 damage counters on each of 2 of
  your opponent's Pokémon.* Free-target spread that ignores where things sit. It sets up KOs a
  turn early and softens a Bench you'll gust later.
- **Gengar & Mimikyu-GX (53/181) ×2** — the closer. **Horror House-GX** [P]: *your opponent
  can't play **any** cards from their hand during their next turn*, and with **one extra Psychic
  Energy attached, both players draw until they have 7 cards**. A one-Energy full lock for a
  turn that also refills your hand — devastating on turn one or two, and a legitimate game-ender
  when they're setting up a comeback. **Poltergeist** [P][P] does **50 per Trainer in their
  hand**, which lands hard on exactly the turn *after* Horror House left their hand intact.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Path to the Peak (148/198) ×2 + Marshadow (81/214) ×2** — treat these as one package.
  Path shuts off Abilities for Rule Box Pokémon, which **includes your own Underworld Door**.
  The line is: set your Energy up first, then drop Path to strangle *their* engine while
  **Max Geist needs no Ability to keep attacking**. **Marshadow's Resetting Hole** discards any
  Stadium in play — including your own — so you can switch Underworld Door back on the moment
  you need it. Marshadow is a plain Basic with no Rule Box, so it still works *under* Path.
- **Marshadow (103/236) ×1** — a different Marshadow entirely. **Shadow Imitation**: copy one
  of your opponent's Active Pokémon's non-GX attacks and use it. A 1-of surprise, and an out
  when a matchup wants a specific effect you don't otherwise have.
- **Phoebe (130/163) ×1** — *this turn, damage from your Pokémon VMAX's attacks isn't affected
  by any effects on your opponent's Active Pokémon.* The answer to damage reduction and
  prevention effects; one copy because it's a specific out, not a maindeck card.
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×2** — **Dedechange**: discard your hand, draw 6. Two copies, since
  Underworld Door wants a full hand of Energy and Dedenne refills it.
- **Boss's Orders ×3** — with Astral Barrage spreading damage around, three gusts is how you
  cash it in.
- **Marnie ×3 / Professor's Research ×4 / Pal Pad ×1** — Pal Pad shuffles back 2 Supporters,
  which in practice means getting **Boss's Orders** back for the last Prize.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Evolution Incense ×3 / Ordinary Rod ×1** — Ordinary Rod recovers Pokémon
  *and* basic Energy, which matters more here than usual given how much Energy you commit.
- **Air Balloon ×3 / Switch ×2** — a 320 HP VMAX with a real Retreat Cost needs help rotating;
  three Balloons is a high count for a reason.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** Shadow Rider Calyrex V Active (Fog Crystal to find it), bench a second V, attach a
  Psychic. If you have the option, **Horror House-GX** off a Gengar & Mimikyu-GX is one of the
  strongest turn-one plays in the format.
- **Turn 2:** evolve to **VMAX**, use **Underworld Door** to attach and draw 2, attach for turn
  as well, and start attacking — Max Geist with 4 Energy on board is already **130**.

## The core loop
Attach with **Underworld Door** (drawing 2 each time), attach manually for turn, and let
**Max Geist** grow. Every Energy you put on the Bench raises your damage *and* your card
advantage. Spread with **Astral Barrage** when you can't one-shot, **Boss's Orders** to convert,
**Horror House-GX** to steal a turn outright, and drop **Path to the Peak** once your own board
is built and theirs still needs Abilities.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Energy on the Bench counts.** Max Geist reads *all of your Pokémon* — don't hold Energy back
  thinking it's wasted on a benched Calyrex; that's exactly where Underworld Door wants it.
- **Sequence Path to the Peak *after* your setup.** It's a lock you're also standing inside.
  Get your attachments in, then play it — and keep a Marshadow ready to blow it up when you
  need Underworld Door back.
- **Multiple VMAX in play = multiple attachments.** Getting a second Shadow Rider VMAX down is
  usually better than a bigger attack this turn.
- **Horror House-GX is often a turn-1 play, not a finisher.** Denying their setup turn is worth
  more than saving the GX attack for later.
- **Count Energy before you attack**, not after — the difference between 220 and 250 decides
  whether a VMAX lives.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Darkness ×2 Weakness** — Eternatus VMAX one-shots you back; this is the matchup that keeps
  the deck honest.
- **Opposing Path to the Peak** — your Ability *is* your engine, and Ice Rider Calyrex plays
  three copies while losing nothing to it. That matchup is largely a Stadium war, which is what
  your two Marshadow are for.
- **Reset Stamp / Marnie into an empty board** — Underworld Door needs Energy in hand; strip the
  hand and the engine stalls.
- **Prize maths** — every Calyrex VMAX you lose is **3 Prizes**. Against single-prize decks like
  Spiritomb you can be winning on damage and losing on Prizes.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Max Geist: **10 + 30 × Psychic Energy attached to *all* your Pokémon** ([C][C][C]).
  4 Energy → **130** · 5 → **160** · 6 → **190** · 7 → **220** · 8 → **250**.
- Underworld Door: attach 1 Psychic from hand to a **Benched** Psychic Pokémon → **draw 2**,
  once per copy per turn.
- Astral Barrage: **50 damage to each of 2 of your opponent's Pokémon** ([C][C][C]).
- Horror House-GX: opponent **can't play any cards from hand** next turn ([P]); with 1 extra
  Psychic attached, **both players draw to 7**.
- Shadow Rider Calyrex VMAX: **320 HP**, **Darkness Weakness ×2**.
