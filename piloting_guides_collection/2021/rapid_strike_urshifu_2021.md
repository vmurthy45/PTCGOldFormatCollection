# How to Pilot — Rapid Strike Urshifu (2021)

_Format: Standard 2021 (TEU–CRE, the Players Cup IV card pool). This build mirrors **Alex
Schemanske's 1st-place** Players Cup IV list._

## One-line identity
The format's premier **bench sniper**: **G-Max Rapid Flow** hits **two** of your opponent's
Pokémon for **120 each**, ignoring Weakness/Resistance on the Bench — and **Passimian's
Throwing Coach** pushes that to **150 against benched Pokémon V and GX**. You don't race the
opponent's attacker; you execute the support Pokémon they need to function, then clean up.

## The meta it lived in
Players Cup IV was defined by the two Calyrex VMAX decks (Shadow Rider and Ice Rider),
Eternatus VMAX, and the Inteleon "Shady Dealings" engine that half the field was built on.
That last point is why Urshifu was so strong: nearly every deck had to bench a **Drizzile**, a
**Crobat V** or a **Dedenne-GX**, and G-Max Rapid Flow kills two of those a turn straight
through the Bench. Schemanske won the event with it.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Rapid Strike Urshifu V (87/163) ×4 → Rapid Strike Urshifu VMAX (88/163) ×3** — the deck.
  **G-Max Rapid Flow** [F][F][C]: *discard all Energy from this Pokémon*, then **120 damage to
  2 of your opponent's Pokémon** (no Weakness/Resistance on the Bench). That discard clause is
  the real cost — every attack leaves you on zero Energy, and the rest of the list exists to
  pay for it. **Gale Thrust** [F]: **30, or 150 if this Pokémon moved from your Bench to the
  Active Spot this turn** — a one-Energy 150 that does *not* discard.
- **Passimian (88/198) ×1** — **Throwing Coach**: your Rapid Strike attacks do **+30 to your
  opponent's benched Pokémon V and Pokémon-GX**. This turns G-Max Rapid Flow from 120 into
  **150 per benched V** — the number that outright one-shots Crobat V and Dedenne-GX. A single
  copy that changes every damage calculation in the deck.
- **Tower of Waters (138/163) ×1** — Rapid Strike Pokémon retreat for **[C][C] less**, which
  makes Urshifu VMAX **free to retreat**. This is what powers the Gale Thrust line: promote a
  fresh Urshifu VMAX from the Bench for nothing and immediately swing for **150** on one
  Energy, no discard. It's the cheap attack turn between Rapid Flows.
- **Sobble (41/198) ×4 → Drizzile (56/202) ×4 → Inteleon (43/198) ×3 / Inteleon (58/202) ×1** —
  **Sobble's Keep Calling** searches **3 Basic Rapid Strike Pokémon** onto your Bench (Sobble,
  Urshifu V and Passimian all qualify) for an explosive turn 1. **Drizzile's Shady Dealings**
  fetches 1 Trainer on evolve; the lone **SSH Inteleon** fetches **2**. The three **CRE
  Inteleon** are there for a different Ability — **Quick Shooting**, *once during your turn, 2
  damage counters on any of your opponent's Pokémon*, **per copy**, so a full board adds **60
  damage a turn** for free. That chip is what converts a 150 snipe into a KO.
- **Rapid Strike Energy (140/163) ×4 + 4 Fighting Energy** — Rapid Strike Energy provides **2
  units** in any mix of Water and Fighting, so one of them plus a basic Fighting already turns
  on the 3-cost G-Max Rapid Flow. Eight Energy in a deck that discards its whole attachment
  every attack is only viable because of this.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Jirachi-GX (79/236) ×1** — **Psychic Zone**: *don't apply Psychic Weakness* for either
  player. Urshifu is **Psychic-Weak ×2** and the format's best deck was **Shadow Rider
  Calyrex**, so this one card deletes the Weakness and flips that matchup. Its **Star Search**
  attack also fetches an Energy if you're stranded.
- **Mew (76/214) ×1** — **Bench Barrier**: prevents *all* damage to your Benched Pokémon from
  attacks. The mirror-breaker — it blanks the opposing G-Max Rapid Flow completely and shields
  your Sobble/Drizzile line from every other spread deck.
- **Karate Belt (201/236) ×2** — while **you have more Prizes remaining than your opponent**,
  the holder's attacks cost **[F] less**. You're usually behind on Prizes early (you're
  sniping support, not taking KOs), so it's live exactly when it matters: G-Max Rapid Flow
  drops to two Energy and you rebuild a turn faster after the discard.
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×1** — **Dedechange**: discard your hand, draw 6.
- **Tool Scrapper (168/192) ×1** — removes opposing Tools, and can clear your own Karate Belt
  if the Prize count flips and it's dead weight.
- **Reset Stamp (206/236) ×1** — shuffles their hand away and gives them one card per
  remaining Prize; save it for when they're down to 1–2.
- **Boss's Orders ×2 / Marnie ×3 / Professor's Research ×4** — gust plus draw. Boss's Orders
  finishes something you already softened from the Bench.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Level Ball ×4 / Evolution Incense ×2 / Pokémon Communication ×2** — Level
  Ball grabs anything **90 HP or less**, which covers Sobble *and* Drizzile.
- **Switch ×1 / Escape Rope ×1 / Air Balloon ×1** — mobility on top of Tower of Waters.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** Sobble Active, **Keep Calling** to fetch Urshifu V + Passimian + another Sobble
  onto the Bench. Attach a Rapid Strike Energy.
- **Turn 2:** evolve to Drizzile (**Shady Dealings** → whatever Trainer you're missing), attach
  the second Energy, and take your first **G-Max Rapid Flow**, ideally on two benched V.

## The core loop
Snipe two support Pokémon a turn with **G-Max Rapid Flow at 150** (Passimian down), chip the
survivors with **Quick Shooting**, and spend the rebuild turns attacking with **Gale Thrust for
150** off a free Tower of Waters retreat. Their deck stops functioning before your Prize count
catches up — then **Boss's Orders** drags up whatever's left.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Passimian before you attack.** 120 and 150 are completely different cards; benching it
  first is usually worth a whole turn.
- **Attack the Bench, not the Active.** Killing a Crobat V and a Dedenne-GX beats chipping a
  320 HP VMAX you can't one-shot anyway.
- **Alternate Rapid Flow and Gale Thrust.** Rapid Flow empties your board of Energy; Gale
  Thrust is the free attack that covers the gap while you rebuild.
- **Mew down against anything that spreads** — especially the mirror, where whoever resolves
  Bench Barrier first usually wins.
- **Jirachi-GX down against Psychic** before you commit an Urshifu VMAX to the Active spot.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Psychic ×2 Weakness** — Shadow Rider Calyrex one-shots Urshifu VMAX without Jirachi-GX on
  board. Prizing Jirachi-GX is a genuine way to lose that matchup.
- **Opposing Bench Barrier (Mew)** — your whole plan is Bench damage; against a resolved Mew
  you have to switch to attacking the Active with Gale Thrust and win a fair fight.
- **Path to the Peak** — it doesn't touch Drizzile/Inteleon/Mew (no Rule Boxes), but it *does*
  shut off **Jirachi-GX's Psychic Zone** and **Dedenne-GX**, which is how Psychic decks buy
  their Weakness advantage back.
- **Single-prize decks (Spiritomb)** — sniping support is far weaker when the opponent's
  attacker *is* the whole deck and only gives up one Prize.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- G-Max Rapid Flow: **120 to 2 Pokémon** ([F][F][C], discard all Energy) → **150** vs. benched
  V/GX with Passimian.
- Gale Thrust: **30**, or **150** if it moved from Bench to Active this turn ([F]).
- Quick Shooting: **2 damage counters per CRE Inteleon, once each per turn** (60 with three).
- Karate Belt: attacks cost **[F] less** while you have more Prizes left than your opponent.
- Urshifu VMAX: **330 HP**, **Psychic Weakness ×2**, free retreat under Tower of Waters.
