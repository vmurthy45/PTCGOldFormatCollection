# How to Pilot — Ice Rider Calyrex (2021)

_Format: Standard 2021 (TEU–CRE, the Players Cup IV card pool). This build mirrors **Christian
Hasbani's 9th-place** Players Cup IV list._

## One-line identity
The format's cleanest **250-damage-a-turn** deck. **Max Lance** costs two Water and one-shots
essentially every Pokémon V in the format; **Melony** puts the discarded Energy straight back,
so the attack effectively costs nothing to maintain. On top of that it plays **three Path to
the Peak** — a Stadium that shuts off Abilities for Rule Box Pokémon and that **this deck is
almost completely immune to**.

## The meta it lived in
Players Cup IV ran on Abilities: Shadow Rider Calyrex's **Underworld Door**, Eternatus VMAX's
**Eternal Zone**, Crobat V, Dedenne-GX, and the **Inteleon** engine that most of the field was
built around. Ice Rider is the deck that punishes all of it — it hits the biggest clean number
in the format while turning the opponent's engine off. Where the *other* Calyrex needs its
Ability to function, Ice Rider genuinely does not.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Ice Rider Calyrex V (45/198) ×4 → Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX (46/198) ×3** — the attacker.
  **Max Lance** [W][W]: **10 damage, +120 more for each Energy you discard from this Pokémon,
  up to 2** — so **250** for two Water. That one-shots almost every Pokémon V and most VMAX
  aren't far behind. The second attack, **Ride of the High King** [C][C], does **10 + 30 for
  each of your opponent's Benched Pokémon** — up to **160** against a full Bench, for two
  *any*-type Energy and **no discard**. Against Inteleon decks that flood the board, that's
  often the better attack. 320 HP on the VMAX.
- **Melony (146/198) ×4** — the engine. *Attach a Water Energy from your **discard pile** to
  one of your Pokémon V, then draw 3.* Max Lance discards two Water; Melony puts one straight
  back **and** draws you three cards. Four copies because this is simultaneously your Energy
  acceleration and a third of your draw. It's why the deck can attack for 250 every single turn
  off only eight Energy.
- **Sobble (41/198) ×4 → Drizzile (56/202) ×4 → Inteleon (58/202) ×2** — the **Shady Dealings**
  engine, used here purely for consistency: Drizzile fetches **1 Trainer** when it evolves,
  Inteleon fetches **2**. Note this build runs only the SSH Inteleon — there's no Quick Shooting
  package, because the deck doesn't need chip damage when it one-shots things outright. Every
  Trainer in the list is effectively searchable.
- **Path to the Peak (148/198) ×3** — *Pokémon with a Rule Box have no Abilities.* Read your own
  board: **Ice Rider Calyrex V and VMAX have no Abilities to lose**, and **Sobble, Drizzile and
  Inteleon have no Rule Box**, so Shady Dealings keeps working. It costs you literally nothing
  and it turns off Underworld Door, Eternal Zone, Dedenne-GX and Crobat V. This is a one-sided
  lock and it's the reason to pick this deck.
- **Capacious Bucket (156/192) ×3 + 8 Water Energy** — Bucket fetches **2 Water Energy** to
  hand. Eight basic Energy is a low count for a deck attacking every turn, and Melony + Bucket
  are what make it enough.

## Support / tech (why the odd counts)
- **Boss's Orders (154/192) ×3** — three copies, deliberately. When you one-shot anything for
  250, the limiting factor stops being damage and becomes *reach*: dragging up the support
  Pokémon or the wounded attacker is how you convert damage into Prizes.
- **Marnie ×3 / Professor's Research ×2 / Bird Keeper ×1** — the draw suite is deliberately
  light on Research because **Melony draws 3** most turns. **Marnie** is favoured for the hand
  disruption; **Bird Keeper** switches your Active *and* draws 3, which matters on a bulky VMAX.
- **Reset Stamp (206/236) ×2** — shuffle their hand back and give them one card per remaining
  Prize. Backbreaking on the turn you take your fourth or fifth Prize, and a genuine comeback
  button when you're behind.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Evolution Incense ×4 / Level Ball ×3 / Pokémon Communication ×1** — Level
  Ball covers Sobble **and** Drizzile (both 90 HP or less); Evolution Incense gets the Inteleon
  line and Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX.
- **Air Balloon (156/202) ×2** — Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX is slow to retreat; Air Balloon is how
  you rotate a damaged one out without spending your Supporter for the turn.

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** Sobble or Ice Rider Calyrex V Active, bench a second Ice Rider V and a Sobble.
  Attach Water.
- **Turn 2:** evolve into **Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX**, evolve a **Drizzile** (Shady Dealings →
  grab Melony, Boss's Orders, or Path to the Peak), attach a second Water, and swing for **250**.
  From here Melony alone sustains the attack indefinitely.

## The core loop
**Max Lance for 250, Melony the Energy back, draw 3, repeat.** Use **Shady Dealings** to fetch
whichever piece the turn needs, drop **Path to the Peak** as soon as their engine matters more
than nothing on your side, and use **Boss's Orders** to pick the target that actually wins the
Prize race. Swap to **Ride of the High King** when their Bench is wide and you'd rather not
discard Energy.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Path to the Peak is nearly free for you — play it early and often.** Three copies means you
  win the Stadium war; against Shadow Rider it can be the whole game.
- **Melony is the deck.** Prizing multiple copies is your worst start; use Shady Dealings to dig
  for one before you dig for anything else.
- **Pick the attack.** Max Lance for the clean 250 KO; **Ride of the High King** when they have
  4–5 Bench Pokémon and you want to keep your Energy attached.
- **Don't over-attach.** Max Lance discards two; a third Water on the same Calyrex is usually
  better spent on the backup attacker via Melony.
- **Save Reset Stamp for their low Prize count** — it scales with how few Prizes they have left.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Metal ×2 Weakness** — Zacian V and other Metal attackers hit a 320 HP VMAX for double.
- **Your own Path to the Peak, mis-sequenced** — it doesn't hurt your Abilities, but it *does*
  stop a benched Dedenne-GX or Crobat V from ever helping you; this list simply doesn't play
  them, which is the correct resolution.
- **Single-prize attackers (Spiritomb)** — one-shotting a 1-Prize Pokémon for 250 is a poor
  trade when you're giving up three Prizes each time you lose a VMAX.
- **Bench snipe (Rapid Strike Urshifu)** — this build runs no Bench Barrier, so a Sobble or
  Drizzile line can be picked apart before it evolves.
- **Opposing Marnie/Reset Stamp** — you rely on Melony resolving each turn; a hand reset on the
  wrong turn breaks the loop.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Max Lance: **10 + 120 per Energy discarded (max 2)** = **250** for [W][W].
- Ride of the High King: **10 + 30 × opponent's Benched Pokémon** ([C][C], no discard) — **160**
  against a full Bench.
- Melony: attach a Water **from the discard** to a Pokémon V, **draw 3**.
- Ice Rider Calyrex VMAX: **320 HP**, **Metal Weakness ×2**.
- Shady Dealings: Drizzile **1 Trainer**, Inteleon **2 Trainers**, on evolve.
- Path to the Peak: **no Abilities for Rule Box Pokémon** — costs this deck nothing.
