# How to Pilot — Blacephalon (2020)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2020 (UPR–SSH/RCL era). "Baby Blacephalon" / "Fireball
Circus" — the single-prize Fire OHKO deck built on the non-GX Blacephalon._

## One-line identity
A single-prize OHKO machine: **Blacephalon's Fireball Circus** discards Fire Energy from your
hand to deal **50 × the number discarded**, scaling to a one-hit KO on *anything* — while
only giving up **one** prize when it dies. **Welder** reloads Fire (and draws 3), a suite of
Fire-search Items keeps your hand stocked, and you trade Blacephalons for the opponent's
2-and-3-prize Pokémon all game.

## The meta it lived in
2020 Standard was ruled by ADPZ and other multi-prize decks (Zacian, PikaRom) — exactly what
a single-prize OHKO deck preys on. Baby Blacephalon 1HKOs a Zacian V or a Tag Team as easily
as a small Basic, only gives up one prize back, and is fast enough to **take the first two
prizes before ADP or Combo Zacian get rolling** (winning that race is the whole matchup). It's
easy to pick up but hard to master — the skill is resource management.

## The discard (not Lost Zone) distinction
This is the **non-GX Blacephalon (32/214)**: Fireball Circus sends Fire to the **discard
pile**, which is **recoverable** (Fire Crystal, Energy Retrieval). That's the opposite of the
2019 Blacephalon-**GX** whose Mind Blown Lost-Zones Energy forever. Here you *can* recycle, so
the deck is more forgiving — but you still plan your Fire so you can take all six prizes.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Blacephalon (32/214) ×4** — the attacker (non-GX, 120 HP, single prize). **Fireball
  Circus**: for 3 Fire attached, discard any number of Fire from hand → **50 damage each**.
  With Welder + Fire search you routinely discard 4–6 for a 200–300 OHKO. **Blazer** can gamble
  for an early prize/KO.
- **Welder ×4** — attach **2 Fire from hand + draw 3** — accelerates *and* refills your hand
  with the Fire you'll discard for Fireball Circus. The core enabler; you want it most turns.
- **Jirachi (99/181) ×3** — **Stellar Wish** (top-5 → a Trainer): finds Welder / Fiery Flint /
  the piece you need; a great opener. Sleeps active — pivot with Escape Board/Switch.
- **Fiery Flint ×4 / Fire Crystal ×4 / Energy Retrieval ×4** — the fuel engine: **Fiery Flint**
  digs 3 Fire to hand, **Fire Crystal** returns 3 Fire from discard to hand, **Energy
  Retrieval** returns 2 — this is what keeps enough Fire *in hand* to Fireball Circus every
  turn (and recycles what you discarded).

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Blacephalon-GX (52/214) ×1** — a secondary attacker; **Burst-GX** takes a quick prize / can
  close a game, and Mind Blown is a backup big hit.
- **Oricorio-GX (95/236) ×1** — **Oricorio's Ability** draws when a Pokémon is KO'd (extra draw
  that eases the Welder-only Supporter line); a Fire body.
- **Zacian V (138/202) ×1** — **Intrepid Sword** draw/utility (a splash for consistency and a
  non-Fire attacker in a pinch).
- **Cramorant V (155/202) ×1** — **Spit Shot** (Ability/attack) snipes for a KO (pick off a
  Dedenne-GX) — reach a benched target without needing a gust.
- **Mew (76/214) ×1** — **Bench Barrier**: protects your bench from snipe (mirror, Naganadel).
- **Phione (57/236) ×1** — **Bubble Beat** gust/pivot utility (pull up a target).
- **Beast Bringer (164/214) ×1** — when a Blacephalon (Ultra Beast) KOs a Pokémon-GX/V, take an
  **extra Prize** — turns a KO into a 2-prize swing, a big tempo boost vs. multi-prize decks.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Switch ×3 / Escape Board ×2 / Friend Ball ×1 / Pal Pad ×1 / Adventure Bag
  ×1 / Lucky Egg ×1 / Stadium Nav ×1** — search, pivot (sleeping Jirachi / reset), Supporter
  recovery, Tool draw.
- **Ultra Space ×1 / Heat Factory Prism Star ×1** — search an Ultra Beast / Fire acceleration
  Stadium.
- **14 Fire** — the high count that Fireball Circus lives on (you discard a lot; recycling keeps
  it flowing).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1–2:** bench a **Blacephalon**, **Jirachi Stellar Wish** for Welder/Fire, and keep Fire
  flowing. Ideal turn 2: attach Fire + **Welder** (2 more Fire, draw 3) → **Fireball Circus** for
  the KO. Recover discarded Fire with Fire Crystal/Energy Retrieval.
- **Take the first two prizes fast** — vs. ADP/Zacian, winning the early race is the game.

## The core loop
Charge a fresh Blacephalon (Welder + attach) → **Fireball Circus**, discarding enough Fire to
KO the target → let Blacephalon get KO'd (only 1 prize) → promote and charge the next one.
**Fire Crystal / Energy Retrieval** recycle your discarded Fire; **Beast Bringer** snags extra
prizes off GX/V KOs; **Cramorant/Phione** reach benched targets. You trade 1 prize for their 2–3
and win the race.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Fuel management is the skill** — Fireball Circus needs Fire *in hand*; sequence Welder + Fire
  search so you can KO every turn without running dry. Plan the discard before you attack.
- **Charge a new Blacephalon every turn** — it dies after attacking; always have the next one
  ready.
- **Race the multi-prize decks** — take the first KOs; **Beast Bringer** off a GX/V KO is a huge
  tempo swing.
- **Only Fireball as big as the KO needs** — you *can* recycle (unlike the GX version), but don't
  waste Fire; keep enough to reach all six prizes.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Water attackers** — Blacephalon is Water-Weak (a Water deck 1HKOs it).
- **Zacian V/Combo & Lucario-Metal** — if they set up their walls (Metal Goggles) or take the
  first two prizes, the race tightens; you must be fast.
- **Sleep / disruption / Chip-Chip Ice Axe** — anything that stalls your every-turn Fireball or
  scrambles your Fire flow.
- **Tapu Fini tech** — several Zacian/ADP decks run it specifically to shore up the Blacephalon
  matchup.

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Fireball Circus: **50 × Fire discarded from hand** (needs 3 Fire attached) — OHKOs anything.
- Welder: **2 Fire from hand + draw 3**; Fire Crystal: **3 Fire discard → hand**; Energy
  Retrieval: **2 Fire discard → hand**.
- Beast Bringer: **+1 Prize** when an Ultra Beast KOs a GX/V.
- Blacephalon: **120 HP, single prize** — discard (recoverable), *not* Lost Zone.
