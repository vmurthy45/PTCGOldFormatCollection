# How to Pilot — Turbo Zacian (2020)

_Format: Sword & Shield-on Standard 2020 (UPR–SSH/RCL era). "Combo Zacian V" — a
streamlined, ADP-free Zacian deck that steals extra prizes with the Jirachi Prism Star combo._

## One-line identity
Fast, consistent Metal aggro with a prize-stealing trick: **Zacian V's Intrepid Sword** +
**Metal Saucer** power a turn-1/2 **Brave Blade (230)**, while the **Jirachi Prism Star + Mr.
Mime + Oranguru + Scoop Up Net** combo lets you claim **extra Prize cards** almost for free —
so, like ADP, you can plan around winning in ~two attacks. Simpler and more consistent than
ADPZ, with one less hoop (no Altered Creation required).

## The meta it lived in
Turbo/"Combo" Zacian was one of the top decks of 2020 (three copies in a Limitless Top 8),
sitting right alongside ADPZ and Lucario & Melmetal. Its pitch over ADPZ: **straightforwardness
and consistency** — you don't need to land Altered Creation-GX, you just turbo a Zacian and
swing, and the Jirachi Prism combo quietly manufactures the extra prizes ADP would get from
its GX attack. It's slightly favored into ADPZ when they don't get a fast GX-attack start.

## Engine cards (why they're here)
- **Zacian V (138/202) ×4** — the deck. **Intrepid Sword** (Ability): draw 3, attach any Energy
  drawn to Zacian (ends turn) — your **draw engine, acceleration, and consistency all in one**.
  **Brave Blade**: 230 — 1HKOs most non-Tag-Team V/GX (the "can't attack next turn" clause is
  covered by Switch). Four copies because Zacian *is* your draw/acceleration — finding one fast
  is priority #1.
- **Metal Saucer ×4** — attach Metal from **discard** to a benched Metal Pokémon — the "turbo":
  power a Zacian as early as turn 1, or refuel instantly after a KO.
- **Jirachi (99/181) ×3** — **Stellar Wish** (top-5 → a Trainer): consistency and a great
  opener; also the non-Prism Jirachi body for the combo setup turns.
- **The prize-steal combo — Jirachi Prism Star (97/168) ×1 + Mr. Mime (66/181) ×2 + Oranguru
  (114/156) ×2 + Scoop Up Net ×4:**
  - **Oranguru** (Primate Wisdom) puts **Jirachi Prism Star on top of your deck**.
  - **Mr. Mime** (Bench Barrier-*style* — here its **Prize-swap** effect) swaps a Prize card
    with the top card — so you place Jirachi Prism into your Prizes at a known spot; taking that
    Prize later effectively nets an **extra Prize**.
  - **Scoop Up Net** picks up Jirachi Prism / Mr. Mime to **reuse the combo** — in a perfect
    game you loop it up to three times, manufacturing ADP-like extra prizes.

## Support / tech (why the odd 1-ofs)
- **Hoopa (140/236) ×1** — **Scoundrel Guard**: can't be damaged by GX/EX (a wall vs. many
  attackers) and a utility body.
- **Dedenne-GX (57/214) ×1** — **Dedechange** draw; consistency (a 2-prize liability you accept).
- **Tapu Fini (53/236) ×1** — tech vs. **Blacephalon / Ultra Beasts** (its attack is cheap when
  they have a UB in play); shores up the Fire matchup.
- **Mawile-GX (141/236) ×1** — **Captivating Wink**: look at the opponent's hand and **put their
  Basic Pokémon onto their bench** — creates extra KO targets (drag out a Dedenne they were
  hiding) and shuts off "play from hand" Abilities; also a backup attacker.
- **Professor's Research ×4 / Boss's Orders ×3 / Marnie ×2** — draw + gust + disruption.
  **Boss's Orders** pulls up the 2-prize Pokémon you want to KO; **Oranguru** softens Marnie's
  sting on you.
- **Quick Ball ×4 / Switch ×4 / Escape Board ×2 / Acro Bike ×3** — search, pivot (reset Brave
  Blade / sleeping Jirachi), dig.
- **Tool Scrapper ×1 / Great Catcher ×1 / Friend Ball ×1** — strip a Tool (Metal Goggles/Frying
  Pan so Brave Blade KOs), gust a benched GX/EX, search a Pokémon.
- **11 Metal Energy** — fuels Intrepid Sword + Metal Saucer + Brave Blade (enough to do two at
  once).

## Ideal early game
- **Turn 1:** find a **Zacian V** and **Intrepid Sword** (draw 3, attach Metal). With **Metal
  Saucer** you can set up a Brave Blade for turn 2 immediately.
- **Set up the combo** in the background (Oranguru → Jirachi Prism on top → Mr. Mime prize-swap)
  when you have a spare turn, so your prizes come faster later.

## The core loop
Turbo a Zacian (Intrepid Sword + Metal Saucer) → **Brave Blade (230)** for a KO → **Switch** to
a fresh Zacian to reset the drawback → repeat. Alongside, run the **Jirachi Prism + Mr. Mime +
Oranguru + Scoop Up Net** loop to bank extra prizes, and **Boss's Orders** to gust the 2-prize
Pokémon you want. Two efficient KOs + the combo's extra prizes closes games fast.

## Priorities / rules of thumb
- **Find Zacian V first — above even your Supporter** — it's your draw *and* acceleration;
  Intrepid Sword early wins games.
- **Bank the combo prizes** — set up Jirachi Prism into your Prizes when you have tempo; Scoop
  Up Net loops it for more. Track exactly where it is.
- **Reset Brave Blade with Switch** — never skip an attack to the "can't attack next turn"
  clause.
- **Mawile-GX to expose hidden multi-prizers** — force a Dedenne/Crobat onto their bench, then
  Boss + KO.

## Weaknesses / bad matchups
- **Fire (Zacian V is Fire-Weak)** — Blacephalon/Fire toolbox 1HKOs Zacian; Tapu Fini + Metal
  Goggles/Frying-Pan-style tools help, but it's the pressure point.
- **Lucario & Melmetal / heavy Metal walls** — Full Metal Wall-GX + Metal Goggles blunt Brave
  Blade (Tool Scrapper is your out).
- **Mill / control (Cinccino)** — a deck-out deck sidesteps your prize race and can target your
  Switches to strand you; unfavorable.
- **Frail combo pieces** — if Mr. Mime/Oranguru/Jirachi Prism are disrupted or prized, the
  extra-prize plan stalls (you fall back on raw Brave Blade).

## Quick reference — the numbers
- Intrepid Sword: **draw 3 + attach Energy drawn to Zacian** (ends turn).
- Brave Blade: **230** (can't attack next turn — reset with Switch); Metal Saucer: Metal from
  discard → bench.
- Combo: **Oranguru → Jirachi Prism on top → Mr. Mime prize-swap → Scoop Up Net to loop** =
  extra Prizes (up to ~3× a game).
- 11 Metal Energy; win plan ≈ **two attacks + combo prizes**.
